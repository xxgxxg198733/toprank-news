5 Best Budget-Friendly Summer Destinations in Europe for 2026: Under $100 a Day

TR
By TopRank Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

With airfare and accommodation prices soaring in 2026, finding a budget-friendly European summer getaway feels like a challenge. But fear not, savvy travelers. We've scoured the continent to find five destinations where you can enjoy a full day of food, activities, and a decent hotel for under $100 (€90). These picks are based on current prices from June 2026 data.

1. Porto, Portugal

Portugal's second city is a steal. A good hostel dorm starts at $20, and a private Airbnb near the Ribeira district is around $70. A pastel de nata and coffee costs $3, and a glass of Port wine at a riverside bar is $5. Free attractions include walking across the Dom Luís I Bridge, exploring the Livraria Lello bookstore (entry is €10, but well worth it), and hiking in the nearby Douro Valley. Total daily cost: $80-90.

2. Ljubljana, Slovenia

Slovenia's charming capital is often overlooked but offers incredible value. A central hotel room is $60-80. A huge plate of local štruklji (rolled dumplings) is $8, and a local beer is $3. The city is incredibly walkable, with free walking tours, a beautiful castle (entry €10), and Tivoli Park. Day trips to Lake Bled are cheap by bus ($5 round trip). Total daily cost: $70-85.

3. Kraków, Poland

Poland remains one of Europe's cheapest destinations. A double room in the Old Town is $50. A hearty pierogi dinner with a beer is $10. Free activities include wandering the Main Market Square, visiting Wawel Castle's grounds, and touring the Schindler's Factory museum (entry $8). Auschwitz-Birkenau day trip is a must, with free entry and low-cost bus. Total daily cost: $60-80.

4. Budapest, Hungary

Budapest offers thermal baths and ruin bars on a budget. Hostels start at $15, and budget hotels at $50. A bowl of goulash is $6, and a pint of local beer is $2. The Széchenyi Thermal Bath entry is $18, and a Danube River cruise is $12. The city's public transport is excellent and cheap. Total daily cost: $65-85.

5. Valencia, Spain

While Barcelona and Madrid have become expensive, Valencia offers a more affordable alternative. A central apartment is $60-80. A paella lunch by the beach is $10, and a glass of sangria is $4. Free attractions include the City of Arts and Sciences complex, the Turia Park, and the beaches. A day trip to the Albufera Natural Park is $15 for a boat tour. Total daily cost: $80-95.

Remember to book early for summer 2026, as these hidden gems are gaining popularity. Happy travels!
