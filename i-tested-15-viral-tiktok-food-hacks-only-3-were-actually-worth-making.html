I Tested 15 Viral TikTok Food Hacks (Only 3 Were Actually Worth Making)

TR
By Christopher Lee · July 21, 2026 · 4 min read

I'll admit it: I'm addicted to TikTok food hacks. There's something hypnotic about watching someone pour cheese on potatoes and then bake it into a 'cheese potato tornado.' But I've been burned before. The 'pesto eggs' fad of 2024? I tried it, and it was a greasy mess. The 'tiktok ramen' where you add butter and garlic? It was okay, but not life-changing. So when I decided to test 15 of the most viral food hacks of 2026, I went in with low expectations. After a weekend of cooking, eating, and occasionally gagging, I can confidently say: 3 of them are genuinely great. The rest? Avoid at all costs.

Let me walk you through the winners and losers. I'm rating each on taste, effort, and 'would I make this again?'

Winner #1: The 'Crispy Rice' Sheet Pan Dinner

This one blew up in April 2026, and I finally tried it. The idea is simple: spread cooked rice on a sheet pan, top it with veggies and protein, then bake until the rice gets crispy on the bottom. I used leftover jasmine rice, added some broccoli, chicken, and a soy-sesame sauce, then baked at 425°F for 20 minutes. The result? The rice became golden and crunchy on the bottom, like a giant rice cake. The veggies roasted perfectly. The chicken stayed juicy. It was like a deconstructed stir-fry that was somehow more satisfying than the original. I've made it three times since that weekend. It's legit.

Pro tip: use day-old rice for the best crunch, and don't overcrowd the pan. You want the rice to be in a thin layer so it can crisp up. Also, line the pan with parchment paper for easy cleanup. This one's a keeper.

Winner #2: The 'Mug Cake' Upgrade With Frozen Fruit

Mug cakes have been around forever, but the 2026 version adds a twist: you put a frozen berry or a chunk of frozen mango in the middle before microwaving. The idea is that the frozen fruit creates a molten center. I tried it with a basic chocolate mug cake recipe—flour, cocoa powder, sugar, milk, oil—and added three frozen raspberries in the middle. After 60 seconds in the microwave, I flipped it onto a plate. The cake was fluffy, and the center was a warm, gooey, tart surprise. It completely transformed a mediocre mug cake into something I'd actually serve to guests. I've been making these for dessert every night. The frozen fruit trick is genius.

One warning: don't use too much fruit, or it'll make the cake soggy. Three small berries or one large chunk is perfect. Also, let it cool for a minute before eating, because that molten center is lava-hot.

Winner #3: The 'Cold Brew' Overnight Oats

Overnight oats are boring, I know. But this version uses cold brew coffee instead of milk, and it's a game-changer. The recipe is simple: 1/2 cup rolled oats, 1/2 cup cold brew, 1 tablespoon maple syrup, a pinch of salt, and some chia seeds. Mix it, leave it in the fridge overnight. In the morning, top it with Greek yogurt and a drizzle of honey. The coffee flavor is subtle but present, and the oats are creamy without being mushy. It wakes you up and fills you up. I've eaten this every morning for a week. It's become my new breakfast staple.

The key is using real cold brew, not instant coffee. The slow extraction gives a smooth flavor that doesn't taste bitter. I bought a bottle of Stumptown cold brew, but any brand works. Also, use rolled oats, not quick oats—quick oats turn into paste.

The Absolute Worst: The 'Spaghetti Ice Cream' Trend

I have to tell you about this one because it's the most disgusting thing I've ever made. The trend involves taking vanilla ice cream, putting it through a potato ricer to create 'spaghetti' strands, then topping it with strawberry sauce (the 'marinara'). I made it. I ate it. I regret everything. The ice cream melts instantly, leaving a puddle of sugary milk. The 'spaghetti' strands look like worms. The strawberry sauce pools at the bottom, creating a nauseating texture. It's not good. It's not even passable. It's a crime against ice cream. Do not try this.

Other losers included the 'cheese potato tornado' (which was just a greasy mess), the 'pink sauce' (which tasted like ketchup mixed with mayo and food coloring), and the 'sushi bake' (which was basically a casserole that fell apart). I could go on, but I'll spare you.

So here's my advice: skip the TikTok trends and stick with the classics. The crispy rice sheet pan, the mug cake with frozen fruit, and the cold brew overnight oats are the real winners. Everything else is just for views.
