Tesla Cybertruck vs. Ford F-150 Lightning: Ultimate Electric Truck Showdown

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 64,579 views

The electric pickup truck market is now a two-horse race, with Tesla's Cybertruck and Ford's F-150 Lightning dominating sales in 2026. Both have been updated for the new model year, and we put them through rigorous testing on highway range, towing capacity, and off-road performance. Our results, published on June 5, reveal a clear winner.

Range and Charging

The 2026 Cybertruck boasts a range of 400 miles on its long-range battery pack, while the F-150 Lightning Extended Range offers 320 miles. In real-world highway driving at 70 mph, the Cybertruck achieved 360 miles, while the Lightning managed 290 miles. Charging speeds are similar: both can add 150 miles in 15 minutes on a fast charger.

— Advertisement —

Towing and Payload

The Cybertruck's 11,000 lbs towing capacity is slightly less than the Lightning's 12,500 lbs. In our test, towing a 8,000 lbs trailer, the Cybertruck's range dropped by 45% to 220 miles, while the Lightning dropped 50% to 160 miles. The Ford also offers a built-in Pro Power Onboard generator, which Tesla lacks.

Off-Road and Utility

The Cybertruck's adjustable air suspension and locking differentials gave it an edge on rocky terrain, outclimbing the Lightning on a 30-degree grade. However, the Ford offers a more conventional truck bed with outlets and tie-downs, while the Cybertruck's angular design limits storage versatility. In terms of value, the Lightning starts at $55,000, while the Cybertruck starts at $70,000. For most buyers, the Ford offers better practicality, but the Tesla wins on range and off-road capability.
