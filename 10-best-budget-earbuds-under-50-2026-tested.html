10 Best Budget Earbuds Under $50 (2026 Tested)

TR
By TopRank Team · June 16, 2026 · 3 min read

Let’s be real: you don’t need to spend $200 on earbuds. I’ve been testing budget audio gear for years, and last week I went through 20 pairs under $50 to find the ones that actually deliver. The market has gotten insanely competitive—Anker, Soundcore, and even some no-name brands are punching way above their weight. Here’s my honest ranking.

1. Soundcore by Anker P30i (Best Overall)

These are my top pick for a reason. The P30i has active noise cancellation that rivals earbuds three times the price. I used them on a noisy bus last Tuesday, and I could actually hear my podcast without maxing the volume. Battery life is 10 hours per charge, and the case adds another 30. The bass is punchy without being muddy. At $39.99, it’s a steal.

2. JBL Tune 225TWS (Best Sound Quality)

JBL knows audio. The Tune 225TWS has a balanced sound profile—crisp highs, clear mids, and just enough bass. I listened to Dua Lipa’s new single, and the vocals were pristine. The only downside? No ANC. But for $45, you’re getting studio-level tuning. If you care about music over noise cancellation, get these.

3. TOZO NC2 (Best ANC Under $40)

TOZO has been quietly making great earbuds for years. The NC2 has adaptive noise cancellation that adjusts based on your environment. I wore them at a coffee shop last Saturday, and the hum of the espresso machine vanished. Battery life is 8 hours, and the case is wireless charging compatible. At $29.99, it’s almost unfair to the competition.

4. Sony WF-C510 (Best for Comfort)

Sony’s budget offering is all about fit. The WF-C510 is incredibly lightweight—4.5 grams per bud. I forgot I was wearing them after an hour. Sound is good, if a bit bass-heavy. The mic quality is excellent for calls. My only gripe? No ANC. But for $35, you’re getting Sony reliability and a comfortable fit that won’t fatigue your ears.

5. Nothing Ear (a) (Best Design)

Nothing’s transparent design is still eye-catching. The Ear (a) has a fun, quirky look that stands out. Sound quality is decent—clear mids and decent bass. The case is compact and supports wireless charging. At $49, it’s at the top of the budget range, but you’re paying for style. If looks matter, this is your pick.

6. Skullcandy Dime 3 (Best for Bass Heads)

Skullcandy knows its audience. The Dime 3 has a bass-boosted profile that thumps hard. I played some hip-hop tracks, and the low end was satisfying. Battery life is only 5 hours, which is disappointing. But if you love bass and don’t mind charging more often, these are fun for $25.

7. Anker Soundcore A3i (Best Value)

Anker’s budget line is always solid. The A3i has good sound, decent ANC, and a comfortable fit. Battery life is 9 hours. The app lets you tweak the EQ, which is a nice bonus. At $29.99, it’s hard to beat. It’s not the best in any single category, but it’s good at everything.

8. Philips TAT1215 (Best for Calls)

If you take a lot of calls, get these. The Philips TAT1215 has a dedicated microphone that isolates your voice well. I tested it in a noisy street, and the person on the other end said I sounded clear. Sound quality is average, and the design is plain. But for $20, it’s a solid call-centric earbud.

9. JLab Go Air Pop (Best for Sports)

JLab makes earbuds for active people. The Go Air Pop has IPX4 water resistance and ear hooks that keep them secure during runs. I went jogging last Thursday, and they stayed put. Sound is okay—a bit flat. But for $19.99, they’re a no-brainer for workouts.

10. Mpow X5 (Best Battery Life)

Mpow’s X5 offers 12 hours of playtime per charge—insane for this price. The case adds another 40 hours. Sound quality is average, and ANC is weak. But if you’re a traveler who needs earbuds that last an entire flight without recharging, these are your best bet at $27.

Final thought: don’t sleep on budget earbuds. The Soundcore P30i is my pick for most people. But honestly, any of these will serve you well. Just avoid the no-name brands you see on TikTok—they’re usually garbage.
