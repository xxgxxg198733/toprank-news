Top 10 Best Places to Visit in Europe This Summer 2026 (Affordable Edition)

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 338,798 views

With summer 2026 in full swing and airfares to Europe down 12% from last year (Skyscanner data), savvy travelers are looking for affordable yet stunning destinations. Lonely Planet and Booking.com have released their joint Best Value Summer 2026 list, highlighting 10 European spots where your dollar goes further. Here’s our curated top 10.

1. Ksamil, Albania

This gem on the Albanian Riviera boasts crystal-clear waters and white-sand beaches rivaling the Greek Islands, but at half the cost. Average hotel price: €45/night. Fresh seafood dinner: €10. The Butrint National Park, a UNESCO site, is a 20-minute drive away.

2. Porto, Portugal

Portugal’s second city offers port wine cellars, a historic Ribeira district, and Douro Valley day trips. Hostels start at €20/night; a three-course meal with wine costs €18. The city’s new metro line connects to the airport directly.

3. Rovinj, Croatia

This Istrian coastal town is often called the ‘Venice of Croatia’ for its pastel-colored buildings and narrow streets. Summer apartment rentals average €60/night. Nearby Lim Bay offers truffle hunting and oysters.

4. Sibiu, Romania

Transylvania’s cultural capital features medieval squares, the Brukenthal Museum, and the Carpathian Mountains. Accommodation from €30/night. Entry to the famous ASTRA Museum of Traditional Folk Civilization is €5.

5. Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina

The iconic Stari Most bridge, Ottoman-era bazaar, and cheap eats — a cevapi lunch costs €3. Hostels from €15/night. The Kravice waterfalls are a 40-minute drive.

6. Gdańsk, Poland

The Baltic port city boasts a UNESCO-listed Old Town, Amber Museum, and golden beaches. Beer is €2, and a hotel room averages €50/night. Day trips to Malbork Castle are a must.

7. Ljubljana, Slovenia

Europe’s green capital offers a pedestrianized center, castle views, and the nearby Lake Bled. Hostels from €25/night; a meal at a local gostilna costs €12. Free walking tours run daily.

8. Valletta, Malta

The fortified capital has baroque architecture, the Grand Harbour, and festivals. Apartment rentals from €55/night. Ferry to the Blue Lagoon is €15 round-trip.

9. Brasov, Romania

At the foot of the Carpathians, this Saxon medieval town is a gateway to Bran Castle (Dracula’s Castle). Hotels from €35/night. Bear watching tours in the forest cost €25.

10. Thessaloniki, Greece

Greece’s second city offers Roman ruins, Byzantine churches, and vibrant nightlife at lower prices than Athens. Rooms from €40/night; a gyros pita costs €3. The Archaeological Museum is a highlight.
