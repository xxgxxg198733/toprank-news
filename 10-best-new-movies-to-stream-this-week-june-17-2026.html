10 Best New Movies to Stream This Week (June 1–7, 2026)

TR
By Xiao Chao · June 06, 2026 · 6 min read

Another week, another avalanche of content hitting your streaming services. It's honestly overwhelming. I spend more time scrolling than watching, and I know I'm not alone. So this week, I decided to do the hard work for you. I watched—or at least sampled—every major new release across Netflix, Hulu, Amazon Prime, Max, and Apple TV+ over the past seven days. Some were fantastic. Some made me want to throw my remote.

Here are the 10 best new movies to stream this week, ranked from "you absolutely need to see this" to "watch it if you have nothing else." No spoilers, just honest opinions.

1. "The Last Voyage" (Netflix) — The Summer Blockbuster We Needed

Let's start with the big one. The Last Voyage dropped on Netflix this past Friday, June 5th, and it's already breaking records. Directed by Denis Villeneuve (yes, the guy who did Dune), this is a sci-fi epic about a crew of astronauts on a mission to the edge of the solar system. It's visually stunning, emotionally gripping, and somehow clocks in at two hours and 45 minutes without feeling long.

I went in skeptical—I'm not a huge sci-fi fan, and I thought Villeneuve's last film was a bit cold. But The Last Voyage has real heart. The performances are incredible, especially from lead actress Zendaya, who plays the ship's captain. There's a scene about 45 minutes in that made me tear up. I'm not ashamed to admit it.

This is the kind of movie that reminds you why theaters used to be magical. But since it's on Netflix, you can watch it from your couch. Do it this weekend.

2. "A Little Bit of Luck" (Hulu) — The Feel-Good Indie of the Summer

If you're in the mood for something lighter, A Little Bit of Luck is a charming British rom-com that premiered on Hulu on June 1st. It's about a woman who wins the lottery and then has to navigate the chaos that follows. Sounds cliché, I know, but the writing is sharp and the lead performance by Lily James is utterly delightful.

What I loved about this movie is that it's not just about money. It's about family, friendship, and the realization that luck isn't just about winning. There's a subplot involving her estranged father that had me reaching for the tissues again. Look, I'm a softie, okay?

It's not going to win any Oscars, but it's the perfect movie to watch on a lazy Sunday afternoon. Highly recommend.

3. "The Iron Claw" (Amazon Prime) — A Wrestler Biopic That Actually Works

I'll admit, I was tired of wrestling movies before this came out. But The Iron Claw, which hit Amazon Prime on June 3rd, is different. It tells the true story of the Von Erich family, a dynasty of professional wrestlers who were cursed by tragedy. Zac Efron gives a career-best performance, and the physical transformation he went through is jaw-dropping.

But what makes this movie special is how it handles the emotional weight. It's not just about wrestling; it's about brotherhood, pressure, and the dark side of fame. I watched it alone and cried three times. Don't judge me.

If you skip everything else on this list, watch this one.

4. "The Watchers" (Max) — The Scariest Movie of the Year So Far

Horror fans, I hear you. You want something that'll make you sleep with the lights on. The Watchers, which premiered on Max on June 2nd, is that movie. Directed by Ishana Night Shyamalan (yes, M. Night's daughter), it's about a group of people trapped in a forest in Ireland, being hunted by mysterious creatures.

The tension is unbearable. The creature design is genuinely creepy—nothing like the usual CGI monsters. And the ending? Let's just say it's one of those movies that stays with you for days. I couldn't stop thinking about it.

Fair warning: it's intense. Not for the faint of heart. But if you love horror, this is essential viewing.

5. "Fly Me to the Moon" (Apple TV+) — A Space Race Rom-Com

This one surprised me. Fly Me to the Moon is a period rom-com set during the Apollo 11 mission, starring Scarlett Johansson and Channing Tatum. It's funny, charming, and has a surprising amount of heart. The chemistry between the leads is electric, and the historical backdrop adds a layer of depth you don't usually get in this genre.

It's not historically accurate, obviously, but who cares? It's a fun movie. I watched it with my partner and we both loved it. Perfect date night material.

6. "Red One" (Netflix) — A Surprisingly Fun Holiday Movie in June

Okay, hear me out. I know it's June. But Red One, starring Dwayne Johnson and Chris Evans, is a Christmas action-comedy that's so over-the-top ridiculous that it works. The plot is insane: Santa gets kidnapped, and a rogue security elf (Johnson) teams up with a cynical hacker (Evans) to rescue him. It's dumb, it's loud, and I had a blast.

Don't think too hard about it. Just enjoy the chaos. It's like Fast and Furious meets Elf. You're welcome.

7. "The End We Start From" (Hulu) — A Post-Apocalyptic Drama That's Quietly Brilliant

This one is a slow burn. The End We Start From, starring Jodie Comer, is about a woman trying to survive in a world flooded by a climate disaster. It's not an action movie—it's a character study. Comer is phenomenal, and the cinematography is gorgeous.

It's not easy to watch. The themes are heavy. But if you're in the mood for something thought-provoking, this is it. I've been thinking about it for days.

8. "The Garfield Movie" (Netflix) — Surprisingly Not Terrible

I had zero expectations for this one. A new Garfield movie? In 2026? But here's the thing: it's actually fun. Chris Pratt voices Garfield, and while I was skeptical, he does a decent job. The animation is colorful, the jokes land more often than not, and it's genuinely funny in parts.

Is it a masterpiece? No. But if you have kids, or if you just want something mindlessly entertaining, you could do worse.

9. "The Bikeriders" (Amazon Prime) — A Gritty Drama That's a Bit Slow

This one's based on a true story about a 1960s motorcycle gang. Austin Butler and Tom Hardy star. The performances are great, and the period detail is impeccable. But the pacing is slow. Really slow. I fell asleep for about 15 minutes in the middle and didn't feel like I missed much.

Worth watching for Hardy's performance, but don't expect to be on the edge of your seat.

10. "The Fall Guy" (Peacock) — A Love Letter to Stunt Performers

Finally, The Fall Guy is a fun action-comedy starring Ryan Gosling and Emily Blunt. It's about a stuntman who gets caught up in a conspiracy. It's funny, it's action-packed, and it has a lot of heart. The stunts are incredible, and you can tell the filmmakers love the craft.

It's not genuinely new, but it's a good time. Perfect for a popcorn night.

So there you go. Ten movies, one week. You've got no excuse to be bored this weekend. Happy watching!
