10 Must-Watch Movies on Netflix Right Now (July 2026)

TR
By Jessica Thompson · July 23, 2026 · 5 min read

Look, I get it. You open Netflix, scroll for twenty minutes, and end up watching The Office for the hundredth time. We've all been there. But July 2026 is actually a killer month for new content, and I've done the hard work of sorting through the noise so you don't have to.

I watched 14 movies over the past week. Fourteen. My couch has a permanent indent. But I found some absolute gems that I'm genuinely excited to talk about. Here's my honest take on the ten best things to stream right now.

1. "Echoes of the Abyss" — The Sci-Fi Movie That Actually Has a Brain

This one snuck up on me. It's a low-budget indie from a first-time director named Maria Kovács, and it's about a deep-sea research team that discovers something they can't explain. But here's the thing—it's not another monster movie. It's about memory, identity, and what happens when technology lets us access other people's experiences. The ending had me staring at my ceiling for an hour.

Critics are calling it "the best sci-fi of the decade so far." I'm not going that far, but it's definitely the most thought-provoking thing I've seen since Arrival. If you like your sci-fi with emotional weight, watch this tonight.

2. "Last Call at the Oasis" — A Documentary That Will Make You Angry

Water scarcity isn't a new topic, but this documentary from the team behind Chasing Ice makes it feel urgent in a way I haven't felt before. They follow communities in California, India, and Jordan dealing with severe droughts. The footage of dry reservoirs and empty wells is haunting. But what got me was the human stories—a farmer in the Central Valley who lost his entire almond crop, a mother in Mumbai whose kids walk three miles for clean water.

Is it a fun watch? No. Is it important? Absolutely. This should be required viewing for anyone who thinks climate change is still a debate.

3. "The Baker's Dozen" — A Feel-Good Comedy That Actually Works

I was skeptical about this one. A comedy about a bakery that accidentally becomes a front for a criminal operation? Sounds like a SNL sketch stretched to two hours. But the cast—led by Awkwafina and John Boyega—is perfect. The jokes land almost every time. It's not trying to be profound. It's just funny, and honestly, that's exactly what I needed.

The scene where they try to bake 500 pies for a mob boss while the health inspector shows up had me laughing so hard I woke up my roommate. Sometimes you just need a movie that makes you happy.

4. "Shadow Protocol" — The Action Movie That Doesn't Let Up

If you're looking for pure adrenaline, this is your pick. It's basically John Wick meets Mission: Impossible, with an ex-spy (played by Dev Patel, who is now officially an action star) trying to stop a cyberattack. The fight choreography is incredible—especially a seven-minute continuous shot through a Bangkok night market.

Plot-wise, it's not winning any awards. But who cares when the action is this good? I watched it twice already.

5. "Letters from Provence" — The Romance You Didn't Know You Needed

I'm not usually a romance guy, but this one got me. It's about two strangers who find each other's letters in a used bookstore and start writing to each other. Yeah, it sounds like a Hallmark movie. But the writing is sharp, the leads have actual chemistry (Lily James and Oscar Isaac), and the Provence setting is gorgeous. It's warm without being syrupy. Perfect for a lazy Sunday.

6. "The Algorithm" — A Horror Movie About Social Media

This is the scariest movie I've seen in years. Not because of jump scares (though there are a few), but because it feels so real. A woman starts getting eerily accurate recommendations from her streaming service, and slowly realizes something is watching her through her devices. It's basically Black Mirror stretched to feature length, but done right. I unplugged my smart speaker after watching this.

7. "Sonic Boom: The Untold Story" — Music Documentary That Rocks

This covers the rise and fall of Napster and the file-sharing revolution. Interviews with Shawn Fanning, Lars Ulrich, and a bunch of artists who lost everything. It's surprisingly balanced—it doesn't just demonize piracy, but also shows how the music industry's greed created the problem. I learned a ton, and the soundtrack is killer.

8. "The Last Dodo" — Animated Movie That Makes You Cry

From the studio that did The Iron Giant comes this beautiful film about a dodo bird who's the last of his species. It's not preachy about extinction—it's a story about friendship and loneliness. The animation is stunning, especially the scenes where the dodo dreams about flying. I cried. Don't judge me.

9. "Cobra" — The Weird One You'll Either Love or Hate

This is a surrealist comedy from the director of The Lobster. It's about a man who wakes up one day and discovers he's turning into a snake. It's bizarre, uncomfortable, and sometimes hilarious. Not for everyone, but if you like weird movies, this is a gem.

10. "Reclamation" — The Drama That Should Win All the Awards

A Native American woman returns to her reservation to fight a pipeline company threatening her community. The performances are incredible—especially newcomer Tala Redfeather, who gives the best debut I've seen since Lupita Nyong'o. It's heartbreaking and infuriating and hopeful all at once. See it.

So there you have it. Ten movies that I can actually recommend without hesitation. Skip the algorithm and just pick one from this list. You'll thank me later.
