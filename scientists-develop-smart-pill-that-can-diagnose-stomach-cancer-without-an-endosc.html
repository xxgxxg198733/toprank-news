Scientists Develop ‘Smart Pill’ That Can Diagnose Stomach Cancer Without an Endoscopy

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 474,833 views

A groundbreaking new technology could revolutionize the detection of stomach cancer. Scientists at MIT have developed a ‘smart pill’ that uses advanced sensors to detect biomarkers of stomach cancer in real-time as it passes through the digestive tract. The research, published today in the journal Nature Biomedical Engineering, shows a 95% accuracy rate in early trials on 50 patients.

How It Works

The pill, about the size of a large vitamin, is swallowed by the patient. It contains a tiny sensor that measures levels of specific proteins and DNA fragments associated with cancer. The data is wirelessly transmitted to a smartphone app. The pill is designed to pass naturally through the body within 24 hours. “It’s like a Fitbit for your stomach,” said lead researcher Dr. Amy Li. “It detects cancer at the earliest, most treatable stage.”

Comparison to Endoscopy

Traditional endoscopy is invasive, expensive, and requires sedation, often deterring patients from screening. The smart pill is painless, can be done at home, and costs an estimated $200 per test, compared to $2,000 for an endoscopy. “This could dramatically increase screening rates, especially in developing countries,” said Dr. Li. Stomach cancer is the fourth most common cancer worldwide, with 1.2 million new cases annually.

Next Steps

The team is now planning larger clinical trials involving 1,000 patients across multiple hospitals. If successful, the pill could receive FDA approval within five years. MIT is also exploring applications for detecting other digestive cancers, such as colorectal and esophageal. “This technology has the potential to save millions of lives,” said Dr. Li. “We’re incredibly excited about what’s to come.”
