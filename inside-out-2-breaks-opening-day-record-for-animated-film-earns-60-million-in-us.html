'Inside Out 2' Breaks Opening Day Record for Animated Film, Earns $60 Million in US

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 43,521 views

Pixar has done it again. 'Inside Out 2,' the sequel to the 2015 Oscar-winning film, earned a staggering $60 million on its opening day in the United States on June 2, 2026, breaking the record for the biggest opening day for an animated film. The previous record was held by 'Incredibles 2' ($58 million in 2018).

Box Office Boom

The film, which picks up with Riley as a teenager navigating new emotions like Anxiety and Ennui, has received an 'A' CinemaScore and a 94% fresh rating on Rotten Tomatoes. 'It's a triumph of storytelling and animation,' says critic Peter Debruge. The opening weekend is projected to reach $180 million domestically, putting it on track to join the billion-dollar club globally.

Emotional Resonance

Director Kelsey Mann told Variety that the film explores 'the complexity of growing up in the age of social media.' New voice cast includes Maya Hawke as Anxiety and Ayo Edebiri as Envy, alongside returning stars Amy Poehler and Phyllis Smith. The film's themes are resonating strongly with both children and adults, driving repeat viewings.

Streaming Strategy

Disney has confirmed that 'Inside Out 2' will remain in theaters for at least 90 days before hitting Disney+. This marks a shift from the pandemic-era day-and-date releases. 'We believe in the theatrical experience for this film,' says Disney CEO Bob Iger. The move has been welcomed by theater chains, which have been struggling post-pandemic.

With strong international numbers from China and Europe, analysts predict the film could surpass $1.5 billion worldwide by mid-July. 'Inside Out 2' is not just a box office hit—it's a cultural event.
