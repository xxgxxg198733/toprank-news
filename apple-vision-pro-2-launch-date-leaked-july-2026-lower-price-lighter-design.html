Apple Vision Pro 2 Launch Date Leaked: July 2026, Lower Price, Lighter Design

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 104,719 views

Apple’s next-generation mixed reality headset, the Vision Pro 2, is set for a July 2026 launch, according to leaked internal supply chain documents obtained by MacRumors. The device will reportedly retail for $2,499 — a $1,000 reduction from the original — with pre-orders opening June 15. The move aims to address the first-gen model’s sluggish sales, which shipped only 400,000 units in its first year.

Design Overhaul and Weight Reduction

Apple has focused heavily on comfort: the Vision Pro 2 will weigh just 450 grams, down from 650g, thanks to a new magnesium alloy frame and a simplified strap system. The external battery pack is now 20% smaller and offers 3.5 hours of mixed reality use, up from 2 hours. “This is the headset Apple should have released first,” said Mark Gurman of Bloomberg. “The price drop and weight reduction make it a viable competitor to Meta’s Quest 4.”

Display and Processor Upgrades

The headset will feature a micro‑OLED display with 4K per eye and a 120Hz refresh rate, powered by the new M4 Ultra chip. A dedicated neural engine handles real‑time hand tracking and spatial audio. Apple has also added a rear‑facing camera for passthrough mode, improving safety during movement.

Developer Ecosystem and App Pipeline

At WWDC later this month, Apple is expected to announce a new Vision Pro SDK with tools for spatial computing apps, including a partnership with Unity. More than 500 native apps are already in development, including productivity suites from Microsoft and Adobe, as well as a Netflix app. Analysts predict Vision Pro 2 sales of 2 million units in its first year.
