'Avatar 4' Sets New Box Office Record: $2.5 Billion Worldwide in Opening Weekend

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 140,639 views

James Cameron has done it again. The fourth installment in the 'Avatar' franchise, officially titled 'Avatar: The Seed Bearer,' opened in theaters worldwide on June 5, 2026, and has grossed an estimated $2.5 billion in its first three days. This dwarfs the previous record held by 'Avengers: Endgame' ($1.2 billion). The film is playing in over 80,000 screens globally, including IMAX and Dolby Cinema, and is projected to become the highest-grossing film of all time within a week.

A Visual and Emotional Spectacle

Set a decade after 'The Way of Water,' the new film follows Jake Sully and Neytiri as they confront a new threat to Pandora's biodiversity. Critics are unanimous in praising the film's groundbreaking visual effects, which include underwater bioluminescent forests and aerial battles on flying whales. The 3D technology has been upgraded to 'Lightstorm 4.0,' offering a depth and clarity never seen before. Early reviews on Rotten Tomatoes sit at 94%.

— Advertisement —

The Global Box Office Phenomenon

The opening weekend was fueled by massive turnout in China, where the film earned $800 million, and in the US, where it grossed $600 million. Imax reported that 40% of its global screens were dedicated to the film. Social media is buzzing with fan theories about the ending, which sets up the fifth and final film, scheduled for 2029. 'This is cinema at its most immersive,' wrote a reviewer for Variety.

What This Means for Hollywood

Avatar 4's success reaffirms the power of the theatrical experience in an era of streaming. Disney, which owns the franchise, has seen its stock rise 5% since the opening. The film's $400 million budget is already recouped. Cameron, now 71, hinted at a possible spin-off series for Disney+. For moviegoers, this is a must-see on the biggest screen possible.
