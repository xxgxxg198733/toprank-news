10 Spiciest Dishes in the World — Eating #1 Is Basically a Medical Emergency

TR
TopRankJune 2, 2026125K views

From Sichuan to Thailand to Mexico, the world's spiciest dishes are not for the faint of heart. Some are so intense that restaurants require customers to sign liability waivers before eating. Here's our ranking from merely painful to genuinely dangerous.

10. Vindaloo (India)

The Goan classic made with ghost peppers and vinegar-marinated meat. Scoville rating: up to 200,000 SHU. Intense but manageable for spice veterans.

9. Sichuan Hot Pot (China)

A bubbling cauldron of Sichuan peppercorns and dried chilies. The numbing effect of the peppercorns makes the heat feel even more intense. Scoville: 150,000+ SHU.

8. Kimchi Jjigae (Korea)

Korean kimchi stew made with gochugaru (Korean chili flakes). The longer it ferments, the spicier it gets. Some grandmothers' recipes have been fermenting for years.

7. Jerk Chicken (Jamaica)

Scotch bonnet peppers give this Caribbean classic its distinctive heat. The allspice and thyme mask the danger until it's too late.

6. Phaal Curry (UK/India)

Invented in Birmingham's curry houses as a dare, phaal uses up to 10 different types of chili, including the fearsome bhut jolokia (ghost pepper).

5. Papa a la Huancaína (Peru)

Don't let the creamy sauce fool you. Authentic versions use rocoto peppers, which have a Scoville rating of up to 250,000 SHU.

4. Sambal Oelek (Indonesia)

A deceptively simple chili paste. The best versions use bird's eye chilies — small, innocent-looking, and devastatingly hot.

3. Tom Yum (Thailand)

When made authentically with fresh Thai bird chilies, this soup can reach 350,000 SHU. The sour lime and lemongrass provide brief moments of relief.

2. Carolina Reaper Wings (USA)

Any chicken wing sauced with Carolina Reaper peppers — currently the world's hottest pepper at 1.6-2.2 million SHU. Most restaurants make you sign a waiver.

1. Death Nut Challenge (USA)

The undisputed champion. Each level progresses through hotter peppers until you reach Level 5: peanuts coated in pure capsaicin extract at 13 million SHU. Hospital visits are not uncommon. The challenge's official website includes emergency room directions.
