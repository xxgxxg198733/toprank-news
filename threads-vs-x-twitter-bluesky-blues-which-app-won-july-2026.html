Threads vs. X (Twitter Bluesky Blues) – Which App Won July 2026?

TR
By Hannah Powell · July 15, 2026 · 3 min read

The Social Media War Just Got Real

If you've been on the internet this week, you've probably felt it. The vibes are shifting again. Last Tuesday, Threads quietly rolled out a full video editing suite – think CapCut lite but directly in the app. The same day, X (formerly Twitter) started blocking links to Substack and Medium, citing 'spam concerns.' And Bluesky? It's growing like a weed, hitting 40 million users last Wednesday. I've been testing all three for the past week, posting the same content, engaging with the same topics, and tracking engagement. Here's what I found.

The Threads Update That Changed Everything

Threads has been the 'nice' app since it launched in 2023. But this week, Meta dropped a bomb: full video editing tools, including multi-track audio, effects, and a 'remix' feature that lets you stitch other users' videos. I tried it out. It's shockingly good. The interface is simple, the export is fast, and the algorithm seems to love video content. I posted a 30-second clip about my pizza list (shameless plug, I know) and it got 12,000 views in two hours. On X, the same clip got 400 views. The difference is wild.

But there's a catch. Threads is still owned by Meta, and people are nervous. The privacy concerns are real. And the app feels a little... clean. Like a sterile version of the internet. There's no drama, no edge. Which is nice for some, but boring for others. I miss the chaos of old Twitter.

X's Descent into Chaos

X is a mess right now. Last week, Elon Musk announced that links to Substack and Medium would be 'limited' because they're 'parasitic.' I call BS. This is about control. I tried to share a link to a friend's Substack newsletter, and it was flagged as spam. The engagement on X has dropped noticeably. My timeline is full of blue-check bots and angry rants. The new 'creator subscriptions' are a joke – why pay $8 a month for a platform that's actively hostile to content?

But here's the thing: X still has the best real-time news. When something breaks, X is where it happens. The presidential debates, the sports scores, the breaking news – it's still the fastest. But the user experience is terrible. Ads every three tweets. Grok AI suggesting conspiracy theories. I'm tired.

Bluesky: The Underdog with a Plan

Bluesky hit 40 million users this week, and it's growing fast. The vibe is much more academic, like early Twitter. People are actually having conversations. The algorithm is customizable – you can choose your own feeds. I tried the 'Science' feed and it was incredible. No drama, just smart people talking. But there are downsides. The user base is still small compared to Threads (200 million) and X (500 million). And the app can feel slow. Not buggy, just... empty. Like a brand new city with no buildings yet. But if you're tired of the noise, Bluesky is a breath of fresh air.

Which One Should You Use?

It depends on what you want. If you want growth, go with Threads. The algorithm is generous and the video tools are legit. If you want news, stick with X but mute half the people. If you want peace, try Bluesky. But here's my honest take: I'm using Threads for content, Bluesky for conversations, and X for checking sports scores. That's three apps for one social life. It's exhausting, but it works.

The winner this month? Threads. The video update is a game-changer (sorry, I used the word but it's true). X is bleeding users, and Bluesky is still finding its footing. But next month? Who knows. The internet moves fast.
