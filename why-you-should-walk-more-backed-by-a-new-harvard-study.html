Why You Should Walk More (Backed by a New Harvard Study)

TR
By Sarah Mitchell · June 25, 2026 · 1 min read

I’ve never been a runner. I hate gyms. But I read a study from Harvard Medical School that came out on June 23, and it changed my mind about fitness. The study tracked 15,000 people over five years and found that walking just 6,000 steps a day—not the often-repeated 10,000—reduced cardiovascular disease risk by 40%. That’s huge. So I decided to try it for seven days.

My Experience

Day one was easy. I walked to the grocery store instead of driving. That got me 4,000 steps. I had to add an evening stroll to hit 6,000. By day three, I noticed I felt less sluggish. My back pain, which I’ve had for years, eased up. By day seven, I was sleeping better. I’m not saying walking cured me, but the difference was real.

The Science

The Harvard study is legit. Published in JAMA Internal Medicine, it controlled for age, diet, and other factors. The sweet spot was 6,000-8,000 steps. Going beyond 10,000 didn’t add much benefit. That’s liberating. You don’t need to be a marathoner. You just need to move consistently.

Practical Tips

I use a cheap pedometer app on my phone. I park farther away. I take phone calls while walking. It adds up fast. The key is consistency. I’m not saying I’ll do this forever, but for now, it’s the easiest health change I’ve ever made.

Bottom Line

If you’re overwhelmed by fitness trends, try walking. It’s free. It’s simple. And science says it works. Start today.
