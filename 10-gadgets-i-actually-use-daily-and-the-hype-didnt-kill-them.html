10 Gadgets I Actually Use Daily (And the Hype Didn't Kill Them)

TR
By Rachel Greene · July 23, 2026 · 5 min read

I'm going to be honest with you — most gadgets are trash. They arrive in sleek boxes, promise to change your life, and end up in a drawer within two weeks. But every once in a while, something sticks. Something earns a permanent spot on your desk, in your bag, or on your nightstand.

I've been testing gear professionally for about five years now. I'm not one of those YouTubers with a wall of keyboards and a ring light in every room. I'm just someone who buys stuff, uses it hard, and has opinions. Loud ones.

So here are 10 gadgets that have earned their place in my daily rotation. Not affiliate-bait. Not "the best you can buy" nonsense. Just stuff I reach for every single day.

1. The Framework Laptop (16-inch) — Because I'm Tired of Planned Obsolescence

I bought the Framework 16 in March, and it's the first laptop in years that doesn't feel like a disposable appliance. You can swap the GPU module, upgrade the RAM, replace the keyboard. When something breaks, you don't throw the whole machine away. It sounds like a small thing, but after three MacBooks with soldered SSDs, this feels like a revolution.

The build quality isn't quite MacBook level — the trackpad is just okay — but knowing I can fix it myself is worth the trade-off.

2. Sony WH-1000XM5 Headphones — Still the King of Quiet

I know everyone says this. But I've tried the AirPods Max (too heavy), the Bose QC Ultra (great but plasticky), and the Sennheiser Momentum 4 (better sound, worse ANC). The XM5s just work. The noise cancellation is so good I sometimes miss my train stop. Multipoint Bluetooth is flawless. Battery life is 30 hours.

Are they perfect? No. The case is stupidly large. But for my daily commute and coffee shop sessions, nothing beats them.

3. Anker 737 Power Bank (PowerCore 24K) — The Brick That Saves Me

I'm a heavy phone user. Like, 6+ hours of screen time heavy. This 24,000mAh beast charges my iPhone 15 Pro Max almost four times. It has a smart display that shows remaining capacity and estimated charge time. It's the size of a thick paperback, but I don't care — it's saved me on layovers, long hikes, and days when I forgot to charge overnight.

4. Logitech MX Master 3S — Boring, Comfortable, Perfect

This mouse looks like something from a 1990s sci-fi movie. It's chunky, has a weird thumb wheel, and costs $100. But I've been using it for two years and I can't go back. The scroll wheel is magnetic — it free-spins for long documents and clicks for precise lines. The ergonomics are perfect for my medium-sized hands. If you spend all day in front of a computer, this is the mouse.

5. Kindle Scribe — I Didn't Think I Needed E-Ink for Notes

I bought the Scribe as a joke. "Look at me, I'm fancy writing on a Kindle." But now I use it daily for meeting notes, journaling, and reading PDFs. The pen feels like a real pen — no slippery glass, no lag. The battery lasts weeks. It's replaced both my paper notebook and my tablet for reading. The only downside? The software is still a bit slow for heavy note-takers.

6. Nothing Phone (2) — Because Phones Should Be Fun Again

I've been using an iPhone as my daily driver for years, but the Nothing Phone (2) is my secondary device, and honestly? I look forward to using it. The Glyph lights on the back are silly but delightful — they pulse for notifications, show charging status, and even work as a timer. The software is clean, close to stock Android. The camera is decent, not great. But it's the first phone in years that made me smile.

7. Fujifilm Instax Mini Evo — The Social Camera

Digital cameras are everywhere. Film is expensive. But the Instax Mini Evo bridges the gap brilliantly. It's a hybrid — you shoot digitally, then choose which shots to print. So you don't waste film. The prints are tiny, imperfect, and nostalgic. I bring it to parties and dinners. People love seeing physical photos appear. It's not a serious camera, but it's the most fun one I own.

8. Oura Ring Gen 3 — Sleep Tracking That Doesn't Annoy Me

I hate wearing watches to bed. The Oura ring is light, unobtrusive, and gives me sleep data that actually helps. I learned I sleep worse after drinking (duh) and that my deep sleep improves when I skip evening screen time. The HRV tracking caught an early sign of overtraining before I felt it. Subscription fee sucks ($6/month), but the hardware is excellent.

9. Dyson Airwrap Complete — My Partner Convinced Me

Okay, I'm a guy with short hair, but my partner uses this every morning, and I've seen the difference. It's not a hair dryer — it's a styling tool that uses air to curl, smooth, and volumize without extreme heat. She says it's cut her styling time in half and her hair looks healthier. The price is absurd ($600), but for people who style daily, it's actually worth it.

10. Tile Slim — For My Wallet, Which I Lose Weekly

I lose my wallet about once a week. The Tile Slim is credit-card-sized and hides in my card slot. When I'm panicking, I open the app, ring it, and hear the faint chirp from under the couch cushions. It's not glamorous. It's not exciting. But it saves me 20 minutes of stress every week. Sometimes the best gadget is the one that prevents a meltdown.

So there you have it — ten things I actually use. Not sponsored. Not hyped. Just stuff that works. If you're looking for something to buy that won't end up in a drawer by August, start here.
