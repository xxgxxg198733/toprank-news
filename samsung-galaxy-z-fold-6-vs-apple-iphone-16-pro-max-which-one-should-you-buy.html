Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 vs Apple iPhone 16 Pro Max: Which One Should You Buy?

TR
By Matthew Anderson · July 22, 2026 · 4 min read

The Two Titans of 2026

Two phones dominate the conversation this year: Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold 6 and Apple's iPhone 16 Pro Max. I've been using both for the past month — the Fold 6 as my daily driver for two weeks, then the iPhone for two weeks. I've put them through real-world tests: camera battles, battery life, multitasking, and everyday usability.

Before I get into the nitty-gritty, a disclaimer: I'm not a fanboy of either brand. I've owned Samsung phones, iPhones, and even a OnePlus. I'm just a guy who wants a phone that works well and doesn't annoy me.

Design and Build: Samsung's Fold vs Apple's Slab

The Galaxy Z Fold 6 is a marvel of engineering. It folds into a compact brick that fits in my jeans pocket, then opens into a 7.6-inch tablet. The hinge feels solid — no wobble. But it's thick: 13.4mm folded. The iPhone 16 Pro Max is a traditional slab: 8.25mm thick, lighter, and easier to hold one-handed.

I'll be honest: the Fold 6 attracts attention. People ask me about it in coffee shops. But it's also fragile. The inner screen has a crease that's visible under light, and dust can get into the hinge. Apple's phone is boring by comparison, but it's also waterproof (IP68) and has a titanium frame that survived a drop from my hand onto concrete. The Fold? I wouldn't risk dropping it without a case.

Display: The Fold 6's Superpower

The Fold 6's main screen is stunning: a 7.6-inch Dynamic AMOLED with 120Hz refresh rate. Watching YouTube or reading PDFs is like having a mini-tablet. The iPhone 16 Pro Max has a 6.9-inch OLED, also 120Hz, but it's a single screen. For media consumption, the Fold wins. I watched Dune Part Two on the Fold and felt like I had a portable cinema.

But for normal use — scrolling Twitter, texting, taking photos — the iPhone's display is more practical. The Fold's cover screen is narrow (6.3 inches but 23.1:9 aspect ratio), making typing a chore. My thumbs kept hitting the wrong keys.

Camera: A Surprising Winner

I took both phones to a park in San Francisco last weekend. The iPhone 16 Pro Max has a 48MP main sensor, 48MP ultrawide, and 12MP telephoto with 5x optical zoom. The Fold 6 has a 50MP main, 12MP ultrawide, and 10MP telephoto with 3x optical zoom.

In daylight, both are excellent. But the iPhone's computational photography (powered by the A18 Pro chip) handles HDR better. A sunset shot with bright sun and dark shadows — the iPhone captured detail in both areas. The Fold 6 blew out the highlights. At night, the iPhone's low-light mode produces less noise. The Fold 6's photos are decent but not flagship-level.

The Fold 6 has a unique party trick: you can fold it halfway and use the bottom half as a stand for hands-free video calls. But if you care about camera quality, get the iPhone.

Performance and Software

Both phones are blazing fast. The Fold 6 uses Samsung's Exynos 2500 (or Snapdragon 8 Gen 4 in some regions), while the iPhone has the A18 Pro. In Geekbench 6, the iPhone scores about 15% higher in single-core performance. In real life, I didn't notice a difference — apps open instantly on both.

Software is where opinions diverge. One UI 6.1 on the Fold is feature-packed: split-screen multitasking, pop-up windows, and a taskbar. I ran Google Maps and YouTube side-by-side on the big screen, and it worked perfectly. iOS 20 on the iPhone is smoother and more intuitive, but it lacks the multitasking power. If you're a power user, the Fold wins. If you want simplicity, Apple wins.

Battery Life and Charging

The iPhone 16 Pro Max has a 4,700mAh battery and lasts me about 2 days with moderate use. The Fold 6 has a 4,800mAh battery but lasts only 1.5 days because the big screen draws more power. Charging: the iPhone supports 40W wired and 25W wireless; the Fold supports 45W wired and 15W wireless. Samsung charges faster, but Apple's battery optimization is better.

I'd give the edge to Apple here. The iPhone's standby time is incredible — I lost only 3% overnight, compared to 8% on the Fold.

Price and Value

The Galaxy Z Fold 6 starts at $1,899. The iPhone 16 Pro Max starts at $1,199. That's a $700 difference. Is the Fold worth it? Only if you'll actually use the folding screen. I know someone who bought a Fold and never opens it — they just use the cover screen. That's a waste of money.

Apple's ecosystem is also a factor: if you own a Mac, iPad, or AirPods, the iPhone integrates seamlessly. Samsung's ecosystem (Galaxy Watch, Buds) is good but not as polished.

The Verdict

I'm going to be honest: I'm recommending the iPhone 16 Pro Max for most people. It's more durable, has a better camera, longer battery life, and costs less. The Galaxy Z Fold 6 is an impressive novelty, but it's a niche product for early adopters who want the biggest screen possible.

If you're a multitasking powerhouse who reads documents on the go and doesn't mind the fragility, get the Fold. If you want a reliable flagship that does everything well, get the iPhone. For me, I'm sticking with Apple this year.
