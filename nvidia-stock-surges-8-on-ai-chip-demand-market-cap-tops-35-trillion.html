Nvidia Stock Surges 8% on AI Chip Demand, Market Cap Tops $3.5 Trillion

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 188,855 views

Nvidia Corporation saw its stock price surge over 8% in trading on Friday, June 5, 2026, reaching a new all-time high of $1,450 per share. The rally pushed the company's market capitalization past $3.5 trillion, making it the most valuable publicly traded company in the world, surpassing even Apple and Microsoft.

Record Earnings Driven by AI Boom

The surge comes after Nvidia reported its Q1 2027 earnings on Thursday, which blew past analyst expectations. Revenue hit $42 billion, a 45% year-over-year increase, driven primarily by its data center and AI chip divisions. CEO Jensen Huang attributed the growth to "the insatiable demand for AI computing power" from cloud providers, enterprises, and governments.

Analysts Raise Price Targets

Following the earnings report, at least 15 major Wall Street firms raised their price targets for Nvidia, with the highest now at $1,800 per share. Analysts cited the company's dominant position in the AI chip market, with an estimated 85% market share for AI training chips. The company also announced a 10-for-1 stock split effective later this month, making shares more accessible to retail investors.

Global Impact and Competition

Nvidia's success has broader implications for the tech industry and global markets. Competitors like AMD and Intel are racing to catch up, but Nvidia's software ecosystem and tight integration with AI frameworks like PyTorch and TensorFlow give it a significant advantage. The company also announced a new partnership with OpenAI to develop the next generation of AI models, further cementing its leadership.

Investors are now watching for potential regulatory scrutiny, as Nvidia's market dominance raises antitrust concerns in the US and Europe. However, for now, the company remains the undisputed king of the AI boom.
