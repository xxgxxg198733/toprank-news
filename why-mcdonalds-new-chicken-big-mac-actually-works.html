Why McDonald’s New Chicken Big Mac Actually Works

TR
By Samantha Cole · June 25, 2026 · 1 min read

When McDonald’s announced the Chicken Big Mac in early June, I rolled my eyes. Another gimmick, I thought. The original Big Mac is sacred. You don’t mess with perfection. But then I saw the lines at my local McDonald’s in Chicago. People were snapping them up. So I caved and bought one. And honestly? It’s good.

What’s Different

The Chicken Big Mac replaces the two beef patties with two breaded chicken patties. Everything else is the same—the special sauce, lettuce, cheese, pickles, and the middle bun. The chicken patties are crispy and juicy. They’re not dry like some fast-food chicken sandwiches. The sauce cuts through the richness well.

Comparing to the Original

It’s not better than the original Big Mac. But it’s a solid alternative. The texture is lighter. The chicken has a satisfying crunch that beef doesn’t. I ate the whole thing without feeling weighed down. That’s rare for McDonald’s. My only complaint: the chicken patties are smaller than the beef ones, so it feels slightly skimpy.

Social Media Hype

Twitter went wild when this launched. Some people called it a “game-changer.” Others hated it. The truth is somewhere in the middle. It’s a creative twist that works, but it’s not revolutionary. I’d order it again, but I’d still pick the original for nostalgia.

Should You Try It?

If you like chicken sandwiches, yes. If you’re a Big Mac purist, stay away. It’s a limited-time item, so grab one while you can. I give it a solid 7/10.
