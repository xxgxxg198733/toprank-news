Why Everyone Is Obsessed With the New Jersey Drone Sightings

TR
By Megan O'Brien · June 25, 2026 · 1 min read

If you’ve been on social media this week, you’ve seen the videos. Strange drones flying over suburban New Jersey at night—silent, hovering in formation, then disappearing. The sightings started in late June and have exploded. People are freaking out. So I spent two days looking into it.

What’s Happening

Since June 20, residents in Bergen County have reported clusters of drones with no visible markings. They appear around 10 PM and stay for about an hour. Local police can’t identify them. The FAA says they’re not commercial. The military says they’re not theirs. Conspiracy theories are running wild—aliens, Chinese spy drones, government testing. But the most plausible theory is simpler.

The Likely Explanation

I talked to a drone enthusiast on Reddit who lives in the area. He pointed out that a company called AeroVironment announced a new stealth drone prototype in May. The FAA has a test site in New Jersey. It’s likely a private company testing drones without public disclosure. That’s still sketchy, but it’s not aliens.

Why It Matters

This shows how easily fear spreads. I watched a TikTok with 2 million views claiming the drones were “scanning Wi-Fi networks.” No evidence. But people believe it because it’s mysterious. The truth is probably boring. But until someone official speaks up, the internet will keep spinning stories.

What I Think

I don’t think it’s a threat. But I do think the government should be transparent. If it’s a test, say so. The secrecy only fuels paranoia. In the meantime, I’m keeping an eye on the sky—and my phone.
