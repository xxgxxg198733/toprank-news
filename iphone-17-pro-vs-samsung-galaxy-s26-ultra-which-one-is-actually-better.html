iPhone 17 Pro vs. Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra: Which One Is Actually Better?

TR
By Hannah Powell · July 23, 2026 · 4 min read

Let's get this out of the way: both phones are incredible. The iPhone 17 Pro and Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra represent the absolute best of what each company can do. But here's the thing—you can only buy one. And after spending two weeks with both devices as my daily driver, I have a clear winner.

First, some context. I've been an Android user for most of my life. I switched to iPhone briefly with the 14 Pro, then went back. So I don't have a strong brand loyalty either way. I'm judging these phones on what actually matters: daily use, camera performance, battery life, and how they feel in your hand.

Design and Build: Apple Finally Caught Up

The iPhone 17 Pro is the first iPhone in years that feels genuinely new. They've moved to a titanium frame with a matte finish that doesn't attract fingerprints. The camera bump is now integrated into the body instead of sticking out like a sore thumb. It's beautiful, honestly.

The Galaxy S26 Ultra, on the other hand, looks almost identical to last year's model. It's still a massive slab of glass and metal with that signature S Pen slot. It's well-built, but it's starting to feel a bit stale design-wise. Samsung has been refining the same look for three generations now.

Winner: iPhone 17 Pro. It's not even close.

Display: Samsung Still Rules Here

But then you turn them on. The Galaxy S26 Ultra has a 6.9-inch Dynamic AMOLED display with a 150Hz refresh rate. The iPhone 17 Pro has a 6.7-inch LTPO OLED at 120Hz. On paper, Samsung wins. In practice, the difference is noticeable but not huge. Scrolling feels slightly smoother on the Samsung, and the colors are more vibrant. But Apple's color accuracy is better for photo editing.

For most people, you won't care. But if you watch a lot of HDR content, the Samsung is noticeably better. The peak brightness of 3,200 nits is insane—I could read the screen in direct sunlight without squinting.

Winner: Galaxy S26 Ultra.

Camera: This One Surprised Me

I expected the Samsung to destroy the iPhone here. Samsung has been pushing hardware specs—200MP sensors, 10x optical zoom, all that. But in real-world use, the iPhone 17 Pro takes better photos. The processing is more natural. Samsung's photos look oversharpened and saturated by comparison.

Low light is where the iPhone really shines. The new sensor captures more detail without introducing noise. Samsung's night mode is good, but it often makes scenes look like daytime—which isn't always what you want.

Video is no contest. iPhone has always been the king of video, and the 17 Pro is no exception. The new stabilization is witchcraft-level good.

Winner: iPhone 17 Pro.

Battery Life: A Surprising Tie

Both phones easily last a full day with heavy use. The Samsung has a larger battery (5,500mAh vs. 4,800mAh), but the iPhone is more efficient. In my testing, both ended the day at around 15-20% after 12 hours of mixed use. Charging is where Samsung wins—45W wired vs. 30W for Apple. But Apple's MagSafe is still the best wireless charging experience.

Winner: Tie. If you need fast charging, get the Samsung. If you use wireless charging, get the iPhone.

Ecosystem and Software

This is where personal preference matters most. iOS is smooth, simple, and works perfectly with Apple Watch, AirPods, and Mac. But it's also restrictive—you can't sideload apps or customize much. One UI 7 (Samsung's Android skin) is more flexible. You can do split-screen multitasking, run multiple apps in pop-up windows, and even use DeX to turn your phone into a desktop.

For power users, Samsung wins easily. For people who just want their phone to work without thinking about it, Apple is better.

Winner: Depends on your needs.

The Verdict

So which one should you buy? If you're coming from an iPhone, stick with the iPhone 17 Pro. It's a fantastic upgrade with the best camera system Apple has ever made. If you're coming from Android and want the absolute best hardware, the Galaxy S26 Ultra is still a beast—but the gap is narrower than ever.

My pick? iPhone 17 Pro. It's the first time in years I've felt like Apple is truly innovating again, while Samsung feels like it's coasting on past success. But I'm keeping both for another week to be sure. I'll update this if my opinion changes.
