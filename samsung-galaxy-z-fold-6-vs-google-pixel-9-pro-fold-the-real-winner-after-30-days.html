Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 vs. Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold: The Real Winner After 30 Days of Testing

TR
By Lauren Davis · July 24, 2026 · 6 min read

I have a problem. I own two foldable phones. Actually, I own three, but we don't talk about the OnePlus Open anymore (it's fine, it's just not in this conversation). For the last month, I've been carrying the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 and the Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold in my bag, swapping my SIM card between them every few days. My partner thinks I've lost my mind. My wallet definitely has.

But someone has to do this work. Both of these phones launched in the last two months and both cost over $1,800. If you're thinking about buying one, you need to know which one actually works in real life — not just on paper or in YouTube reviews filmed in perfect lighting. I live in a rainy city, I drop things, and I take photos of my cat more than anything else. Here's what I learned.

First, Let's Talk About the Hardware

The Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 came out in late June 2026. It's thinner and lighter than the Fold 5 — Samsung finally listened to everyone complaining about the weight. It's 239 grams now, which is still heavy but manageable. The cover screen is slightly wider, which makes typing less of a nightmare. I can actually reply to texts without autocorrect turning "sounds good" into "sound good" every single time.

The Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold landed in early July. It's 245 grams — a bit heavier, but it feels more balanced in the hand. Google redesigned the hinge completely. The previous Pixel Fold had a hinge that felt stiff and made a weird creaking noise after a few months. This one is smooth, silent, and opens flat. Like, completely flat. Samsung's still has a tiny gap when closed, which lets dust in. I found lint in my Fold 5's hinge after six months. Not great.

Winner on hardware: Google, by a narrow margin. The flat fold and smoother hinge win it.

Cameras: Google's Superpower vs. Samsung's Consistency

This is where it gets interesting. Samsung put a 50MP main sensor, a 12MP ultrawide, and a 10MP telephoto with 3x optical zoom in the Fold 6. It's the same camera setup as the S25 Ultra, basically. That means good photos — consistent, reliable, never embarrassing. But also not exciting. Samsung's processing makes everything look slightly oversaturated. My cat looks like an animated character sometimes.

Google's Pixel 9 Pro Fold has a 64MP main sensor, a 12MP ultrawide, and a 10.8MP telephoto with 5x optical zoom. The difference in zoom is real. I took photos of a sign from 20 meters away on both phones. The Pixel's was readable. Samsung's was a blurry mess. Google's computational photography is still the best in the business. Night mode on the Pixel is genuinely spooky — it makes darkness look like golden hour.

But — and this is a big but — the Pixel's video is worse. Samsung records video that looks smooth, stable, and natural. Google's video has this weird jitteriness, like it's struggling to keep up. If you film a lot of moving subjects (kids, pets, sports), Samsung wins. If you take mostly photos, Google wins.

I'm giving this category to Google, but only by a hair. If Samsung fixes its video processing, it's game over.

Battery Life: The Surprise Result

I expected Samsung to win here. The Fold 6 has a 4,400mAh battery. The Pixel 9 Pro Fold has a 4,650mAh battery. Both support 45W charging. But in real-world testing, the Pixel lasted longer. Every single day.

I'm a heavy user — emails, Slack, YouTube, podcasts, Instagram, and about an hour of reading on the inner screen. The Samsung would hit 15% by 8 PM. The Pixel would be at 25-30%. I don't know exactly why. Maybe Google's Tensor G5 chip is more efficient than Samsung's Snapdragon 8 Gen 4. Maybe the software is better optimized. Whatever it is, I'm charging the Pixel every night but the Samsung every afternoon.

Winner: Pixel. No contest.

The Inner Screen Experience

Both phones have beautiful 7.6-inch inner screens. Both run at 120Hz. Both are bright enough to use in direct sunlight. But the crease — that line down the middle where the screen folds — is less noticeable on the Pixel. Samsung's crease has gotten better over the years, but it's still there. You feel it when you swipe. Google's crease is barely visible and almost impossible to feel.

Also, Google's aspect ratio is better for watching video. It's squarer, which means less letterboxing. Samsung's tall, narrow inner screen makes YouTube videos look tiny unless you zoom in and crop them. I watch a lot of YouTube. This matters to me.

Winner: Pixel again.

Software: One UI vs. Pixel Experience

Samsung's One UI 7 (based on Android 16) is packed with features. Multi-window, floating apps, a taskbar, DeX mode — you can do almost anything. The problem is, a lot of these features are buried in menus I'll never find. I've been using Samsung phones for years and I still discover new settings by accident.

Google's Pixel software is simpler. Cleaner. It's Android the way Google intended. The foldable-specific features — like app pairs that open together — work well and are easy to set up. Google also promises 7 years of OS updates. Samsung promises 7 years too, but historically, Google's updates arrive faster.

I prefer Google's approach. I don't need my phone to be a desktop computer. I need it to not annoy me. The Pixel doesn't annoy me. The Samsung does, occasionally, when I can't find a setting or when Bixby pops up by accident (yes, Bixby still exists, and I hate it).

Winner: Pixel.

Price and Value

Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6: $1,899.99. Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold: $1,799.99. That's a $100 difference. Not nothing, but not huge either. Both are absurdly expensive for a phone. But if I'm spending this much, I want the best experience. And for my money, the Pixel delivers more.

Samsung has better trade-in deals, though. If you have an old phone, you might get $600-800 off from Samsung. Google's trade-in values are lower. So the actual price you pay depends on what you're trading.

My Final Verdict

After 30 days, the Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold is the better foldable phone. Better battery, better cameras (for photos), better software, better hinge. It feels more polished, more thoughtful.

But — and this matters — Samsung's Fold 6 is more reliable for video, has better accessories (the S Pen is genuinely useful for notes), and Samsung's customer service is less of a nightmare than Google's. If something breaks, Google might make you wait weeks. Samsung has physical stores.

So here's my honest advice: If you take photos, read a lot, and want the phone to just work, get the Pixel 9 Pro Fold. If you shoot video, use the S Pen, or need reliable support, get the Galaxy Z Fold 6.

Me? I'm keeping the Pixel. But I'm keeping my old Samsung as a backup. Just in case.
