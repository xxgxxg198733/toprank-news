'Minecraft Movie' Trailer Breaks Internet, Sets Record for Most Views in 24 Hours

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

Warner Bros. released the first official trailer for the live-action "Minecraft" movie on June 5, 2026, and it quickly became the most-watched trailer in the first 24 hours in history. According to the studio, the trailer garnered over 250 million views across YouTube, TikTok, and other platforms, surpassing the previous record held by "Avengers: Endgame."

The Trailer's Content

The trailer stars Jason Momoa as Steve, the iconic blocky protagonist, alongside an ensemble cast including Emma Stone, Jack Black, and Zendaya. It opens with Momoa waking up in a pixelated world, then quickly escalates into a comedic and action-packed adventure. Key moments include a giant Creeper chase, a battle against the Ender Dragon, and a hilarious scene where Jack Black's character tries to craft a diamond sword. The visual effects, a blend of live-action and CGI, have been praised for faithfully recreating the game's aesthetic while adding a fresh cinematic feel.

Social Media Reaction

Fans and critics alike are buzzing. On Twitter (now X), the trailer trended worldwide for over 12 hours. Early reviews from film bloggers are positive, with many noting the film's humor and heart. Director Jared Hess ("Napoleon Dynamite") said in a statement, "We wanted to capture the creativity and joy of Minecraft while telling a story that resonates with both players and newcomers." The movie is set for release on December 18, 2026, just in time for the holiday season.

Box Office Predictions

Analysts are already predicting a massive opening weekend, with early estimates ranging from $150 million to $200 million domestically. If those numbers hold, it could become the highest-grossing video game movie of all time, surpassing the "Super Mario Bros. Movie" ($1.36 billion worldwide). The success of the trailer also boosted stock prices for Warner Bros. Discovery and Mojang Studios. The "Minecraft" movie is shaping up to be the must-see event of the holiday season.
