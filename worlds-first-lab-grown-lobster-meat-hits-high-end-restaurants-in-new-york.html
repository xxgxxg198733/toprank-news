World's First Lab-Grown Lobster Meat Hits High-End Restaurants in New York

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 173,914 views

Diners in New York City will now have the chance to taste the world's first lab-grown lobster meat, starting June 5, 2026. The product, developed by the San Francisco-based startup 'CrustaCell,' is being served at three Michelin-starred restaurants: Le Bernardin, Per Se, and Eleven Madison Park. The launch aims to address overfishing in the Atlantic.

The Science Behind the Meat

CrustaCell uses a process called cellular agriculture, where lobster cells are harvested from a live crustacean and then fed a nutrient-rich solution in bioreactors. The cells multiply and form muscle tissue, which is then harvested and shaped into lobster tails and claws. The entire process takes about 4 weeks, compared to years for wild lobsters.

First Reactions and Cost

Early reviews are positive. Chef Eric Ripert of Le Bernardin described the texture as 'uncannily similar to wild lobster,' noting a slightly milder flavor. However, the cost is prohibitive: a single lab-grown lobster tail currently costs $150, making it a luxury item. CrustaCell says scaling up production could reduce the price to $30 within three years.

Environmental and Ethical Implications

The company claims its process uses 90% less water and 95% less land than traditional lobster fishing. Animal rights groups have praised the move, as no lobsters are killed in the process. However, some conservationists worry that it could reduce pressure to protect wild lobster habitats. The U.S. FDA has approved the product as safe for consumption.
