Global Heatwave Shatters Records: Europe, Asia, and US Brace for 45°C+ Temperatures

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 352,713 views

An unprecedented heatwave is scorching parts of Europe, Asia, and the United States, with temperatures soaring past 45°C (113°F) in several locations. On June 3, 2026, Madrid, Spain hit 46.2°C, breaking the city's all-time record, while Delhi, India reached 49.5°C—among the highest ever recorded. The extreme weather has prompted emergency measures across the globe, from school closures to rolling blackouts.

Europe Under Siege

Southern Europe is bearing the brunt of the heat, with Italy, Greece, and Spain declaring states of emergency. In Rome, the government opened cooling centers and distributed free water bottles to tourists, while Athens saw a spike in heatstroke cases. The heatwave has also fueled wildfires in Portugal and Croatia, destroying thousands of acres of forest. Meteorologists blame a persistent 'heat dome' over the Mediterranean, exacerbated by climate change.

Asia's Deadly Toll

In South Asia, the heatwave has turned deadly. India's Health Ministry reported over 500 heat-related deaths in the past week, with the most vulnerable—construction workers and the elderly—hit hardest. Pakistan's city of Jacobabad recorded 52.3°C, approaching the theoretical limit for human survivability. Bangladesh and Nepal have also issued heat alerts, with power grids straining under record demand for air conditioning.

US and Global Response

In the United States, the Southwest is baking under triple-digit temperatures, with Phoenix, Arizona hitting 47°C on June 2. California's grid operator has urged residents to conserve energy to avoid blackouts. Scientists warn that such extreme events will become more frequent. 'This is not an anomaly—it's the new normal,' said Dr. Michael Mann of the University of Pennsylvania. 'Without drastic emissions reductions, summers like this will be routine by 2040.' As the world swelters, governments scramble to adapt to a rapidly warming planet.
