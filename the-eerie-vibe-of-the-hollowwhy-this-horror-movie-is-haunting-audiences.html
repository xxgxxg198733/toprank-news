The Eerie Vibe of 'The Hollow'—Why This Horror Movie Is Haunting Audiences

TR
By TopRank Team · June 19, 2026 · 1 min read

I love horror movies, but most of them don't scare me anymore. Jump scares are cheap. Gore is just gross. But The Hollow, which came out last Friday, messed me up. I saw it at a packed theater, and when the credits rolled, nobody spoke. People just sat there, shaken. That's rare. Let me tell you why this movie works.

The Plot: Simple but Effective

A family moves into a Victorian house in rural Maine. The husband (played by a fantastic Aaron Taylor-Johnson) is a writer looking for inspiration. The wife (Florence Pugh) is a therapist. Their daughter (newcomer Mia Sinclair) starts talking to an imaginary friend. But the friend isn't imaginary. It's a ghost. That sounds generic, but the execution is anything but.

What Makes It Scary: The Sound Design

Most horror relies on loud noises. The Hollow uses silence. There's a scene where Florence Pugh is walking down a hallway, and all you hear is the creak of floorboards. Then nothing. For 30 seconds. The audience held their breath. Then a whisper. Not a scream. A whisper. It was terrifying. I've never seen a film use sound like this.

The Performances Are Stellar

Florence Pugh is incredible. She plays a woman slowly losing her grip, and you feel every moment. Aaron Taylor-Johnson is good too, but Pugh carries the film. The real star is the little girl, Mia Sinclair. She's creepy without trying. Her blank stares made me uncomfortable.

Why It's Not for Everyone

This isn't a fun horror movie. It's slow, atmospheric, and depressing. The ending is bleak. Some people in my theater walked out. If you want a quick scare, watch something else. But if you want a film that stays with you for days, see The Hollow.
