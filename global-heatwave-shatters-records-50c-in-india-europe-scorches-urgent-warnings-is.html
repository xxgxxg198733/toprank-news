Global Heatwave Shatters Records: 50°C in India, Europe Scorches – Urgent Warnings Issued

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 445,621 views

A massive heatwave sweeping across the Northern Hemisphere on June 3, 2026, has shattered temperature records, with Delhi hitting 50.2°C (122°F) and Paris reaching 44°C (111°F). Meteorologists attribute the event to a persistent high-pressure system and climate change.

India in Crisis: Deaths and Power Outages

In India, the heatwave has claimed over 200 lives in the past week, particularly among outdoor workers. Power grids strained as air conditioner demand surged, causing rolling blackouts. The government declared a national emergency, opening cooling centers and urging people to stay indoors. The monsoon season, expected to bring relief, is delayed by two weeks.

Europe Bakes: Wildfires and Travel Disruption

In Europe, wildfires have erupted in Spain, Italy, and Greece, forcing evacuations of tourist resorts. The UK issued its first-ever 'red extreme heat' warning, with London hitting 40°C. Airports in Paris and Rome limited flights due to heat-induced runway damage. Environmentalists point to insufficient climate action as a cause.

Health and Economic Toll

Hospitals report a surge in heatstroke cases, especially among elderly and children. The economic cost is estimated at $10 billion daily, from lost productivity to agricultural damage. As the heatwave is forecast to persist for another week, leaders are calling for accelerated climate adaptation measures, including urban tree cover and renewable energy expansion.
