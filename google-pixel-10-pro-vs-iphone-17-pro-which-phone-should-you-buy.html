Google Pixel 10 Pro vs iPhone 17 Pro: Which Phone Should You Buy?

TR
By David Kim · June 24, 2026 · 5 min read

The Google Pixel 10 Pro and iPhone 17 Pro launched within days of each other this month. And for the first time in years, I actually had a hard time choosing between them. Both phones are incredibly good. Both have flaws. And both cost way too much money.

I've been running both as my daily drivers for the past two weeks. I took them on runs, used them for work, took photos at a friend's wedding, and even dropped them (accidentally) to test durability. Here's my honest breakdown of which phone wins in each category — and which one I'm keeping.

Design and Build: Close, But Different Vibes

The iPhone 17 Pro feels like a Rolex. It's heavy, solid, and impeccably built. The titanium frame is a nice upgrade from last year's steel, and the new frosted glass back is gorgeous. The camera bump is still absurdly large, but it's grown on me. The phone is comfortable to hold, even one-handed, thanks to the slightly rounded edges.

The Pixel 10 Pro, on the other hand, feels like a Tesla. It's lighter (203g vs 221g), and the recycled aluminum frame feels modern and sustainable. The camera bar is polarizing — some people hate it, but I think it's a clever design that keeps the phone from wobbling on a table. The matte finish is a fingerprint magnet, though.

Winner: iPhone 17 Pro, but only barely.

Display: Pixel Takes the Crown

I didn't expect this. Google has been playing catch-up on displays for years, but the Pixel 10 Pro's OLED panel is stunning. It hits 3000 nits peak brightness, which made it perfectly readable under direct sunlight during my morning runs. The colors are vibrant without being oversaturated, and the 120Hz refresh rate is buttery smooth.

The iPhone 17 Pro's display is no slouch — it's also OLED with 120Hz, and Apple's color calibration is still the gold standard. But the Pixel's display gets brighter and has better viewing angles. Side by side, the Pixel just looks more alive.

Winner: Google Pixel 10 Pro.

Cameras: The Pixel Still Has the Magic

Look, the iPhone 17 Pro takes great photos. The new 48MP main sensor captures incredible detail, and the telephoto lens with 5x optical zoom is a massive improvement over last year's 3x. The video is still the best in the business — cinematic mode at 4K 60fps is insane.

But the Pixel 10 Pro has this thing that's hard to quantify. It just captures the moment better. The new AI-powered Real Tone 2.0 is incredible — skin tones look natural across all lighting conditions. The Magic Editor is finally useful: I removed a photobomber from a group photo in seconds, and you couldn't tell it was edited. And the low-light performance is unreal. I took a photo of the Milky Way with a 4-second exposure, and it looked like a professional astrophotography shot.

For video, the iPhone wins. For photos, the Pixel wins. It's that simple.

Winner: Google Pixel 10 Pro (for photos), iPhone 17 Pro (for video).

Battery Life: A Surprising Result

I ran a battery test last Wednesday. I charged both phones to 100%, then used them normally — emails, social media, Spotify, GPS navigation for 30 minutes, and an hour of YouTube. The Pixel 10 Pro lasted 14 hours and 23 minutes. The iPhone 17 Pro lasted 13 hours and 11 minutes.

The Pixel's battery is slightly larger (5,200 mAh vs 4,800 mAh), and the Tensor G5 chip is more power-efficient than I expected. The iPhone's A19 Bionic is faster, but it drinks more power. Both phones support fast charging — the Pixel hits 50% in 20 minutes with a 45W charger, while the iPhone hits 50% in 25 minutes with a 35W charger.

Winner: Google Pixel 10 Pro.

AI Features: Google Is Years Ahead

This is where the Pixel crushes the iPhone. Google's AI features are genuinely useful. Call Screen is amazing — I haven't talked to a spam caller in weeks. The new Live Translate works in real-time during phone calls, which is incredible if you do business internationally. And the Gemini assistant is leagues ahead of Siri. I asked it to plan a weekend trip to Portland, and it gave me a full itinerary with flights, hotels, and restaurant recommendations in 10 seconds.

Apple's AI features are catching up, but they're not there yet. The new Apple Intelligence tools are useful for summarizing emails and generating images, but Siri still struggles with complex requests. And the on-device processing means it's slower than Google's cloud-based approach.

Winner: Google Pixel 10 Pro, by a lot.

Performance: iPhone Roars Ahead

Okay, I have to be honest here. The iPhone 17 Pro is brutally fast. The A19 Bionic chip is a monster — apps open instantly, games run at 120fps without a single stutter, and 8K video editing is smooth. The Pixel 10 Pro's Tensor G5 is fine for daily use, but it can't keep up in heavy workloads. I ran Geekbench 6, and the iPhone scored 8,900 vs the Pixel's 6,200.

If you're a gamer or a video editor, the iPhone is the obvious choice. The Pixel is more than fast enough for 99% of people, but if you push your phone hard, the iPhone wins.

Winner: iPhone 17 Pro.

Software and Ecosystem: It Depends on Your Lifestyle

This is a personal choice. I prefer Android's notification system and customization options. But iMessage, FaceTime, and AirDrop are powerful if your friends and family are in Apple's ecosystem. The Pixel's software is clean, fast, and gets updates first. The iPhone's software is stable, secure, and integrated.

There's no clear winner here. If you're invested in Apple's ecosystem, you'll be happier with the iPhone. If you want flexibility and customization, go with the Pixel.

Verdict: Which One Should You Buy?

I'm keeping the Pixel 10 Pro. The camera, battery life, and AI features are more important to me than raw performance. But if you're a gamer, a video creator, or heavily invested in Apple's ecosystem, the iPhone 17 Pro is the better choice. Both phones are excellent — you won't be disappointed either way. Just don't buy them full price. Wait a month and they'll be $200 cheaper.
