Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 6 vs. Motorola Razr Plus 2026: Which Foldable Wins?

TR
By Amanda Brooks · July 22, 2026 · 4 min read

Let me start by saying I was a skeptic. When foldable phones first showed up, I thought they were a solution in search of a problem. But after two weeks with both the Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 6 and the Motorola Razr Plus (2026 edition), I've changed my mind. These things are actually good now. The question is: which one should you buy?

I used both phones as my daily drivers — calls, texts, photos, social media, even some light gaming. I dropped one (accidentally, I swear), took them on a rainy walk, and tested the cameras in low light. Here's the real story, not the marketing fluff.

Design and Build: The Flip Feels More Premium

The Galaxy Z Flip 6 has a glossy glass back and a metal frame that feels solid in the hand. It's a bit thicker than a regular phone when folded, but it's still pocketable. The hinge is tight — no wobble. Motorola's Razr Plus has a vegan leather back that's grippy and nice to hold, but it feels slightly less premium. The hinge on the Razr is also good, but Samsung's feels more refined this year. Both are IP48 water-resistant (splash proof, not dunk-proof). Honestly, Samsung edges ahead here because of the build quality.

Display: Samsung's Main Screen Wins, Motorola's Cover Screen Shines

The Flip 6 has a 6.7-inch AMOLED main display with a 120Hz refresh rate. It's bright — 2600 nits peak brightness — and colors pop. The crease in the middle is still visible but less noticeable than last year. The Razr Plus also has a 6.9-inch pOLED display at 165Hz, which is smoother for scrolling, but the crease is more prominent. But here's the thing: Motorola's cover screen is huge — 4.1 inches — and it's actually useful. You can reply to texts, use Google Maps, and even play simple games without opening the phone. Samsung's cover screen is a tiny 3.4 inches and feels cramped. For quick interactions, Motorola wins.

Camera: I Was Surprised by the Razr

I expected Samsung to dominate here, but Motorola surprised me. The Z Flip 6 has a 50MP main camera and a 12MP ultrawide. Photos are sharp, with good dynamic range, but colors can be oversaturated. The Razr Plus has a 50MP main, a 12MP ultrawide, and a 10MP telephoto with 2x optical zoom. The telephoto made a difference — I could zoom in on subjects without losing detail. Low-light performance was comparable, but the Razr's night mode handled highlights better. For selfies, both use the main camera with the cover screen as a viewfinder. It's a tie. But if you zoom a lot, the Razr wins.

Performance: Samsung's Chipset Edges Ahead

The Flip 6 runs on the Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 for Galaxy, while the Razr Plus uses the MediaTek Dimensity 9300. In real-world use, both are fast. Apps open instantly, scrolling is smooth, and gaming is fine. But in benchmarks, Samsung's chip is about 15% faster. I noticed it when editing video — the Flip 6 exported clips quicker. For daily tasks, you won't notice. But if you're a power user, Samsung has the edge.

Battery Life: Motorola Lasts Longer

This was the biggest surprise. The Flip 6 has a 4000mAh battery, while the Razr Plus packs 4500mAh. In my testing, the Razr consistently lasted a full day with heavy use — about 7 hours of screen-on time. The Flip 6 was closer to 5.5 hours. Both support fast charging (25W for Samsung, 30W for Motorola), but wireless charging is faster on the Samsung. If battery life matters to you, get the Razr.

Software and Features: Samsung's One UI Is More Polished

Samsung's One UI 6.1 is mature and packed with features — DeX mode, Samsung Health, Good Lock customization. It also gets four years of OS updates. Motorola's MyUX is cleaner but lacks some features. The Razr gets three years of updates. Both have AI features (Samsung's Galaxy AI, Motorola's Moto AI), but Samsung's are more integrated — like Live Translate and Circle to Search. Motorola's AI is fine, but not as useful.

Price and Value: Motorola Wins on Affordability

The Galaxy Z Flip 6 starts at $999, while the Razr Plus starts at $899. Both are expensive, but the Razr offers more for less — bigger cover screen, longer battery, telephoto camera. Samsung has better software support and a slightly better build. But for $100 less, Motorola is the better value.

Final Verdict: It Depends on What You Value

If you want the best build quality, longer software support, and a more polished ecosystem, get the Galaxy Z Flip 6. If you want a bigger cover screen, better battery life, and a telephoto camera for less money, get the Motorola Razr Plus. For me personally, I'd pick the Razr Plus. The battery life alone made it easier to live with. But your mileage may vary.

Either way, foldables are here to stay. And honestly? They're finally good enough to recommend.
