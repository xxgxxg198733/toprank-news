Top 10 Most Popular Dog Breeds of 2026: French Bulldog Dethroned by Labrador Retriever

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 337,778 views

The American Kennel Club (AKC) has released its annual list of the most popular dog breeds in the United States for 2026, and there's a shake-up at the top. After three years of the French Bulldog holding the number one spot, the Labrador Retriever has reclaimed its throne. The AKC's ranking, based on registration data from 2025, shows that Americans are once again favoring the friendly, energetic Labrador.

The Top 10 for 2026

Here is the full top 10 list: 1. Labrador Retriever (reclaiming #1), 2. French Bulldog (dropping to #2), 3. Golden Retriever (steady at #3), 4. German Shepherd Dog (#4), 5. Poodle (#5), 6. Bulldog (#6), 7. Beagle (#7), 8. Rottweiler (#8), 9. Dachshund (#9), 10. Pembroke Welsh Corgi (#10). The Corgi enters the top 10 for the first time, reflecting a surge in popularity driven by social media and the breed's association with the British royal family.

— Advertisement —

Why the Shift?

The rise of the Labrador Retriever can be attributed to its reputation as an ideal family dog, known for its patience with children and trainability. The French Bulldog's decline may be linked to growing awareness of its health issues, including breathing problems and spinal disorders. Vets have increasingly warned against breeding extreme brachycephalic (flat-faced) dogs. Meanwhile, the Corgi's popularity is fueled by viral videos and its role in the popular TV series 'The Crown: New Generation.'

Breeding and Responsibility

The AKC emphasizes that breed popularity shouldn't be the sole factor in choosing a pet. Puppy scams and irresponsible breeding remain concerns. Prospective owners are urged to research breeds carefully and consider adopting from shelters. Notably, mixed-breed dogs remain the most common type of pet in the U.S., but the AKC's list reflects the preferences of purebred registrations.
