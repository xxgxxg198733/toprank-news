Inside the Mega Millions Jackpot: $1.2 Billion Winner Announced in California

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 170,722 views

A life-changing moment occurred on the night of June 2, 2026, when a single Mega Millions ticket sold in California matched all six numbers for a staggering $1.2 billion annuity prize. The winning numbers were 7, 14, 23, 38, 45, and Mega Ball 11. The ticket was purchased at a 7-Eleven in downtown Los Angeles. This is the third-largest lottery jackpot in U.S. history, trailing only the $1.6 billion Powerball in 2023.

The Winner: Anonymous or Not?

As of June 4, the winner has not come forward. California law requires lottery winners to be publicly named, but they can form a trust or LLC to claim the prize anonymously. Lottery officials advise winners to sign the ticket, lock it in a safe, and hire a team of lawyers and financial advisors immediately. The store that sold the winning ticket will receive a $1 million bonus. The odds of winning were 1 in 302 million, making this a truly random event.

Where Does the Money Go?

The winner can choose a lump sum of approximately $645 million before taxes, or the full $1.2 billion paid over 30 years. Federal taxes will take about 37%, and California state taxes add another 13.3%. That leaves roughly $320 million for the lump sum option. Historically, winners often go bankrupt within five years, so financial planning is critical. The lottery also raises funds for public schools; California's education system will receive an estimated $100 million from this draw.

How to Play (and Not Get Scammed)

If you feeling lucky, remember that no official lottery will ask you to pay fees upfront to claim a prize. Always buy tickets from licensed retailers. The next Mega Millions drawing is June 5, with a reset jackpot of $20 million. For now, all eyes are on the mystery winner in LA. Will they remain anonymous? Will they donate to charity? Only time will tell. One thing is certain: their life will never be the same.
