Battle of the Flagships: Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra vs. iPhone 17 Pro Max vs. Google Pixel 11 Pro

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 318,829 views

The smartphone wars are heating up in 2026, with Samsung, Apple, and Google all releasing their flagship devices within weeks of each other. We've put the Galaxy S26 Ultra, iPhone 17 Pro Max, and Pixel 11 Pro through rigorous testing to help you decide which one to buy. Here's our head-to-head comparison.

Camera Face-Off

The Galaxy S26 Ultra features a 300-megapixel main sensor with variable aperture, offering stunning low-light performance and 8K video at 60fps. The iPhone 17 Pro Max sticks with a 48-megapixel sensor but adds a new 'Quadra-Lens' system for computational photography that produces incredibly natural colors and depth. However, the Pixel 11 Pro takes the crown with its 'Magic Editor 3.0,' powered by Google's Gemini Pro AI, which can remove objects, change lighting, and even animate photos with terrifying accuracy. In our blind tests, 40% of participants preferred the Pixel's photos, 35% chose the Galaxy, and 25% chose the iPhone.

Performance and Battery

All three phones use cutting-edge 3nm chips: the Galaxy uses the Snapdragon 8 Gen 5, the iPhone uses the A19 Bionic, and the Pixel uses the Tensor G6. In benchmark tests, the iPhone leads in single-core performance, while the Galaxy dominates in multi-core and GPU tasks. The Pixel, however, excels in AI-driven tasks like real-time translation and voice recognition. Battery life is a surprise win for Samsung, with the S26 Ultra lasting 14 hours and 20 minutes in our video playback test, compared to 13 hours for the iPhone and 12.5 hours for the Pixel. All three support 65W+ fast charging, but only the Galaxy includes a charger in the box.

Software and Ecosystem

iOS 20 on the iPhone 17 Pro Max is polished and secure, with new widgets and improved multitasking. Samsung's One UI 7 on Android 16 offers unprecedented customization and a new 'DeX Pro' mode for desktop-like computing. Google's stock Android experience on the Pixel 11 Pro is clean and receives updates first, but its standout feature is the 'AI Assistant 2.0' that can book appointments, order groceries, and control smart home devices effortlessly. Verdict? If you're deep in the Apple ecosystem, get the iPhone. For raw power and flexibility, choose the Galaxy. And if you want the smartest AI phone on the market, the Pixel 11 Pro is your best bet.
