Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 vs. Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold: Which Folding Phone Actually Works?

TR
By Ryan Cooper · July 21, 2026 · 4 min read

I've been skeptical about folding phones since day one. They felt like expensive experiments — cool demos for tech YouTube channels, not things real people should buy. But after spending a week with both the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 and the Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold, I'm starting to change my mind. Slightly.

Both phones launched this summer. Samsung's Z Fold 6 came out in late June, and Google's Pixel 9 Pro Fold hit stores in early July. I bought both — yes, I spent way too much money — and used them as my daily drivers. Here's what I found.

Design and Build Quality

The Samsung feels like a brick. A really polished, expensive brick. It's thick — thicker than two regular phones stacked together. But it's also solid. The hinge feels tight, like a well-made door. Samsung's been doing this for five generations, and it shows. The outer screen is narrow, which took me a day to get used to, but it works for quick replies and maps.

The Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold is thinner and lighter. It feels more like a regular phone that happens to open up. The outer screen is wider, which makes typing less cramped. But the hinge feels looser. I worried about durability. Google's first folding phone had some issues, and the second generation still doesn't inspire the same confidence as Samsung's.

Winner for design: Google. It's more practical as a daily phone. Winner for build quality: Samsung. It feels like it could survive a drop.

The Crease Problem

Here's the thing nobody tells you: the crease is still there. On both phones. Samsung's is less noticeable because it's been working on the display tech longer. Google's crease is deeper and more visible when the screen is off. When it's on, you forget about it — until you swipe across it and feel the bump. It's not a dealbreaker, but it's annoying.

Samsung wins this round. The crease is less prominent, and the screen feels smoother.

Software Experience

This is where Google kills it. The Pixel 9 Pro Fold runs pure Android. No bloatware, no duplicate apps, no weird Samsung versions of Google apps. Everything is smooth. The multitasking works better too — you can open two apps side by side, and Google's software handles the transition seamlessly.

Samsung's One UI is better than it used to be, but it's still cluttered. Bixby is still there. Samsung's browser is still there. There's a learning curve. If you want a phone that just works, get the Pixel.

Google wins software, no contest.

Camera Performance

I took both phones to a park and shot the same scenes. Samsung's cameras are good — sharp, vibrant, but sometimes oversaturated. Google's cameras are great. The Pixel's computational photography is still the best in the business. Low-light shots were clearer. Portrait mode had better edge detection. The selfie camera on the Pixel is also better, which matters if you take a lot of video calls.

Google wins cameras, hands down.

Battery Life and Charging

Samsung's battery is slightly larger, and it shows. The Z Fold 6 lasted about a day and a half with moderate use. The Pixel 9 Pro Fold barely made it through a full day. Charging speeds are similar — both take about an hour to go from zero to full. But Samsung has wireless charging, which the Pixel lacks if you buy the base model.

Samsung wins battery life. But neither is great. Folding phones still have battery compromises.

Price and Value

The Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6 starts at $1,899. The Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold starts at $1,799. That's $100 less, but both are absurdly expensive. You could buy a regular flagship phone and a tablet for less. But if you're in the market for a folding phone, the Pixel is the better value — better software, better camera, slightly lower price.

The Verdict

If I had to choose one, I'd pick the Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold. It's not perfect — the battery life worries me, and the hinge feels less durable. But the software experience is so much better that it outweighs those flaws. Samsung's Z Fold 6 is a better-built device, but it's harder to use. And in daily life, usability matters more than durability spec sheets.

That said, I'm still not sure folding phones are ready for everyone. The crease, the weight, the price — these are real compromises. If you're the kind of person who upgrades every two years and wants the newest thing, go for it. But if you just want a phone that works, save your money and buy a regular Pixel or Galaxy. You won't miss the fold.
