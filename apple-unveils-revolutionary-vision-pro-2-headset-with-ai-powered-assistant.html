Apple Unveils Revolutionary 'Vision Pro 2' Headset with AI-Powered Assistant

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 23,385 views

Apple today officially unveiled the second generation of its mixed reality headset, the 'Apple Vision Pro 2,' at its Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC) keynote in Cupertino, California. The device, priced at $3,299, marks a significant leap forward in spatial computing and AI integration.

Hardware Upgrades

The Vision Pro 2 is 30% lighter than its predecessor, thanks to a new magnesium alloy frame and a more efficient battery pack that now lasts up to 4 hours on a single charge. The display resolution has been bumped to 4K per eye, and the device now includes a dedicated 'R1' co-processor that handles spatial mapping and hand tracking with near-zero latency. Apple also introduced a new 'EyeSight 2.0' feature that projects a more realistic digital replica of the user's eyes onto the external display, improving social interactions.

— Advertisement —

The Star of the Show: Siri Pro

The most talked-about feature is 'Siri Pro,' an AI-powered digital assistant that uses the headset's array of cameras and sensors to see and understand the user's environment. Siri Pro can identify objects, read text from books or signs, and even provide context-aware suggestions. For example, during a cooking session, Siri Pro can overlay recipe steps onto the user's kitchen counter, identify ingredients, and time cooking stages. Apple CEO Tim Cook demonstrated the feature live, asking Siri Pro to 'organize my digital workspace,' which instantly arranged multiple virtual windows around the user's physical desk.

Developer Ecosystem and Launch

Apple also announced a new 'Spatial Kit' for developers, allowing them to create apps that seamlessly blend digital and physical worlds. Pre-orders begin June 11, with the device shipping on June 25 in the US, UK, and Japan. Analysts predict this could be a turning point for the mixed reality industry, as the Vision Pro 2 offers a more practical and integrated experience than its predecessor, which sold an estimated 500,000 units in its first year.
