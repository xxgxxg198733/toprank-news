World's First Lab-Grown Coffee Hits Cafés: A Brewing Revolution

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 159,718 views

On June 1, 2026, Helsinki's popular café Kaffa Roastery began serving brews made from lab-grown coffee cells, developed by VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland. This cellular agriculture breakthrough promises to slash deforestation and water use linked to traditional coffee farming.

How It's Made: From Cells to Cup

VTT's process cultivates coffee plant cells in bioreactors using a nutrient broth of sugars and minerals. The cells mature in 6 weeks—compared to 3-5 years for trees—and are roasted like conventional beans. Each cup costs €3.50, slightly more than average, but VTT aims to halve costs by 2027.

— Advertisement —

Environmental and Ethical Impact

Traditional coffee production drives 10% of tropical deforestation, but lab-grown coffee uses 90% less water and zero pesticides. It also eliminates child labor issues in major coffee regions. Environmental groups like WWF have praised the innovation, though some farmers fear economic disruption.

Taste Test: Does It Measure Up?

Early reviews from customers are positive, noting a smooth, rich flavor with subtle chocolate notes. Barista Anna Kivinen says, 'It's surprisingly complex, almost like a premium Arabica.' However, purists argue it lacks the terroir of single-origin beans. VTT plans to expand to Tokyo and San Francisco by fall.
