Santorini Isn't Worth It Anymore (Go to This Greek Island Instead)

TR
By Hannah Powell · July 21, 2026 · 4 min read

I have a confession: I used to dream about Santorini. The white buildings, the blue domes, the sunsets over the caldera—it's been on my bucket list for years. So when I finally booked a trip to Greece this June, I was thrilled. I spent three weeks hopping between islands: Mykonos, Naxos, Crete, and of course, Santorini. And you know what? Santorini was the biggest disappointment of the trip. It's overcrowded, overpriced, and honestly, it's lost its magic. But I found something better. An island that's cheaper, quieter, and in my opinion, even more beautiful. It's called Milos.

Let me explain why Santorini let me down, and why you should skip it for Milos on your next trip.

What's Wrong With Santorini?

I arrived in Santorini on a Tuesday afternoon in late June. The ferry was packed with tourists. The port was chaos—hundreds of people trying to catch buses, taxis, or shuttles. I finally got to my hotel in Fira, and the first thing I noticed was the noise. Construction everywhere. Crowds on every street. The famous sunset view from Oia? There were so many people that you couldn't even get close to the edge. I paid €80 for a sunset dinner at a restaurant with a view, and the food was mediocre. The service was rushed. I felt like I was in a theme park, not a Greek island.

And the prices? A simple dinner of grilled fish and a glass of wine cost €50. A bottle of water at a kiosk was €3. A taxi from Fira to Oia (a 15-minute drive) was €40. Santorini has become a playground for the rich, and everyone else is just squeezing through the cracks. I talked to locals, and they told me that the island's infrastructure can't handle the 2 million tourists who visit each year. The water shortage is real. The garbage piles up. It's not sustainable.

I'm not saying Santorini is ugly—it's still stunning in photos. But the experience of being there is ruined by the crowds. If you go, go in the off-season (October or April), and expect to pay a premium. But if you want the real Greece, the one with quiet beaches and affordable tavernas, go to Milos.

Why Milos Is the Better Choice

Milos is about a 2-hour ferry ride from Santorini, and it feels like a different world. The island has 70 beaches, each with unique rock formations and crystal-clear water. The main town, Plaka, is a maze of narrow streets with whitewashed houses and bougainvillea. There's no cruise ship port, so the crowds are manageable. The locals are friendly. And the prices? I had a three-course dinner with fresh octopus, grilled vegetables, and local wine for €25. Yes, €25.

The beaches are the star. Sarakiniko Beach looks like a lunar landscape, with white volcanic rock formations that plunge into turquoise water. Firiplaka Beach has colorful cliffs and warm shallows. Paleochori Beach has a taverna right on the sand where you can eat fresh fish with your feet in the water. And here's the best part: even in late June, I had entire stretches of beach to myself. I'd walk 10 minutes from the parking lot and find a secluded cove with no one else. That's something you'll never get in Santorini.

Milos also has a fascinating history. The Venus de Milo was discovered here, and you can visit the catacombs and the ancient theater. The island's mining history has left behind colorful cliffs and caves that you can explore by boat. I took a day trip around the island on a small boat, and we stopped at sea caves, hot springs, and a deserted beach where we snorkeled with fish. It was the best day of my entire trip.

How to Do Milos Right

First, rent a car or a quad bike. Public transport is limited, and you'll want the freedom to explore the beaches. Second, stay in Plaka or Pollonia. Plaka has the best restaurants and views, while Pollonia is quieter and has a lovely beach. Third, book a boat tour for at least one day—it's the only way to see the sea caves and the hidden beaches. Fourth, eat at local tavernas in the villages, not the tourist traps near the port. I had the best moussaka of my life at a tiny place called 'O Hamos' in Plaka. It cost €12.

One more tip: don't try to do everything. Milos is about slowing down. Spend a morning at a beach, have a long lunch, take a nap, then watch the sunset from the castle in Plaka. That's the Greek experience you're looking for.

The Verdict

I'm not saying Santorini is terrible. If you've never been, you might still want to see it for yourself. But if you're looking for beauty, peace, and value, Milos is the better island. It's what Santorini was 20 years ago: authentic, affordable, and breathtaking. I'm already planning my return trip. Skip the crowds, skip the overpriced cocktails, and go to Milos. You'll thank me.
