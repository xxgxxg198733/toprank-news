Elon Musk's xAI Unveils Grok-4: The 'Godfather of All AI' with 10 Trillion Parameters

TR
By TopRank Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

Elon Musk's artificial intelligence company, xAI, made waves on June 5, 2026, with the launch of Grok-4, its most advanced large language model yet. In a livestreamed event, Musk declared Grok-4 as 'the godfather of all AI models,' boasting an estimated 10 trillion parameters, dwarfing OpenAI's GPT-5 (rumored at 8 trillion). The model is now available exclusively to X Premium+ subscribers at $16 per month.

Unprecedented Scale and Capabilities

Grok-4's sheer size allows it to process context windows of up to 2 million tokens, meaning it can analyze entire book trilogies in one go. Early benchmark leaks show it scoring 98% on the MMLU (Massive Multitask Language Understanding) benchmark, a new record. xAI claims Grok-4 can generate real-time 3D environments, debug complex code, and even write academic papers with human-level coherence. The model also integrates real-time access to X's data stream, giving it up-to-the-minute knowledge of global events.

Controversy and Competition

The announcement has stirred controversy. Critics question the environmental cost of training such a massive model, estimating energy consumption equivalent to a small city. Meanwhile, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman responded on X, saying 'Benchmarks aren't everything. Safety and alignment matter more.' Google DeepMind also noted that Grok-4's 'truth-seeking' mode, which Musk touts as uncensored, has shown a tendency to generate politically charged content. xAI insists the model is 'maximally curious' and includes guardrails to prevent misuse.

How to Access Grok-4

For now, Grok-4 is limited to X Premium+ users. A standalone API for developers is expected later this summer. xAI also announced a new chatbot interface called 'Grok Universe,' which allows users to create custom AI agents for specific tasks like travel planning or legal research. Musk hinted that a consumer version for Tesla vehicles is in development, promising 'the smartest car assistant ever.' The AI race has just entered a new, more intense phase.
