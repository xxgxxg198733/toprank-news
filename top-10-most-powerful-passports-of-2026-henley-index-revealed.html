Top 10 Most Powerful Passports of 2026: Henley Index Revealed

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 395,407 views

The Henley Passport Index for 2026 has been released, ranking the world's most powerful passports based on visa-free access to 227 destinations. Singapore takes the top spot for the fifth consecutive year, with its citizens able to visit 195 countries without a visa.

Top 10 Passports of 2026

Here are the top 10: 1. Singapore (195 destinations), 2. Japan (193), 3. Germany, Finland, Luxembourg, South Korea (all tied at 192), 4. Italy, Spain, Netherlands (191), 5. Denmark, Sweden, Portugal (190), 6. Austria, Ireland, Switzerland (189), 7. United States, United Kingdom (188), 8. Belgium, Canada, Greece, New Zealand (187), 9. Malta, Norway (186), 10. Australia, Czech Republic, Poland (185).

Biggest Movers

The United Arab Emirates jumped 15 places to 12th (183 destinations), thanks to new visa waiver agreements with 10 countries in the past year. China's passport climbed to 58th (88 destinations), up from 69th in 2023, as Beijing continues to open up post-pandemic. Afghanistan remains at the bottom with just 27 visa-free destinations.

What This Means for Travelers

Singapore passport holders can now travel to 195 countries without a visa, including all of Europe, the Americas, and major Asian hubs. However, they still need a visa for countries like Afghanistan, North Korea, and Syria. The index is closely watched by wealthy investors, as many countries offer 'golden visas' — residency by investment — that can lead to stronger passports.

The gap between the top and bottom continues to widen. While the average passport grants access to 110 countries, the bottom 20 passports average just 40. The Henley Index is based on data from the International Air Transport Association (IATA) and updated quarterly.
