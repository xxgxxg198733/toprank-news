'Top 10 Best-Selling Cars of May 2026' Reveals EV Dominance, Tesla Model Y Tops

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read

New data released on June 4, 2026, by Kelley Blue Book reveals the top 10 best-selling passenger vehicles in the United States for May 2026. The list shows a dramatic shift toward electric vehicles, with the Tesla Model Y claiming the top spot for the third consecutive month, followed by the Tesla Model 3 and Ford Mustang Mach-E.

The Top 10 List

  1. Tesla Model Y - 48,235 units sold
  2. Tesla Model 3 - 32,100 units
  3. Ford Mustang Mach-E - 28,500 units
  4. Toyota RAV4 - 26,400 units
  5. Honda CR-V - 24,800 units
  6. Hyundai Ioniq 5 - 22,100 units
  7. Chevrolet Silverado - 21,500 units
  8. Ford F-150 - 20,100 units
  9. Nissan Leaf - 18,900 units
  10. Kia EV6 - 17,600 units

EVs Surge Past Gas-Powered Trucks

For the first time, electric vehicles occupy six of the top 10 positions, including the top three. The Tesla Model Y's dominance is attributed to recent price cuts and the expansion of Supercharger network access to non-Tesla EVs. The Ford F-150, historically the best-selling vehicle in America, slipped to 8th place, as supply chain issues and a shift in consumer preference toward smaller, more efficient vehicles affected sales.

Legacy Automakers Respond

In response, legacy automakers like Toyota and Honda are accelerating their EV rollouts. Toyota announced on June 5 that it will launch three new electric SUVs in the US by early 2027, while Honda unveiled plans for a $500 million EV battery plant in Ohio. The data also shows that hybrid vehicles, like the Toyota RAV4 (now offered in hybrid and plug-in hybrid variants), are seeing strong demand as a transitional option for buyers not ready to go fully electric.

Industry analysts predict that by the end of 2026, EVs could account for over 40% of new vehicle sales in the US, driven by federal tax incentives and falling battery costs.
