Top 5 Hidden Beach Destinations for Summer 2026, Voted by Travel Experts

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 157,661 views

As summer 2026 heats up, travel experts at Condé Nast Traveler have released their annual list of the top five hidden beach destinations for those seeking solitude and natural beauty. The list, published on June 4, features locations that are off the beaten path and require a bit of effort to reach, but reward visitors with pristine conditions.

1. Navagio Beach, Zakynthos, Greece

While not entirely unknown, the 'Shipwreck Beach' is best visited early morning or via a kayak tour to avoid crowds. The stunning white sand and turquoise water are framed by limestone cliffs. Access is only by boat, and experts recommend booking with a small operator.

2. Pink Beach, Komodo, Indonesia

One of only seven pink sand beaches in the world, Pink Beach gets its hue from microscopic red organisms. It's located within Komodo National Park, so visitors can combine a trip with dragon sightings. The beach is best reached via a liveaboard from Labuan Bajo.

3. Secret Beach, El Nido, Philippines

This hidden lagoon is accessible only through a small crevice in a limestone cliff at low tide. Once inside, a semi-circle of white sand surrounded by emerald water awaits. It's less than 100 meters wide, offering an intimate escape.

4. Playa de las Catedrales, Ribadeo, Spain

At low tide, this beach reveals cathedral-like arches and caves carved by the Atlantic. It's a protected natural monument, and visitors must book a free permit in advance. Summer crowds are managed by timed entry, making it a serene experience.

5. Anse Source d'Argent, La Digue, Seychelles

Famous for its granite boulders, this beach is best visited by bicycle or on foot. Despite its fame, the remote location of La Digue island keeps crowds thin. The best spot is the less frequented Grand Source, accessible only by a rocky path.
