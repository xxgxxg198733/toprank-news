Global Food Shortage Looms: 'Mega-Drought' in Europe and US Threatens Wheat Supply

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 276,647 views

Farmers across the Northern Hemisphere are facing an unprecedented crisis. A 'mega-drought' that began in late spring 2026 has intensified, with parts of France, Germany, and the US Midwest experiencing the driest June on record. According to the European Drought Observatory, soil moisture levels in key agricultural regions are at historic lows. The impact on staple crops like wheat, corn, and soybeans is severe, with harvest forecasts being revised downward by up to 30%.

Breadbaskets Under Threat

The US Department of Agriculture (USDA) reported on June 5 that the winter wheat crop in Kansas and Oklahoma is 40% below the five-year average. In Europe, France's wheat harvest is expected to be the smallest since 2016, while Germany's crop is threatened by water restrictions. The situation is compounded by heatwaves, with temperatures in Spain exceeding 40°C (104°F) for the sixth consecutive day. Analysts warn that global wheat reserves could drop to a 15-year low.

Ripple Effects on Global Food Prices

The drought is already driving up prices. The UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) Food Price Index rose 4% in May, led by cereals. Bread prices in the EU have increased by 12% since April. Experts fear that if the drought continues for another month, it could trigger a crisis similar to 2007-2008, when food riots occurred in over 30 countries. The Black Sea Grain Initiative, which stabilized markets after the Russia-Ukraine war, is also under strain due to reduced exports from Ukraine.

What Can Be Done?

Governments are racing to respond. The EU has activated a crisis reserve of 500 million euros for farmers, while the US is considering emergency irrigation projects. Longer-term solutions include investing in drought-resistant genetically modified crops and better water management. For consumers, experts recommend reducing food waste and diversifying diets. The coming weeks will be critical as global leaders meet at the G20 summit in Brazil to discuss food security.
