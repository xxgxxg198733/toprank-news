10 Gadgets That Saved Me Time and Money This Month (July 2026)

TR
By Rachel Greene · July 22, 2026 · 6 min read

I have a confession: I buy way too many gadgets. My apartment is basically a graveyard of half-used smart plugs, forgotten fitness trackers, and a robot vacuum that got stuck under my couch so often I named it "Stuck." But every once in a while, something comes along that actually makes life better — not just more complicated.

Earlier this month, I found myself staring at a pile of receipts from Amazon, thinking about all the stuff I bought that I barely touched. So I decided to do something different. For the past 30 days, I've been keeping a journal of every gadget I use daily — the ones I actually reach for, not the ones I just own. These are the top 10 that earned their spot in my pocket, kitchen, and workspace. Some are new this month, some are old favorites I rediscovered, but every single one saved me either time or money — sometimes both.

1. Tile Mate (2026 Edition) — The $25 Key Finder That Saved My Saturday

Last Saturday, I spent 45 minutes looking for my keys before realizing they were in the fridge. (Don't ask.) I'd bought a Tile Mate a few months ago but never actually set it up. After that fridge incident, I synced it with my phone. Now, when I can't find my keys, wallet, or even my backpack, I just open the app and it plays a loud ring. The 2026 edition has a longer range — about 400 feet — and the battery lasts a full year. It's not sexy, but it's saved me at least two hours of searching this month alone. Time is money, and $25 for 2 hours? That's a deal.

2. Ember Smart Mug 2 — The $150 Coffee Saver I Didn't Want to Love

I'll be honest: when I first saw the Ember Smart Mug, I rolled my eyes. A mug that keeps your coffee hot? That's what microwaves are for, right? But I got one as a gift, and now I'm hooked. I work from home three days a week, and my coffee always goes cold by the time I finish a call. This mug keeps it at exactly 135°F for hours. No more nuking coffee that tastes like burnt regret. Is it overpriced? Yeah, probably. But I've stopped wasting coffee, and that alone saves me about $20 a week in Starbucks runs. Plus, the app lets you control the temp from your phone. Fancy, but practical.

3. Anker PowerCore 26800mAh Battery Pack — The $50 Lifeline

I travel a lot, and my phone battery dies like clockwork at 3 PM. This battery pack is massive — it can charge my iPhone 14 Pro five times over — and it's saved me on three trips this month alone. Once at the airport when my flight was delayed, once on a long train ride, and once during a power outage. It's not flashy, but it's reliable. Anker has been making these for years, and the 2026 version charges faster than ever. If you own a phone, you need one.

4. Kindle Paperwhite (Latest Gen) — The $130 Distraction Killer

I used to think I didn't have time to read. Turns out, I just didn't have a device that made it easy. The Kindle Paperwhite changed that. It's lightweight, waterproof (I read in the bath now, don't judge), and the battery lasts weeks. This month alone, I've finished three books: "The Ministry for the Future" by Kim Stanley Robinson, "Project Hail Mary" by Andy Weir, and "The Anxious Generation" by Jonathan Haidt. Total cost for the books: $0 from my local library via Libby. The Kindle paid for itself in saved book costs in about three months.

5. Oura Ring Gen 4 — The $350 Sleep Tracker That Actually Changed My Habits

I know, I know — wearables are everywhere. But I tried the Oura Ring after a friend convinced me, and I'm surprised by how much I like it. It tracks sleep, activity, and body temperature without a screen. No notifications buzzing on my wrist. The data is surprisingly useful: it told me that drinking coffee after 3 PM ruins my sleep quality (confirmed with my own logs). This month, I improved my sleep score by 15 points just by cutting caffeine earlier. That's worth the price for me.

6. Logitech MX Master 3S Mouse — The $100 Productivity Beast

If you spend all day on a computer, a good mouse matters more than you think. The MX Master 3S is quiet, ergonomic, and has a scroll wheel that can spin freely. I use it with my MacBook and my gaming PC. The gesture controls save me dozens of clicks per day. It's not new, but it's still the best productivity mouse you can buy. Period.

7. Ninja Creami Ice Cream Maker — The $200 Kitchen Toy That Paid for Itself

This one is a little ridiculous, but I'm including it because it saved me money on dessert. The Ninja Creami lets you make ice cream from literally anything — frozen fruit, protein shakes, even leftover cereal. I've made protein ice cream that tastes better than Halo Top for a fraction of the cost. This month, I made 12 pints at home, saving about $60 compared to store-bought. Plus, it's fun.

8. Google Pixel 7a — The $499 Phone That Does Everything Well

I upgraded from an iPhone 12 to the Pixel 7a last month, and I'm not looking back. The camera is incredible for the price — the Night Sight mode is better than my old iPhone's, and the software experience is clean. Google's AI features, like Call Screen and Magic Eraser, actually work well. It's not flashy, but it's the best value phone on the market right now. Saved me about $600 compared to a flagship Samsung or iPhone.

9. Breville Juice Fountain Cold Plus — The $200 Health Investment

I started juicing this month after a health scare (high cholesterol). The Breville is fast, easy to clean, and makes legit good juice. I've been making carrot, ginger, and orange juice daily. A bag of carrots costs $2 and makes 4 glasses. Compare that to $8 for a small bottle at a juice bar. Saved about $40 this month alone.

10. Samsung T7 Shield 2TB External SSD — The $180 Storage Savior

I lost a hard drive in college and swore I'd never lose data again. The T7 Shield is rugged, fast (USB-C, 1050MB/s), and small enough to fit in my pocket. I back up my laptop automatically every night. This month, my laptop crashed during a Zoom call, and I had a backup ready in 10 minutes. That saved me at least a day of work. Worth every penny.

Look, not every gadget is going to change your life. But these ten? They actually did something for me — saved time, saved money, or made my daily life a little less annoying. If you're thinking about buying a new toy, start here. Your wallet and your sanity will thank you.
