Nvidia Overtakes Apple as World's Most Valuable Company Amid AI Chip Frenzy

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 292,232 views

In a seismic shift in the tech landscape, Nvidia has officially overtaken Apple to become the world's most valuable publicly traded company. At market close on June 3, 2026, Nvidia's market capitalization surpassed $3.6 trillion, edging past Apple's $3.5 trillion valuation. The milestone caps a meteoric rise for the chipmaker, which has become the undisputed king of artificial intelligence hardware.

The AI Chip Gold Rush

Nvidia's surge is fueled by explosive demand for its H200 and upcoming B200 'Blackwell' GPUs, which are essential for training large language models and powering generative AI applications. Major cloud providers like Microsoft, Amazon, and Google have placed massive orders, with Nvidia's data center revenue doubling year-over-year to $47 billion in its most recent quarter. CEO Jensen Huang described the company as 'the engine of the industrial revolution of AI,' a sentiment echoed by analysts who see no end to the growth trajectory.

— Advertisement —

Apple's Stumble

Apple, meanwhile, has faced headwinds including slowing iPhone sales, particularly in China, and a lack of a clear AI strategy. While Apple recently unveiled its 'Apple Intelligence' initiative at WWDC 2025, the rollout has been slower than competitors, and its latest earnings report showed a 4% drop in revenue. Investors have rotated out of consumer hardware and into AI infrastructure, with Nvidia as the primary beneficiary.

Market Implications

The changing of the guard signals a broader market shift. Tech giants are now evaluated not by their device ecosystems but by their AI capabilities. Nvidia's rise has also lifted other semiconductor stocks, including AMD and TSMC. However, some analysts caution that Nvidia's valuation—trading at over 50 times earnings—leaves little room for error. 'Nvidia is pricing in perfection,' said Goldman Sachs analyst Toshiya Hari. 'Any hiccup in AI demand could trigger a sharp correction.' Still, for now, Nvidia stands alone at the summit, a symbol of the AI era's transformative power.
