Top 10 Most Visited Countries in 2026: France Retains Crown, Japan Surges into Top 5

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 426,896 views

The United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO) released its annual 'World Tourism Barometer' on Thursday, ranking the most visited countries in 2026. The list reflects the continued global travel rebound, with international tourist arrivals reaching 98% of pre-pandemic levels. France retains its top spot, but Asian destinations see major gains.

The Top 10 List Revealed

1. France — 100.2 million visitors. Paris, the French Riviera, and the Alps continue to attract global travelers. The 2024 Olympics legacy and new direct flights from the US contributed to the growth. 2. Spain — 88.5 million. Sun, culture, and affordable prices keep Spain strong. 3. United States — 79.4 million. New York, Los Angeles, and national parks are top draws. 4. Japan — 45.3 million. A surge from 25 million in 2025, driven by the weak yen and eased visa rules for Southeast Asian countries. 5. Italy — 44.1 million. Rome, Venice, and Florence remain perennial favorites.

Climbers and Surprises

6. Turkey — 40.2 million. Istanbul and Cappadocia are booming. 7. Mexico — 39.8 million. Cancun and Riviera Maya are top beach destinations. 8. Thailand — 35.5 million. Rebounding strongly after China reopened. 9. Germany — 34.9 million. Berlin and the Christmas markets attract year-round visitors. 10. United Kingdom — 33.1 million. London remains a cultural powerhouse, but post-Brexit visa hassles slightly dampened growth. Notably, China, once in the top 5, fell to 12th as domestic tourism remains prioritized and outbound travel has not fully recovered.

Travel Trends Driving the Rankings

The report highlights several key trends. 'Revenge travel' is still fueling demand, especially from Asian markets. The weak yen made Japan a bargain for Western tourists. 'Digital nomad visas' in Spain, Portugal, and Thailand are boosting long-stay tourism. Sustainability also plays a role, with eco-conscious travelers choosing destinations with better green credentials, such as Costa Rica (ranked 14th) and Slovenia (ranked 22nd).

For the first time, the UNWTO also ranked 'least visited countries,' with Tuvalu, Nauru, and Kiribati at the bottom, seeing fewer than 5,000 annual visitors each. These rankings continue to shape global marketing campaigns for tourism boards worldwide.
