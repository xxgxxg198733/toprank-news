World's Most Expensive Burger Now Served at Dubai's Burj Al Arab: $5,000 Gold-Crusted Creation

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 150,486 views

Dubai's Burj Al Arab hotel, long known for its opulence, has unveiled what is now the world's most expensive burger: the 'Royal 24K', priced at $5,000. Launched on June 1, 2026, this masterpiece of culinary excess is made with Japanese Wagyu beef, foie gras, black truffles, beluga caviar, and a bun infused with 24-karat gold leaf. It's served on a custom-made plate with a side of lobster bisque.

What's Inside the $5,000 Burger?

The patty is a blend of A5 Wagyu from Kobe, Japan, and dry-aged USDA Prime beef. It's topped with seared foie gras, shaved Alba white truffles, and Iranian Beluga caviar. The bun is egg-washed with gold dust and baked with saffron. The chef, Jean-Georges Vongerichten, says the burger requires 48 hours of preparation. 'It's an experience, not just a meal,' he told Food & Wine. It also comes with a glass of Dom Pérignon Champagne.

— Advertisement —

Is It Worth the Hype? Our Taste Test

Our reviewer was skeptical but admitted the flavors were surprisingly balanced. 'The gold leaf is basically tasteless, but the truffles and foie gras create a rich, umami bomb,' she wrote. The caviar adds a salty pop, and the Wagyu is incredibly tender. However, for the price, you could feed a family for a month. The burger is limited to five servings per day, and reservations are required. It's clearly a status symbol for the ultra-rich.

The Trend of Ultra-Luxury Fast Food

This isn't the first over-the-top burger. In 2023, a New York restaurant sold a $666 'Douche Burger' (caviar, gold). But the Royal 24K sets a new record. Chefs say the trend reflects a growing demand for 'experiential dining' among high-net-worth individuals. For those of us with normal budgets, there are plenty of excellent burgers for under $20. But if you win the lottery, you know where to go.
