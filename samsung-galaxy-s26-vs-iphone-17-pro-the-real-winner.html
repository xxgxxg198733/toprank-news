Samsung Galaxy S26 vs. iPhone 17 Pro: The Real Winner

TR
By Daniel Wilson · July 11, 2026 · 3 min read

It's that time again. Samsung and Apple dropped their flagship phones within days of each other, and the internet is losing its mind. I've been using both the Galaxy S26 and the iPhone 17 Pro since launch day—July 1, 2026. Here's the honest truth: both are incredible phones, but one is clearly better for most people.

Design and Build: Samsung's Edge

The Galaxy S26 is a looker. It's thinner than ever, with a titanium frame that feels premium. The screen is a 6.8-inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X with a 144Hz refresh rate. Scrolling is buttery. The iPhone 17 Pro, meanwhile, sticks with the flat-edge design from the 16 Pro. It's solid, but it feels bulkier. The notch is gone—replaced by a pill-shaped cutout—but the bezels are slightly thicker than Samsung's. In hand, the S26 wins. It's lighter and more comfortable to hold for long periods.

Performance: A Close Race

Both phones use 3nm chips. Samsung's Exynos 2600 (finally, no more Snapdragon in the US) is fast, but Apple's A19 Bionic still edges it out in raw benchmarks. In real-world use, though? I couldn't tell a difference. Apps open instantly, games run at max settings, and multitasking is smooth on both. The iPhone has a slight advantage in sustained performance—it doesn't throttle as much under heavy load. But for 99% of users, you won't notice.

Camera: Samsung's Surprise

Apple's cameras have been the gold standard for years. But Samsung finally caught up. The S26 has a 200MP main sensor and a 50MP periscope zoom lens. The photos are sharp, with natural colors. The iPhone 17 Pro's 48MP main sensor is no slouch, but Samsung's low-light performance is better. I took photos at a dimly lit concert, and the S26 captured detail the iPhone missed. The iPhone still wins in video—Cinematic Mode is smoother—but for stills, Samsung takes the crown.

Battery Life: Apple's Quiet Victory

Here's where it gets interesting. Samsung has a 5,500mAh battery, and Apple has 4,500mAh. But the iPhone 17 Pro lasts longer. I got 14 hours of screen-on time with the iPhone versus 12 on the Samsung. Apple's efficiency is insane. Both charge fast—45W on Samsung, 35W on Apple—but the iPhone gets to 50% in 20 minutes, while Samsung takes 25. It's not a huge gap, but it's noticeable.

Software: One UI vs. iOS

I prefer Android for customization, but iOS 20 is polished. Samsung's One UI 7 is cleaner than ever, with fewer bloatware apps. The iPhone's ecosystem is still tighter—AirDrop with a Mac is seamless—but Samsung's DeX mode lets you connect to a monitor and use it like a desktop. That's a killer feature for power users. If you're deep in Apple's ecosystem, stick with the iPhone. If you're not, the S26 is more flexible.

Price and Verdict

The Galaxy S26 starts at $1,199, and the iPhone 17 Pro starts at $1,299. Both are expensive. But Samsung often has trade-in deals that drop the price to $799. Apple doesn't. So here's my pick: if you want the best camera and a lighter phone, get the S26. If you want the best battery and seamless ecosystem, get the iPhone. For me? I'm keeping the S26. The camera won me over.
