Tesla’s FSD v13 in China Nears Approval; Global EV Sales Surge 22% in May

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 291,353 views

In a major breakthrough for autonomous driving, Tesla’s Full Self-Driving (FSD) v13 software has received provisional approval from Chinese authorities, according to multiple industry sources close to the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology. The decision, expected to be formally announced next week, would allow Tesla to roll out the advanced driver-assistance system to its 1.7 million vehicles in China, the company’s largest market outside the U.S.

Global EV Sales Momentum

The FSD news comes as global electric vehicle sales surged 22% in May, reaching 1.2 million units, data from Rho Motion shows. China led the charge with 680,000 EVs sold, a 30% jump from last year, driven by price cuts and new models from BYD and Nio. Europe saw a 12% increase to 280,000 units, while the U.S. market grew 8% to 120,000, partly due to the launch of the Cybertruck.

Regulatory Hurdles and Data Security

China’s approval of FSD v13 was contingent on Tesla addressing data security concerns. The company has agreed to store all Chinese user data on local servers and submitted to third-party audits. “This is a landmark moment for autonomous driving in China,” said Dr. Li Wei, an analyst at Beijing-based AutoForesight. “It sets a precedent for other automakers and signals Beijing’s willingness to embrace AI-driven technologies.”

Market Reaction and Outlook

Tesla shares jumped 3.5% in pre-market trading on the news. Competitors like Xpeng and Huawei-backed Seres are accelerating their own FSD-like systems to stay competitive. With global EV penetration now at 18%, analysts predict 2026 could be the inflection year when EVs outsell combustion engine cars in China.
