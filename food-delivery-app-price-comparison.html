We Spent a Month Ordering the Same Meal From DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub — The Price Gap Is Staggering

TR
TopRank Editorial TeamMay 26, 2026⏱ 4 min read256,100 views

Consumer Reports conducted a controlled experiment during April 2026: for 30 consecutive days, researchers ordered the same meal — a large pepperoni pizza and a Caesar salad from a specific Pizza Hut location in Dallas, Texas, exactly 2.3 miles from the Consumer Reports testing office — on DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub. All orders were placed at 12:15 PM Central Time to control for dynamic pricing variation. The results quantify what many consumers have long suspected: the platform you choose significantly affects what you pay for exactly the same food.

The base price of the meal at the restaurant — ordering directly from Pizza Hut for pickup — was $18.47 (including tax). This is the baseline against which all delivery platform pricing is compared.

DoorDash (30 orders): Average total per order: $30.12. The average delivery fee was $4.21 (DoorDash's fees vary based on distance and demand). The average service fee was $3.14 (calculated as a percentage of the order subtotal). Average Dasher tip: $4.30. Compared to the pickup baseline, DoorDash orders cost an average of 63% more.

Uber Eats (30 orders): Average total per order: $31.87. Delivery fee averaged $3.82. Service fee averaged $3.96 (Uber Eats' service fee percentage was consistently the highest of the three platforms). Average courier tip: $4.62 (Uber Eats' default tip suggestions were $1-2 higher than competitors'). Cost premium over pickup baseline: 72.5%.

Grubhub (30 orders): Average total per order: $28.96. Delivery fee averaged $3.10 (with Grubhub+ subscription, which costs $9.99/month and eliminates delivery fees on orders over $12 at participating restaurants). Service fee averaged $2.45. Average driver tip: $3.94. Cost premium over pickup baseline: 57%.

Subscription Savings: For consumers who order delivery more than twice per month, subscription programs meaningfully reduce costs. DoorDash DashPass ($9.99/month) saved our testers an average of $5.10 per order on delivery and service fees. Uber One ($9.99/month) saved an average of $4.20 per order. Grubhub+ ($9.99/month) saved an average of $4.80 per order. At three orders per month (the US average, according to market research firm Mintel), the subscriptions pay for themselves on all three platforms.

The Surprising Finding: While Grubhub was cheapest on average across 30 orders, individual daily price variation was enormous. On six of the 30 days, DoorDash was the cheapest option for that specific order. On four days, Uber Eats was cheapest. Platform pricing algorithms fluctuate based on demand, courier availability, weather, and a range of factors that the companies consider proprietary. Consumer Reports' recommendation: "Use all three apps. Check the price on each before you order. The platform that was cheapest yesterday is not guaranteed to be cheapest today. The five minutes you spend comparing could save you $5-8 per order — which, at the average American's delivery frequency of three orders per month, adds up to $180-288 per year."
