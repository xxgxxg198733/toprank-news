Apple vs. Samsung: Galaxy Z Fold 6 vs. iPhone Fold Pro – Which is Better in 2026?

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

The foldable smartphone war is heating up. On June 3, 2026, Apple officially launched its first foldable phone, the iPhone Fold Pro, directly challenging Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold 6, which debuted in May. Both devices promise to redefine the smartphone experience, but which one comes out on top? We break down the key differences.

Design and Display

The Galaxy Z Fold 6 features a 7.6-inch inner AMOLED display with a 120Hz refresh rate and a 6.2-inch cover screen. It weighs 253 grams and is 6.1mm thick when unfolded. The iPhone Fold Pro, however, offers a slightly larger 7.9-inch inner OLED display (also 120Hz) and a 6.5-inch outer screen, weighing 268 grams. Apple's hinge design uses a new 'liquid metal' alloy that reduces the crease visibility by 90%, a major advantage over the Z Fold 6's still-visible crease. Both are IP48 rated for water resistance.

Performance and Cameras

Under the hood, the Z Fold 6 uses Samsung's Exynos 2600 chip (or Snapdragon 8 Gen 5 in some markets), while the iPhone Fold Pro packs Apple's A19 Bionic chip. Early benchmarks show the A19 is about 15% faster in multi-core tasks, but the Exynos 2600 offers better GPU performance for gaming. On the camera front, the Z Fold 6 has a 50MP main sensor, a 12MP ultra-wide, and a 10MP telephoto (3x optical zoom). The iPhone Fold Pro counters with a 48MP main sensor (with a larger sensor), a 12MP ultra-wide, and a 12MP periscope telephoto (5x optical zoom), along with Apple's advanced computational photography features.

Price and Verdict

The Galaxy Z Fold 6 starts at $1,899, while the iPhone Fold Pro is significantly more expensive at $2,299. Samsung also offers a generous trade-in program, while Apple is promoting a 'flexible' payment plan. For most users, the Galaxy Z Fold 6 offers the best value with its proven foldable technology and slightly lighter weight. However, for those who want the best cameras, the smoothest crease-less display, and deep integration with the Apple ecosystem, the iPhone Fold Pro is the clear winner. The battle is tight, but the iPhone Fold Pro edges ahead for its superior camera and innovative hinge design.
