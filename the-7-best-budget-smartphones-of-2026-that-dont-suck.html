The 7 Best Budget Smartphones of 2026 That Don't Suck

TR
By TopRank Team · June 19, 2026 · 4 min read

I've been reviewing smartphones for over a decade. I've used flagships that cost $1,500 and budget phones that cost $150. The gap between them has never been smaller. In 2026, you can get a phone for under $500 that takes good photos, runs smoothly, and has a decent battery. I tested seven of the most popular budget phones to find the best ones. Here's what I found.

1. Google Pixel 8a: The King of Budget Phones

The Pixel 8a is the best budget phone you can buy. Period. It has the same Tensor G3 chip as the Pixel 8, which means it's fast and handles AI features like Magic Eraser and real-time translation. The camera is incredible for the price. The main sensor takes photos that rival the iPhone 15. The screen is a 6.1-inch OLED with 120Hz refresh rate. It's smooth and bright. The battery lasts a full day, and it charges quickly. The only downside is the design. It looks generic. But for $449, it's a steal.

2. Samsung Galaxy A56: The All-Rounder

Samsung's A-series has been solid for years. The Galaxy A56 is the latest, and it's a great all-around phone. It has a 6.6-inch Super AMOLED display, a 50MP main camera, and a 5,000mAh battery. It performs well in everyday tasks, but it's not as fast as the Pixel. The camera is good, but the processing is a bit aggressive. Skin tones can look artificial. The software experience is Samsung's One UI, which is packed with features but can be bloated. It costs $449, same as the Pixel. I'd still choose the Pixel.

3. OnePlus Nord N30 5G: The Speed Demon

OnePlus is known for fast charging, and the Nord N30 delivers. It charges to 50% in 15 minutes with the included 67W charger. The phone itself is decent. It has a 6.7-inch LCD screen, a 108MP main camera, and a Snapdragon 695 chip. The screen is not as good as OLED, but it's acceptable. The camera takes decent photos in good light but struggles in low light. The software is clean and fast. It's $379, which makes it a good value. But the display is the weak point.

4. Motorola Moto G Stylus 5G (2026): The Creativity Option

This phone has a built-in stylus, which is rare in the budget space. The stylus is great for note-taking and drawing. The phone itself is average. It has a 6.8-inch LCD screen, a 50MP camera, and a MediaTek Dimensity 7020 chip. Performance is fine for browsing and social media, but it lags with games. The camera is mediocre. The battery lasts two days, which is impressive. It's $399. I'd recommend it only if you really want the stylus.

5. Xiaomi Redmi Note 13 Pro+: The Value King

Xiaomi's Redmi Note series is legendary for value. The Note 13 Pro+ has a 6.67-inch OLED screen, a 200MP main camera, and a MediaTek Dimensity 7200 chip. It's fast, the screen is gorgeous, and the camera is surprisingly good for the price. The software is MIUI, which is not my favorite. It has ads in the system apps, which is annoying. The phone also lacks water resistance. It costs $429. If you can tolerate MIUI, it's a great phone.

6. Nokia G60 5G: The Reliable Option

Nokia is not the force it once was, but the G60 is a solid phone. It has a 6.6-inch LCD screen, a 50MP camera, and a Snapdragon 695 chip. It's nothing special, but it's reliable. The software is clean Android, with three years of updates. The camera is decent. The battery lasts a day and a half. It costs $349. It's a safe choice, but not an exciting one.

7. TCL 40 XL: The Budget King

The TCL 40 XL costs just $199. It's not great. The screen is a 6.75-inch LCD with a low resolution. The MediaTek Helio G37 chip is slow. The camera takes blurry photos. The battery lasts a day, but the phone feels cheap. It's fine for a backup or a kid's phone, but I wouldn't use it as my daily driver. Spend the extra money on something better.

The Verdict: Don't Overthink It

The Google Pixel 8a is the clear winner. It's $449, and it delivers a flagship experience in most areas. If you want a bigger screen, get the Samsung Galaxy A56. If you're on a tight budget, the OnePlus Nord N30 is a good compromise. Avoid the TCL unless you absolutely have to. The budget phone market has never been better. You don't need to spend $1,000 to get a great phone. Trust me.
