The Umami Burger From Bar Iris in Chicago Changed My Mind About Veggie Burgers

TR
By Robert Martinez · July 22, 2026 · 3 min read

I have a confession: I've always been a little snobby about veggie burgers. To me, they were the sad, dry patties you order at a barbecue because you're trying to be healthy, not because you actually want to eat them. The texture is usually mushy, the flavor is one-note, and they fall apart the second you pick them up. So when my friend dragged me to Bar Iris in Chicago's West Loop to try their "Umami Burger," I was skeptical. But I'm glad I went, because this thing is a revelation.

Bar Iris isn't a vegan restaurant. It's a cozy, dimly lit spot that serves craft cocktails and small plates. The Umami Burger is their signature dish, and it's been getting buzz on Reddit and Yelp. The secret? It's a mushroom-based patty that's been smoked, grilled, and topped with a miso-truffle aioli. I went in expecting a glorified portobello mushroom. What I got was something else entirely.

The Patty: Smoked and Grilled to Perfection

The base of the burger is a mix of shiitake, cremini, and oyster mushrooms that are finely chopped and formed into a patty. But here's where it gets interesting: they smoke the patty over hickory wood for two hours before grilling it. The result is a deep, almost meaty flavor that hits you right away. The texture is firm but tender — it doesn't fall apart, and it has a slight chew that mimics a medium-rare beef burger. I asked the chef, David Park, how they do it. He told me they use a combination of mushrooms, black beans, and quinoa for structure, plus a touch of beet juice for color. The smoking process is key. Without it, he said, it would just be a veggie burger. With it, it's umami bomb.

The Toppings: Simple but Perfect

The patty is topped with aged cheddar, pickled red onions, arugula, and a miso-truffle aioli. The aioli is the star — it's creamy, slightly salty from the miso, and earthy from the truffle. It ties everything together. The pickled onions add acidity to cut through the richness, and the arugula adds a peppery bite. The bun is a brioche from a local bakery, Pletz's, that's toasted and buttery. It's not too thick, so you can taste the patty. Every bite is balanced.

The Sides: Don't Skip the Fries

I ordered the burger with a side of their truffle fries. They're double-fried, crispy, and tossed in parmesan and truffle oil. They're $8 extra, which is steep, but worth it. The portion is generous — enough for two people. I also tried their roasted cauliflower with tahini and pomegranate, which was good but not life-changing. The burger is the star.

The Price: $19 for a Burger That Feels Like a Meal

At $19, the Umami Burger is not cheap. But compared to other Chicago burgers — like the $22 Au Cheval burger or the $20 Loyalist burger — it's on par. And honestly, the quality justifies the price. It's not a fast-food burger; it's a carefully crafted dish that takes hours to prepare. I felt like I was getting my money's worth.

Why It Changed My Mind

I've always thought that a good burger needs meat. But this one proved me wrong. The Umami Burger doesn't try to be a beef burger — it's its own thing, with a flavor profile that's rich, smoky, and deeply savory. It's not trying to fool you into thinking it's meat; it's celebrating what mushrooms can do. After eating it, I realized that veggie burgers don't have to be a compromise. They can be a destination dish. I've already planned a return trip to try their other menu items, like the smoked mushroom tacos and the truffle mac and cheese.

If you're in Chicago, do yourself a favor and go to Bar Iris. Sit at the bar, order the Umami Burger and a cocktail (their smoked old fashioned is fantastic), and enjoy. You might just change your mind about veggie burgers too.
