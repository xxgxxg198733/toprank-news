Apple Vision Pro vs Meta Quest 4: The Ultimate VR/AR Battle of 2026

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 206,672 views

The mixed reality headset war is heating up. With Apple launching the second-generation Vision Pro on June 2, 2026, and Meta unveiling the Quest 4 earlier this week, consumers face a tough choice. Both devices promise to blend digital content with the real world, but they take radically different approaches to price, design, and usability. Here's a head-to-head comparison to help you decide.

Price and Accessibility

The biggest differentiator remains cost. Apple's Vision Pro 2 starts at $3,499, a slight reduction from the original but still firmly in luxury territory. In contrast, the Meta Quest 4 starts at $499 for the base model, making it accessible to a much wider audience. Meta's strategy focuses on mass adoption, while Apple targets professionals and early adopters willing to pay a premium for seamless integration with the Apple ecosystem.

— Advertisement —

Performance and Features

Under the hood, both headsets pack serious power. Apple uses a new M4 Ultra chip with a dedicated R2 coprocessor, delivering 8K per-eye resolution and advanced eye-tracking. Meta's Quest 4 employs a custom Qualcomm Snapdragon XR3 Gen 2 chip, offering 4K per-eye resolution but with superior field-of-view (120 degrees vs Apple's 110). Both support full-color passthrough for AR. Early reviews praise Apple's crisp passthrough and intuitive gesture controls, while Meta wins on hand tracking latency and a larger app library.

Ecosystem and Content

Apple leverages its existing ecosystem, offering seamless integration with Mac, iPhone, and iPad. You can run thousands of iPad apps in mixed reality. Meta, on the other hand, boasts the largest VR game library, including exclusive titles like 'Horizon Worlds 2.0' and 'Beat Saber: Next Gen.' For productivity, Apple's 'Spatial Desktop' is a game-changer, while Meta's 'Workrooms' is more collaborative. Ultimately, your choice depends on whether you prioritize gaming (Meta) or productivity and refinement (Apple).
