Inside Out 2 Is the Best Pixar Movie in Years

TR
By Christopher Lee · June 25, 2026 · 1 min read

I’ll admit it—I was worried. Pixar has been on a rough streak lately. Lightyear bombed. Elemental was fine but forgettable. So when Inside Out 2 came out last Friday, I had low expectations. Two hours later, I was wiping tears in the theater. This movie is a return to form.

The Setup

The sequel picks up with Riley as a teenager. New emotions show up: Anxiety, Embarrassment, Envy, and Ennui. Anxiety is voiced by Maya Hawke, and she’s terrifyingly accurate. The movie tackles what it feels like when your brain starts second-guessing everything. I’ve never seen anxiety portrayed so perfectly on screen.

What Works

The animation is gorgeous. The new emotions are designed brilliantly—Envy is a tiny blue creature, Embarrassment is a giant pink blob. The humor lands for both kids and adults. There’s a running joke about sarcasm that had parents howling. But the emotional core is what got me. There’s a scene where Joy realizes that sadness is necessary for growth. My daughter grabbed my hand and said, “That’s like us.” I lost it.

Does It Top the Original?

Almost. The first Inside Out is a perfect movie, in my opinion. The sequel doesn’t quite reach those heights—the ending feels slightly rushed. But it’s close. It’s smarter, braver, and more honest than almost any kids’ movie in the last five years. I give it a 9/10.

Should You See It?

Yes. Take your kids. Take your parents. Go alone. It’s a conversation starter about mental health. Pixar proved they still have magic left.
