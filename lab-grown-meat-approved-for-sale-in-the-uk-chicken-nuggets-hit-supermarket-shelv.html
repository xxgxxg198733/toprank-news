Lab-Grown Meat Approved for Sale in the UK: 'Chicken' Nuggets Hit Supermarket Shelves

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 298,486 views

In a landmark decision for the food industry, the UK's Food Standards Agency (FSA) on Thursday granted regulatory approval for the sale of cultivated meat, also known as lab-grown meat. The first product to hit shelves will be chicken nuggets made by the company Good Meat, a subsidiary of Eat Just, which will be available at Tesco supermarkets starting June 15. This makes the UK the first European country to approve the sale of cell-cultivated meat.

How It's Made and What It Tastes Like

Good Meat's chicken is produced by taking cells from a living chicken, feeding them a nutrient-rich broth inside bioreactors, and harvesting the resulting meat after three weeks. The process uses no animals beyond the initial cell sample. Independent taste testers, including professional chefs, have described the product as indistinguishable from traditional chicken nuggets in texture and flavor. The FSA's approval comes after a rigorous safety review, confirming the meat is free from antibiotics and pathogens.

Environmental and Ethical Implications

Cultivated meat promises significant environmental benefits. A 2023 study from the University of Oxford found that lab-grown chicken generates up to 92% less greenhouse gas emissions and uses 95% less land than conventional chicken farming. Animal welfare groups have praised the move, as it eliminates the need for slaughterhouses. However, critics point to the high energy requirements of bioreactors and the still-unproven scalability of the technology. Good Meat says its current UK production facility can produce 1,000 kg per week, enough for about 5,000 meals.

Next Steps and Industry Impact

The FSA is also reviewing applications from other companies, including Upside Foods and Mosa Meat, for beef and pork products. Industry analysts predict that the UK market for cultivated meat could reach £500 million by 2030. The approval is expected to accelerate regulatory processes in other European countries, though France and Italy have expressed strong opposition, passing laws to ban the term 'meat' from lab-grown products. For now, UK consumers will be able to vote with their wallets, with a pack of six nuggets priced at £5.99, comparable to premium organic chicken nuggets.

Good Meat plans to expand to Sainsbury's and Waitrose by the end of the year. The future of protein is taking shape, one nugget at a time.
