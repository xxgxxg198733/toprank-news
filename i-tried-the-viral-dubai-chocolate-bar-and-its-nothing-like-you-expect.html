I Tried the Viral Dubai Chocolate Bar and It's Nothing Like You Expect

TR
By Megan O'Brien · July 20, 2026 · 3 min read

The Hype Is Real, But Not in the Way You Think

If you've been on TikTok or Instagram in the past month, you've seen it: the Dubai Chocolate Bar. It's a chunky, ridged bar filled with a bright green pistachio cream and shredded phyllo dough (called knafeh in Arabic). People are flying to Dubai just to buy it. Resellers are charging $100 a bar. I thought it was just another internet fad. But then my friend brought one back from a layover in Dubai, and we tried it together. I need to talk about this.

What Exactly Is This Thing?

The bar is made by Fix Dessert Chocolatier, a small shop in Dubai that went viral in late 2025 after a food blogger posted a video of the bar being sliced open. The green filling oozed out in slow motion, and the internet lost its mind. The chocolate itself is a thick Belgian milk chocolate shell, studded with small ridges. Inside, it's filled with a paste made from pistachio cream (like a Middle Eastern Nutella) and crispy kataifi pastry. The combination is pure genius: creamy, nutty, crunchy, and sweet but not cloying.

I was skeptical. I've been burned by viral foods before. Remember the cronut? The rainbow bagel? The pink sauce? Yeah. So I went in with low expectations. But this... this is different.

The First Bite

I'll be honest: my first bite was confusing. The chocolate is incredibly thick—like, almost half an inch thick. You need to really commit to biting into it. The shell cracks loudly. Then you hit the filling, which is dense and slightly chewy from the phyllo. The pistachio flavor is strong and nutty, not artificial. It's like a really, really high-end candy bar. But it's also messy. The filling gets everywhere. I had green crumbs on my shirt. My friend got it on her couch. It's not a neat snack.

But the taste? Unforgettable. It's rich without being heavy. The sweetness is balanced by the saltiness of the pistachio. The texture is a rollercoaster: crunch, cream, crunch. I finished half the bar in one sitting, which I immediately regretted (my stomach hurt). But I couldn't stop.

​

The Dark Side of the Hype

Here's the thing nobody talks about: the hype has completely broken the supply chain. Fix Dessert Chocolatier is a tiny shop. They weren't ready for this. Now they have hour-long lines, limits on purchases, and a black market that's charging absurd prices. I saw a bar listed for $250 on eBay. That's insane. It's a chocolate bar. It's good, but it's not $250 good.

And because of the demand, there are now dozens of knockoffs. I tried one from a shop in New York that claims to make a "Dubai-style" bar. It was okay, but it wasn't the same. The filling was too sweet, the chocolate was thin. The original is special because of the quality of the ingredients. The pistachio cream is imported from Iran. The chocolate is single-origin from Colombia. The phyllo is made fresh daily. You can't replicate that easily.

Should You Buy One?

If you can get your hands on one at retail price (around $20-30 in Dubai), absolutely. It's a unique experience. But don't pay scalper prices. The hype will die down eventually, and the shop will likely expand. In the meantime, I've been experimenting with making my own version at home. I found a recipe from a Lebanese chef on YouTube, and honestly, it's 80% as good. It takes a few hours, but it's worth it.

The Dubai Chocolate Bar is a genuine innovation in confectionery. It deserves the attention. But the internet's obsession with scarcity and exclusivity has made it into something it's not. It's a chocolate bar. A damn good one. But just a chocolate bar.

My advice? Watch the videos. Salivate. Then make a batch yourself. Your wallet and your taste buds will thank you.
