Universal's Epic Universe Opens July 2026: What to Expect

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 188,789 views

Universal Orlando Resort has announced that its highly anticipated third theme park, Epic Universe, will officially open to the public on July 1, 2026. The $6 billion park, under construction since 2021, promises to be the largest and most immersive theme park experience ever built.

The Lands

Epic Universe is divided into five themed lands: Super Nintendo World (featuring Donkey Kong, Mario Kart, and Yoshi rides), How to Train Your Dragon - Isle of Berk (with a flying coaster and live dragon show), Dark Universe (based on Universal's classic monsters like Dracula and Frankenstein), Celestial Park (a celestial-themed hub with a massive carousel and water rides), and The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Ministry of Magic (a new area set in 1920s Paris).

New Technology

The park introduces cutting-edge technology, including 'Hyper-Reality' glasses that overlay digital elements onto physical rides. The Mario Kart: Bowser's Challenge ride uses augmented reality to make players feel like they're racing through iconic video game tracks. The Dark Universe's 'Frankenstein's Lab' ride features a fully animatronic monster that interacts with guests in real time.

Tickets and Capacity

One-day tickets start at $149 for adults, with a 'Universal Express' pass available for an additional $80. Annual passes will include access to all three parks. Epic Universe is expected to draw 10 million visitors in its first year, boosting Orlando's tourism economy. Hotels on-site include the Universal Stella Nova Resort and the Helios Grand Hotel.

Early reviews from test runs have been overwhelmingly positive. Fans are especially excited about the How to Train Your Dragon flying coaster, which simulates riding a dragon over a Nordic landscape. Epic Universe is set to be the theme park event of the decade.
