Apple Watch Series 11 vs. Samsung Galaxy Watch 8: Which Smartwatch Wins in 2026?

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 427,705 views

Two of the most popular smartwatches on the market, the Apple Watch Series 11 and the Samsung Galaxy Watch 8, have just been released, and consumers are torn. Both offer advanced health tracking, seamless phone integration, and stylish designs. But which one is right for you? We put them through a series of tests to find out.

Health and Fitness Tracking

The Apple Watch Series 11 introduces a non-invasive blood glucose monitor, a game-changer for diabetics and health-conscious users. It also offers improved sleep staging and a new 'vitals' app that summarizes key metrics. The Galaxy Watch 8, meanwhile, counters with a built-in body composition analyzer (via bioelectrical impedance) and an FDA-cleared atrial fibrillation detection feature. Both watches are excellent, but Apple's glucose monitor gives it the edge for medical utility.

Battery Life and Charging

Battery life has long been Apple's Achilles' heel, and the Series 11 still only lasts 36 hours on a typical charge. The Galaxy Watch 8, however, boasts a 4-day battery life in standard mode and 10 days in low-power mode. Samsung also offers faster wireless charging—a full charge in 60 minutes versus Apple's 90. For users who hate charging daily, the Galaxy Watch is the clear winner.

Ecosystem and Compatibility

Here, the battle is clear-cut: the Apple Watch works only with iPhones, while the Galaxy Watch works with Android phones (and has limited support for iOS). If you're an iPhone user, the Apple Watch offers seamless integration, including camera remote, Apple Pay, and Find My network. For Android users, the Galaxy Watch provides a smooth experience with Samsung Health and Google Assistant. Neither watch is bad, but your choice will largely depend on your phone.

Verdict

The Apple Watch Series 11 wins for health features and iPhone integration. The Samsung Galaxy Watch 8 wins for battery life and Android compatibility. If you own an iPhone, go Apple. If you own a Samsung or other Android phone, go Galaxy. It's that simple—but both are excellent devices.
