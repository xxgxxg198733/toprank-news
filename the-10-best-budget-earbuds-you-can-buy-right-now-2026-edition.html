The 10 Best Budget Earbuds You Can Buy Right Now (2026 Edition)

TR
By Nicole Barnes · July 23, 2026 · 6 min read

Let's be real: spending $300 on earbuds feels ridiculous. I've bought expensive ones before, and yeah, they sound great, but I've also lost two pairs of AirPods Pro in coffee shops and cried a little each time. That's why I've spent the last month testing 30 different budget earbuds under $100. Not all of them are winners — some sounded like tin cans, others had batteries that died faster than my motivation on a Monday morning. But these 10? These are the ones I'd actually recommend to a friend.

Why Budget Earbuds Are Better Than Ever

The tech has gotten way better in the last two years. Companies like Soundcore, EarFun, and even Anker are using the same Bluetooth 5.3 chips and 10mm drivers that you'd find in $200 sets. The difference? They're cutting costs on the case material and skipping the fancy noise-canceling algorithms. But for most people, that's fine. I don't need my earbuds to cancel out a jet engine — I just want to hear my podcasts on the subway and not embarrass myself on Zoom calls.

I tested each pair for at least three days: commutes, gym sessions, and late-night Netflix binges. I also made sure to check the call quality, because nothing says "I'm a professional" like a colleague hearing your toilet flush through your mic.

1. Soundcore Space A40 ($79)

This is the one. Seriously, just buy these. Soundcore's been crushing the budget space for years, and the Space A40 is their best work yet. They pack 11mm drivers that deliver clear mids and tight bass — not the muddy boom you get from cheap earbuds. The noise cancellation is nearly as good as my old AirPods Pro, and they fit snug without falling out during my morning runs. Battery life is 10 hours with ANC on, and the case charges wirelessly. For $79, it's almost unfair.

One thing I don't like: the touch controls are too sensitive. I accidentally paused my music three times while adjusting them. Still, that's a small complaint.

2. EarFun Air Pro 4 ($69)

If you're on a tighter budget, these are the dark horses. EarFun isn't a household name, but they should be. The Air Pro 4 has a balanced sound profile, good ANC (not great, but solid), and a comfortable fit for small ears. I gave them to my sister, who has tiny ear canals, and she said they were the first earbuds that didn't hurt after an hour. The app is basic but functional — you can adjust the EQ and check battery levels. My only gripe: the case feels a little cheap, with a plastic hinge that might break if you drop it. But for $69, you can't be too picky.

3. Anker Life P3 ($59)

Anker's been making solid budget tech for years, and the Life P3 is no exception. They're not the best-sounding earbuds on this list, but they're the most reliable. The connection never drops, the battery lasts 9 hours, and the IPX5 water resistance means they survive sweat and light rain. They also have a transparency mode that's surprisingly natural — I could hear my own voice clearly when talking to a barista. The bass is a little too punchy for my taste (it overpowers the mids), but if you listen to EDM or hip-hop, you'll love them.

4. Beats Flex ($49)

Okay, these aren't earbuds — they're neckband headphones. But I included them because they're the best option for people who hate losing individual earbuds. The Beats Flex wrap around your neck and connect via a cable. The sound is decent (typical Beats — heavy on bass), and the battery lasts 12 hours. More importantly, they support the Apple W1 chip, so pairing with an iPhone is seamless. I used these during a 10-hour flight and never worried about one falling out. They're not for everyone, but if you're clumsy, they're a lifesaver.

5. JLab Go Air Pop ($25)

I didn't expect much from $25 earbuds, but the Go Air Pop surprised me. They sound remarkably clear for the price, with a balanced EQ that doesn't get muddy. The battery life is 8 hours, and the charging case is tiny — smaller than a Zippo lighter. The biggest downside: no ANC, so you'll hear everything around you. But for listening to audiobooks or taking calls in a quiet room, they're fantastic. I actually bought a pair for my dad, who only uses earbuds for phone calls, and he loves them.

6. Soundcore Life P2 Mini ($39)

These are the Space A40's little siblings. They're smaller, lighter, and cheaper, but they still pack 10mm drivers and IPX7 waterproofing (you can submerge them in water for 30 minutes — not that you would). The sound is warm and pleasant, though the treble can be a bit harsh at high volumes. They also have a mic that's good enough for calls, but don't expect studio quality. For $39, they're a steal for gym use.

7. Nothing Ear (a) ($99)

I was skeptical of Nothing's transparent design — it felt like a gimmick. But after trying the Ear (a), I'm converted. They sound incredible for the price: detailed, wide soundstage, and punchy bass that doesn't distort. The ANC is solid, and the app lets you customize the controls extensively. The design is genuinely cool — you can see the internal components through the case. The only issue: the fit is a bit loose for smaller ears. I had to use the smallest tips to get a seal.

8. Sony WF-C500 ($89)

Sony's budget earbuds are often overlooked, but the WF-C500 delivers the same tuning expertise as their flagship models. The sound is neutral and detailed, with excellent separation between instruments. They don't have ANC, but the passive isolation is decent if you use foam tips. The battery life is 10 hours, and the case is tiny. I use these when I want to hear my music without any artificial enhancement. They're not exciting, but they're honest.

9. OnePlus Buds 3 ($99)

OnePlus made a name for itself with smartphones, but their earbuds are surprisingly good. The Buds 3 have dual drivers (a 10.4mm woofer and 6mm tweeter) that deliver clear highs and deep bass. The ANC is good, not great, and the fit is comfortable for long sessions. The best feature: they pair instantly with OnePlus phones and support spatial audio. But they also work fine with iPhones, though you lose the spatial audio. I'd recommend these to anyone who owns a OnePlus phone.

10. JBL Tune 230NC ($79)

JBL's Tune series is known for bass-heavy sound, and the 230NC doesn't disappoint. They have a fun, energetic sound that's great for workouts. The ANC is decent, and the battery lasts 10 hours. The case is bulky — about the size of a deck of cards — but it supports wireless charging. The biggest issue: the ear tips are too stiff, and they don't create a good seal for many people. You'll need to buy third-party tips. Still, if you love bass, these are worth it.

Final Thoughts

The budget earbud market has matured so much that you no longer have to sacrifice quality to save money. The Soundcore Space A40 is my top pick because it offers 90% of the experience of premium earbuds at half the price. But if you're truly broke, the JLab Go Air Pop is a miracle at $25. Just don't expect miracles — you get what you pay for, but these days, what you get is pretty darn good.
