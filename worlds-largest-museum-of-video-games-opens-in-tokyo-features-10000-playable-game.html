World’s Largest Museum of Video Games Opens in Tokyo; Features 10,000 Playable Games

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 312,104 views

Gamers around the world have a new pilgrimage site. The National Video Game Museum (NVGM) officially opened its doors today in Tokyo’s Akihabara district, home to the world’s largest collection of playable video games. The museum spans six floors and 50,000 square feet, with 10,000 games from 1972 to the present day.

The Collection

Highlights include a rare 1972 Magnavox Odyssey (only 100,000 ever made), a complete set of Nintendo’s Famicom Disk System games, and a room dedicated to arcade cabinets from the golden age. Visitors can play everything from Space Invaders to Elden Ring. The museum also hosts the world’s first playable version of the unreleased Star Wars: Battlefront III.

Interactive Exhibits

The museum uses RFID wristbands to save progress across games. The second floor features a ‘History of Handhelds’ exhibit with over 500 devices, from the Game Boy to the Steam Deck OLED. There’s also a developer space where indie studios showcase early builds. “We wanted to create a living archive, not a static display,” said curator Yuki Tanaka.

Opening Day Reception

More than 10,000 visitors queued on opening day, with some camping overnight. Tickets are ¥3,000 for adults (about $20) and ¥1,500 for children. The museum is open 365 days a year. Analysts predict it will attract 2 million annual visitors, boosting tourism to Akihabara.
