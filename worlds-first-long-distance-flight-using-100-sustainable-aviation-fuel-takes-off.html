World’s First Long-Distance Flight Using 100% Sustainable Aviation Fuel Takes Off

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 197,497 views

Aviation history was made today when a United Airlines Boeing 787 Dreamliner completed the first long‑haul commercial flight powered entirely by 100% sustainable aviation fuel (SAF). The flight, UA880, departed London Heathrow at 8:30 am BST and landed at Newark Liberty at 11:15 am EDT, carrying 174 passengers.

The Fuel and Emissions Reduction

The SAF was produced by World Energy from used cooking oil and animal fat waste. It reduces lifecycle carbon emissions by 80% compared to traditional jet fuel. The flight saved approximately 85 metric tons of CO2 — equivalent to taking 18 cars off the road for a year. “This is a proof of concept that the technology works,” said United CEO Scott Kirby. “Now we need scale.”

Challenges Ahead

SAF currently accounts for less than 0.1% of global aviation fuel, and costs three times more than kerosene. United has invested in several SAF startups, but production capacity is limited. The flight required approval from the FAA and EASA, which certified the 787’s engines to handle the fuel’s different combustion properties.

Industry Impact

The flight comes as the airline industry faces pressure to decarbonize. The International Air Transport Association has set a goal of net‑zero emissions by 2050. Airlines including British Airways and Delta have pledged to increase SAF usage. The Biden administration’s SAF Grand Challenge aims for 3 billion gallons by 2030.
