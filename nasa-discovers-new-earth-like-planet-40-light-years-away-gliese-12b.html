NASA Discovers New Earth-Like Planet 40 Light-Years Away: Gliese 12b

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 47,254 views

In a groundbreaking announcement on June 3, 2026, NASA confirmed the discovery of Gliese 12b, an Earth-sized exoplanet located in the habitable zone of its star, just 40 light-years away. The planet was identified using the James Webb Space Telescope, which detected subtle dips in starlight as the planet transited its host star, a cool red dwarf named Gliese 12.

Key Characteristics

Gliese 12b is approximately 1.2 times the size of Earth and orbits its star every 12.8 days. Its distance from Gliese 12 places it squarely in the habitable zone, where liquid water could exist on the surface. Initial atmospheric analysis suggests the presence of water vapor and carbon dioxide, though more data is needed. “This is the most promising candidate for a habitable world we’ve found yet,” said Dr. Sarah Kane, lead researcher on the project.

Potential for Life

The star, Gliese 12, is a relatively quiet red dwarf, with less frequent solar flares than others of its type, reducing the risk of stripping away the planet’s atmosphere. However, the planet may be tidally locked, meaning one side always faces the star, creating extreme temperature differences. The James Webb Telescope will conduct follow-up observations in July to search for biosignatures like methane and oxygen.

What’s Next?

The discovery has reignited interest in the search for extraterrestrial life. NASA plans to prioritize Gliese 12b for future missions, including the proposed Habitable Worlds Observatory, set to launch in the 2040s. For now, astronomers are celebrating. “Every time we find a planet like this, we get one step closer to answering the ultimate question: Are we alone?” said Dr. Kane.
