Top 10 Most Powerful Passports in the World for 2026: Singapore Tops Henley Index

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 256,813 views

The latest Henley Passport Index for 2026 has been released, and Singapore has once again claimed the title of the world's most powerful passport. Citizens of the city-state can now enjoy visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 195 destinations worldwide, edging out Japan, which drops to second place with 193 destinations. The index, based on data from the International Air Transport Association (IATA), serves as a benchmark for global mobility and reflects geopolitical stability and international relations.

Top 10 Rankings for 2026

According to the Henley & Partners report published on June 4, 2026, the top 10 are dominated by European and Asian nations. Singapore leads, followed by Japan (2nd), and a tie for 3rd between Germany, France, and Italy (192 destinations). South Korea and Spain share 4th place (191), while Finland, Sweden, and the Netherlands round out the top 5 (190). The United States ranks 8th (186), and the United Kingdom places 7th (188).

Biggest Movers and Shakers

This year's index highlights notable gains. The United Arab Emirates (UAE) has surged into the top 10, now ranked 9th with 183 destinations, reflecting its growing diplomatic influence. Conversely, Afghanistan remains at the bottom of the index, with its citizens able to access only 26 destinations without a visa, a stark reminder of the global inequality in travel freedom.

What This Means for Travelers

For globetrotters, a powerful passport means more spontaneous trips and fewer bureaucratic hurdles. Singaporeans can now travel to remote destinations like Bhutan and many African nations without pre-arranged visas. However, experts note that the gap between the top and bottom tiers continues to widen, with the average score for top 10 countries now 190, compared to just 40 for the bottom 10. The index serves as a valuable tool for investors and expats considering second passports.
