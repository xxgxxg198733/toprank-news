We Drove a Tesla Model Y, Rivian R2, and Ford Explorer EV for a Week Each — Here's Which One to Buy

TR
TopRank Editorial TeamJune 1, 2026⏱ 5 min read289,400 views

The electric SUV segment in 2026 has three vehicles that automotive journalists and consumers consistently compare: the refreshed Tesla Model Y, the new Rivian R2, and the Ford Explorer EV. Edmunds conducted a comparative test in which three editors each spent seven consecutive days with each vehicle, logging a minimum of 400 miles per car across a standardized mix of highway, city, and suburban driving. Here are the results.

Tesla Model Y (2026 Refresh) — Starting MSRP: $44,990: The refreshed Model Y, manufactured at Tesla's Austin, Texas Gigafactory, received updated exterior styling, a redesigned interior with ventilated front seats and an 8-inch rear passenger display, and what Tesla claims is a 10% improvement in aerodynamic efficiency. Our test vehicle, a Long Range AWD model with the optional 20-inch wheels ($2,000), achieved 347 miles of real-world range on our standardized 70-mph highway loop — 11 miles short of the EPA estimate of 358 miles, a discrepancy well within normal testing variation. The Supercharger network remains the decisive advantage. On a 1,200-mile road trip from Los Angeles to Portland, the Tesla required 3 hours and 12 minutes of total charging time across six stops. No competitor matched this. The Model Y's 0-60 time of 4.2 seconds (measured on a VBOX GPS accelerometer) makes it the quickest vehicle in this comparison by a significant margin.

Rivian R2 — Starting MSRP: $47,000: The R2 is manufactured at Rivian's Normal, Illinois plant, the same facility that produces the R1T and R1S. Our test vehicle was a Dual-Motor AWD model with the Large battery pack ($5,500 option) and the Adventure Package ($3,800), bringing the as-tested price to $56,300. The R2 recorded 302 miles on our highway range test against an EPA estimate of 310 miles. The R2's standout feature is its off-road capability — on a standardized off-road course at Hungry Valley SVRA in California, the R2 completed every obstacle without difficulty, including a 22-degree ascent over loose rock that caused the Explorer EV to lose traction and the Model Y to scrape its underbody. The interior uses recycled materials extensively, including seat fabric made from recycled ocean plastics. The R2's 0-60 time is 4.5 seconds, placing it between the Tesla and the Ford.

Ford Explorer EV — Starting MSRP: $42,500: Manufactured at Ford's Cologne Electrification Centre in Germany, the Explorer EV is built on Volkswagen's MEB platform under the Ford-VW collaboration agreement announced in 2020. Our test vehicle, a Premium RWD Extended Range model ($48,200 as tested), achieved 318 miles on our highway range test against an EPA estimate of 325 miles — the smallest discrepancy in the test. The Explorer EV's interior is the quietest of the three vehicles at 70 mph, registering 64 decibels on our sound meter compared to 67 dB for the Tesla and 68 dB for the Rivian. Ford's SYNC 5 infotainment system, while improved from previous generations, remains the weakest software experience — menu navigation is less intuitive than Tesla's or Rivian's systems, and we experienced two instances of wireless Apple CarPlay disconnection during our test week. The Explorer EV's 0-60 time of 5.8 seconds makes it the slowest vehicle in this group, though still quicker than the average gasoline SUV.

Edmunds' Verdict: For drivers who prioritize technology and charging infrastructure, the Tesla Model Y is the recommendation. For drivers who need genuine off-road capability or prefer an adventure-oriented design, the Rivian R2 is the clear choice. For drivers who prioritize ride comfort, interior quietness, and build quality, the Ford Explorer EV wins. "This is the first time in the modern EV era that consumers have three genuinely excellent options in the midsize electric SUV segment," the editors concluded. "You cannot make a bad choice here. You can only make the choice that best fits your specific needs."
