AirPods Pro 3 vs Galaxy Buds Pro 3: Which Should You Buy in 2026?

TR
By Andrew Foster · July 22, 2026 · 8 min read

I have a problem. I can't stop buying earbuds. Over the past decade, I've owned probably 15 pairs — from $20 Ankers to $400 Sonys. Every year, Apple and Samsung release new flagships, and every year, I convince myself that this time, one of them will be perfect. Spoiler: neither is perfect. But in 2026, one of them comes a lot closer.

The AirPods Pro 3 launched in September 2025, and the Galaxy Buds Pro 3 followed in February 2026. I've been using both for the last two weeks — switching between them daily, testing them in every scenario I could think of: noisy coffee shops, quiet home offices, windy walks, gym sessions, phone calls, and late-night movie watching. I even had a friend call me from a loud bar to test microphone quality.

Here's the short version: if you have an iPhone, buy the AirPods Pro 3. If you have a Samsung phone, buy the Galaxy Buds Pro 3. But if you're on the fence — maybe you use a Pixel or a OnePlus — read on. Because the differences go deeper than just ecosystem lock-in.

Design and Comfort: Apple Refines, Samsung Experiments

The AirPods Pro 3 look almost identical to the AirPods Pro 2. Same stem, same white plastic, same silicone ear tips. Apple made one meaningful change: a new vent system that reduces the "suction" feeling when you push them in. It works. I can wear them for 4-5 hours without discomfort. The ear tips are slightly softer and come in four sizes now, including an XS for smaller ears. That's a big deal — the AirPods Pro 2 only had three sizes, and many people (including my partner) found them too large.

The Galaxy Buds Pro 3, by contrast, look completely different from their predecessors. Samsung ditched the glossy, pebble-like design for a matte, angular shape with a chrome ring around the driver. They're smaller than the Buds Pro 2, but they protrude more from the ear — like little antennas. Comfort-wise, they're fine for 2-3 hours, but after that, I start feeling pressure on the outer ear. The included ear tips are silicone, not foam, which means they don't seal as well for some ear shapes. I had to buy third-party foam tips to get a good seal.

Winner: AirPods Pro 3, but only slightly. The Galaxy Buds Pro 3 look more distinctive (if you like that sort of thing), but the AirPods are more comfortable for long sessions.

Sound Quality: Two Different Philosophies

This is where things get interesting. Apple and Samsung have very different ideas about what earbuds should sound like.

The AirPods Pro 3 have a balanced, neutral sound signature. Bass is present but not overpowering. Mids are clear, and highs are crisp without being harsh. It's the kind of sound that audiophiles call "accurate" — it doesn't add any color or excitement, but it also doesn't distort anything. Apple's Adaptive EQ adjusts the frequency response in real-time based on the fit of the earbuds in your ear. It works surprisingly well. I listened to Daft Punk's "Random Access Memories" (the 2026 Atmos remaster) and heard details I'd never noticed before — the subtle reverb on the vocals, the texture of the drum hits.

The Galaxy Buds Pro 3 are the opposite. They're tuned for fun. Bass is boosted significantly — sometimes too much. Songs with heavy bass (think Billie Eilish or Travis Scott) sound punchy and exciting, but acoustic tracks can sound muddy. Samsung's 360 Audio feature creates a spatial audio experience that's impressive for movies but inconsistent for music. Some songs sound like they're coming from a concert hall; others sound like the instruments are scattered around a gymnasium. It's a gimmick I turned off after a week.

Both support high-resolution audio codecs: Apple uses AAC (which is fine, but not lossless over Bluetooth), and Samsung uses its proprietary Samsung Seamless Codec (SSC) for 24-bit/96kHz audio. In practice, I couldn't hear a difference in a blind test. Bluetooth just isn't there yet for lossless.

Winner: tie. If you want accuracy, get the AirPods. If you want bass, get the Galaxy Buds.

Noise Cancellation: Apple Dominates

I tested noise cancellation on the New York City subway — specifically the Q train, which is one of the loudest in the system. The AirPods Pro 3 are phenomenal. Apple's H3 chip (yes, they're still using custom silicon) cancels out almost all low-frequency rumble — the sound of the train, the HVAC, the hum of the tunnel. Voices are muffled but still audible. I could listen to a podcast at 40% volume and hear every word.

The Galaxy Buds Pro 3 are good, but not great. They cancel low-frequency noise well, but mid-frequency sounds (like conversations or announcements) bleed through more. On a windy street, the Galaxy Buds had audible wind noise that the AirPods handled elegantly with their mesh-covered vents. Transparency mode is also better on the AirPods — it sounds almost like you're not wearing earbuds, whereas Samsung's version has a slight processed, hollow quality.

Winner: AirPods Pro 3, by a wide margin.

Call Quality: Samsung Catches Up

I make a lot of calls. Like, a lot. My job involves interviewing people, and I'm often on the go. Call quality is non-negotiable for me.

Previous Galaxy Buds were terrible for calls — they'd let in too much background noise, making me sound like I was talking from a wind tunnel. The Buds Pro 3 fix this. Samsung added a new AI-based noise reduction that isolates the wearer's voice from background sounds. I tested it by calling my sister from a Starbucks. She said I sounded clear — better than her AirPods Pro 2. Wind noise reduction is also improved.

The AirPods Pro 3 are still excellent, but they're not noticeably better than the Galaxy Buds Pro 3 anymore. Apple's voice isolation is slightly more natural, but Samsung's is more aggressive in noisy environments.

Winner: tie. Both are excellent for calls.

Battery Life and Charging: Samsung Wins on Paper, Apple Wins in Practice

Samsung's specs look better: 8 hours of listening with ANC on (vs. Apple's 6 hours) and 32 hours total with the case (vs. 30 hours). But in real-world use, I found the Galaxy Buds Pro 3 drained faster — about 6.5 hours with ANC and 360 Audio enabled. The AirPods Pro 3 consistently hit 6 hours, sometimes more. Samsung also doesn't support wireless charging as fast — 2.5 hours for a full charge vs. 1.5 hours for the AirPods.

Both cases support Qi wireless charging and USB-C. Apple's case has a built-in speaker for Find My, which is handy when you lose it under a couch cushion. Samsung's case has a tiny screen on the front (like the Buds 2 Pro) that shows battery levels — a nice touch, but I rarely looked at it.

Winner: edge to AirPods Pro 3 for more consistent real-world battery.

Ecosystem and Features: The Lock-In Is Real

Here's the thing: if you have an iPhone, the AirPods Pro 3 are magical. They switch seamlessly between your iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Watch. You can answer a call on your Mac, walk away, and the audio automatically transfers to your AirPods. Spatial Audio with head tracking works flawlessly. Siri integration is deep — you can ask it to send a message, set a timer, or control smart home devices without touching your phone.

If you have a Samsung phone, the Galaxy Buds Pro 3 offer similar integration: seamless switching between Galaxy devices, Bixby support, and Samsung's own spatial audio. But if you have a Pixel, OnePlus, or any other Android phone, you lose most of these features. The Galaxy Buds app on non-Samsung phones is basic — no 360 Audio, no voice assistant integration, no seamless switching.

The AirPods Pro 3 also work fine with Android — you lose the seamless switching and Siri, but you get basic controls and decent sound. The Galaxy Buds Pro 3 don't work with iPhones at all (no companion app on iOS).

Winner: AirPods Pro 3 for Apple users, Galaxy Buds Pro 3 for Samsung users. For everyone else, it's a toss-up.

Price and Value: A Surprising Twist

The AirPods Pro 3 cost $249. The Galaxy Buds Pro 3 cost $229. But here's the thing: Samsung has been running trade-in deals that bring the effective price down to $149 (I traded in my Buds 2 Pro for $80 off). Apple rarely offers meaningful discounts on AirPods. So if you can trade in an old pair, the Galaxy Buds are better value.

But resale value favors Apple. Used AirPods Pro 3 sell for $180-200 on eBay; Galaxy Buds Pro 3 go for $120-140. Over three years, the cost of ownership is roughly the same.

The Verdict: Which One Should You Buy?

I've been going back and forth for two weeks. Here's my honest recommendation:

Buy the AirPods Pro 3 if: You have an iPhone or iPad. You value noise cancellation, comfort, and ecosystem integration above all else. You want earbuds that just work, every time, without fuss.

Buy the Galaxy Buds Pro 3 if: You have a Samsung phone. You prefer a bass-heavy sound signature. You need longer battery life (on paper, at least). You can get a good trade-in deal.

If you have a Pixel or another Android phone: Honestly, I'd look elsewhere. The Google Pixel Buds Pro 2 (released in 2025) are a better fit for stock Android, with Google Assistant integration and a more balanced sound. They're also $199. Or consider the Nothing Ear (3) — they're $179, sound great, and have a unique design that's actually functional.

But if I had to pick one pair to keep for the next two years? I'd go with the AirPods Pro 3. They're not perfect — I wish they had better bass and longer battery life — but they're the most polished, reliable, and comfortable earbuds I've ever used. And in a world full of compromises, that's probably the best you can ask for.
