The Viral Dubai Chocolate Bar – Is It Worth the $40 Price Tag?

TR
By Christopher Lee · July 15, 2026 · 2 min read

The Chocolate Bar That Broke the Internet

You've probably seen it on TikTok. A gold-foiled chocolate bar being smashed open, revealing a neon green filling that's supposedly knafeh – the Middle Eastern pastry made with shredded phyllo dough and sweet cheese. It's called the "Can't Get Knafeh of It" bar from Fix Dessert Chocolatier in Dubai. And it's going for $40 on eBay. I paid $35 from a reseller and waited two weeks for it to arrive. Was it worth it? Let me tell you.

The Unboxing: First Impressions

The package arrived in a small box with no branding – just a plain brown envelope. The bar itself is heavy, about 200 grams. The gold foil is shiny but cheap-looking, like something from a dollar store. I unwrapped it and the smell hit me: chocolate, sesame, and something sweet and buttery. The bar is thick, about an inch deep. The green filling is actually pistachio paste mixed with shredded knafeh dough. It looks weird but appetizing.

The Taste Test: Sweet, Salty, and Addictive

I took a bite and my eyes widened. The chocolate is rich – they use Belgian milk chocolate with 43% cocoa. It's smooth and melts quickly. Then you hit the filling: the pistachio paste is nutty and sweet, and the knafeh dough adds a crunchy, almost flaky texture. There's a hint of salt that balances the sweetness. It's genuinely delicious. But here's the thing: it's incredibly sweet. Like, one square is enough. I couldn't finish the whole bar in one sitting. It's more of a dessert you share.

The Hype vs. Reality

The TikTok videos make it look life-changing. It's not. It's a really good chocolate bar with a unique filling. But $40? That's absurd. The bar itself probably costs $15 retail in Dubai. The resellers are gouging you. If you can get it for under $20, it's worth trying once. But don't pay $40. I regret spending that much. The novelty wears off quickly. You're paying for the hype, not the quality. There are better chocolate bars out there – try a Tony's Chocolonely for $5 and get 80% of the experience.

The Cultural Angle: What Is Knafeh?

Knafeh is a traditional Middle Eastern dessert that's been around for centuries. It's made with a cheese base, topped with shredded phyllo dough or semolina, and soaked in sweet syrup. The Fix bar takes inspiration from it, but it's not authentic. The real knafeh is warm, gooey, and eaten fresh. This is a chocolate bar with knafeh-inspired flavor. Don't confuse the two. If you want real knafeh, go to a Middle Eastern restaurant. It's better.

So, is it worth the hype? For the experience, maybe. But for the price, no. Save your $40 and buy a box of good Middle Eastern sweets instead. Your wallet will thank you.
