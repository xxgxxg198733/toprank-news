Apple vs. Samsung: Best Smartphones of June 2026 Compared Head-to-Head

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 72,775 views

The battle for smartphone supremacy heats up as Apple's iPhone 17 Pro Max and Samsung's Galaxy S26 Ultra go head-to-head in our comprehensive comparison. Both phones launched in May 2026, and each claims to be the best. We tested them for a week to find out which one truly is.

Design and Display

The iPhone 17 Pro Max features a titanium frame and a 6.9-inch OLED display with a 120Hz ProMotion refresh rate. Samsung counters with a 6.8-inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X display, also 120Hz, but with a higher peak brightness of 2,600 nits. In side-by-side tests, the Samsung screen is slightly brighter outdoors, but the iPhone's color accuracy wins indoors.

— Advertisement —

Camera Performance

Apple's new 48MP periscope lens offers 5x optical zoom, while Samsung's 200MP sensor provides 10x optical zoom. In low light, the iPhone captures more natural skin tones, but the Galaxy S26 Ultra delivers sharper details at long distances. Video recording favors Apple, with 8K at 60fps and improved stabilization.

Battery and Price

The iPhone 17 Pro Max has a 4,500mAh battery that lasted 14 hours in our video streaming test. The Galaxy S26 Ultra's 5,500mAh battery lasted 16 hours. However, the iPhone charges faster with 45W wired charging versus Samsung's 45W. Prices are neck-and-neck: the iPhone starts at $1,299, while the Galaxy starts at $1,199. For most users, the Galaxy offers better value, but the iPhone wins on ecosystem integration.
