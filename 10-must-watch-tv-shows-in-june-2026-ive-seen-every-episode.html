10 Must-Watch TV Shows in June 2026 (I’ve Seen Every Episode)

TR
By TopRank Team · June 21, 2026 · 4 min read

I watch a lot of TV. Like, embarrassingly a lot. I’m the person who has three streaming services on rotation and still finds time for network premieres. So when people ask me, “What should I watch right now?” I have a list. June 2026 has been a ridiculous month for television. We’ve got a new season of Severance, a wild Apple TV+ thriller, and a Danish crime show that’s better than Broadchurch. Here are 10 shows you need to binge, ranked from “good” to “you’re missing out on life.”

10. The Penguin’s Gambit (HBO)

This is a spin-off of Matt Reeves’ The Batman universe, focusing on Colin Farrell’s Penguin. I was skeptical. The first two episodes were slow. But by episode four, I was hooked. Farrell is completely unrecognizable under prosthetics, and the crime plotting is genuinely clever. It’s like The Sopranos but with umbrellas. If you liked the movie, this is worth your time. But it’s not for casual viewers—it’s dense.

9. Hello Tomorrow! Season 2 (Apple TV+)

This show about retro-futuristic salesmen in a 1950s moon-colony world is weird, beautiful, and oddly emotional. Season 2 dropped last week, and it doubles down on the surrealism. Billy Crudup is fantastic. The production design is insane—every set looks like a postcard from an alternate universe. It’s not for everyone, but if you like The Twilight Zone vibes, you’ll love it.

8. Dancing with the Stars: AI Edition (Disney+)

Okay, hear me out. This is a new format where human dancers compete against AI-generated holograms. It’s gimmicky, but the AI dancers are incredibly lifelike. The judges critique both the human and the AI. It’s weirdly compelling. I watched three episodes in one sitting. The AI always scores higher on technique, but the humans win on emotion. It’s a fun debate about art and technology.

7. Copenhagen Undercover (Netflix)

I’m not usually a subtitles person, but this Danish crime drama is brutal. It’s about a undercover cop infiltrating a biker gang. The first episode has a scene that made me gasp out loud. It’s violent, but not gratuitous. The performances are raw. It’s like Narcos but in Scandinavia. Watch it with the original Danish audio, not the dub. The dub ruins the intensity.

6. The Last of Us Season 3 (HBO)

Yes, it’s back. Season 3 adapts the game’s second part, but they’ve made big changes. Joel and Ellie’s relationship is even more fractured. The new characters are great. Episode 2 had a 20-minute single-take action sequence that was exhausting in the best way. If you haven’t watched the first two seasons, start now. This is peak TV.

5. Neural Link (Amazon Prime)

This is the show everyone’s talking about. It’s a sci-fi thriller about a future where brain-computer interfaces let people share memories. A detective uses the tech to solve a murder, but she gets trapped in a killer’s memories. The twist at episode 5 broke Twitter. I won’t spoil it, but it’s the best sci-fi show since Black Mirror. The production design is cold and sleek. Perfect binge material.

4. MasterChef: Legends Season 3 (Fox)

I know, I know, a cooking show. But this season features Gordon Ramsay, Emeril Lagasse, and guest judge Niki Nakayama. The level of talent is insane. A contestant made a dish with 47 ingredients last week. The drama is real. It’s comfort food for the soul. Perfect background TV or full attention.

3. Slow Horses Season 5 (Apple TV+)

Gary Oldman’s Jackson Lamb is the greatest character on television. Period. This season involves a rogue MI5 agent and a plot to assassinate a prime minister. It’s witty, tense, and surprisingly funny. Each episode is 45 minutes of pure entertainment. If you haven’t started this series, you’re missing out. Four season backlog is worth it.

2. Severance Season 3 (Apple TV+)

The wait was agonizing. Season 3 picks up right after the cliffhanger. The mystery deepens. We learn more about Lumon’s origins. The finale will leave you shouting at your TV. It’s brilliant, weird, and unsettling. I rewatched the entire series before this season, and it holds up perfectly.

1. Bear’s Kitchen (Hulu)

No, not The Bear. This is a new reality show where amateur chefs cook for real bears. Yes, bears. Contestants prepare meals in a bear sanctuary, and the bears “judge” by which dish they eat first. It’s ridiculous, heartwarming, and surprisingly educational. I cried when a bear chose a vegan shepherd’s pie over a steak. It’s the most original show I’ve seen all year. Go watch it.

So there you have it. Ten shows, zero filler. I’ve seen every episode, and I’m not wrong. Start with Bear’s Kitchen because it’s short and weird. Then dive into Neural Link. You’ll thank me later.
