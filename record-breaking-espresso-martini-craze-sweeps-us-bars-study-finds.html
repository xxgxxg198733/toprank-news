Record-Breaking 'Espresso' Martini Craze Sweeps US Bars, Study Finds

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 72,204 views

Move over, margarita. The espresso martini is having a moment unlike any other. According to a new report from beverage data firm CGA by NIQ, sales of the caffeinated cocktail surged 45% in Q1 2026 compared to the same period last year, making it the fastest-growing mixed drink in the United States.

The Perfect Storm

Bartenders attribute the boom to a confluence of trends. 'It's the perfect after-dinner drink that also gives you energy,' says James Williams, head mixologist at The Aviary in Chicago. The rise of 'aperitivo hour' and 'work-appropriate drinking' has boosted demand for lower-ABV, flavor-forward options. Espresso martinis typically contain vodka, coffee liqueur, and fresh espresso, averaging 15% ABV.

Social Media Fuel

TikTok has been a major driver. The hashtag #EspressoMartini has amassed over 2.5 billion views, with influencers showcasing elaborate garnishes like frothy art and edible glitter. 'It's photogenic and sophisticated,' says food trend analyst Fiona Wang. Brands like Kahlúa and Mr Black are launching limited-edition espresso martini kits, further fueling the craze.

— Advertisement —

Regional Variations

In New York, bars are adding a splash of vanilla vodka; in LA, coconut cream is trending. Meanwhile, coffee giant Starbucks reports a 30% increase in requests for 'espresso martini' style cold brews. 'People want that flavor profile at home,' says CEO Laxman Narasimhan. The trend shows no signs of slowing down, with major spirit brands investing in coffee-flavored products.

For home bartenders, the recipe is simple: 2 oz vodka, 1 oz coffee liqueur, 1 oz fresh espresso, and a dash of simple syrup. Shake vigorously and strain. Serve in a chilled coupe. Cheers to the caffeinated cocktail revolution.
