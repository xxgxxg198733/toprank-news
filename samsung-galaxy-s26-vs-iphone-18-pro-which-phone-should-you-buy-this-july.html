Samsung Galaxy S26 vs iPhone 18 Pro: Which Phone Should You Buy This July?

TR
By Nicole Barnes · July 23, 2026 · 7 min read

It's July 2026, and the two biggest phone launches of the year just hit stores. Samsung dropped the Galaxy S26 on July 9, and Apple followed with the iPhone 18 Pro on July 16. I've been using both as my daily drivers for the past two weeks, and I have thoughts. Lots of them.

Before I get into the nitty-gritty, let me confess something: I've been an iPhone user since the iPhone 6. I'm not a fanboy — I just got used to the ecosystem. But this year, Samsung made a phone that genuinely made me consider switching. The Galaxy S26 is that good. But is it good enough to pull you away from Apple's walled garden? Let's break it down.

Design and Build: Two Very Different Philosophies

The iPhone 18 Pro looks like an iPhone. That's not a bad thing — Apple's design language is refined and premium. The titanium frame feels solid, the matte glass back doesn't attract fingerprints, and the Dynamic Island is still here (though it's now more interactive, showing live notifications for things like Uber ETA). It's a phone that says "I'm serious."

The Galaxy S26, on the other hand, is a riot of color. Samsung went with a ceramic back this year, which feels even more premium than glass. The camera bump is a horizontal strip that runs across the top, like a mini soundbar. It's bold. I got the "Violet Dream" color, and people stopped me on the street to ask what phone it was. The S26 also has a slightly curved edge display that makes it feel thinner in the hand — 7.2mm vs the iPhone's 7.8mm.

Which is better? It depends on what you value. The iPhone feels like a precise tool. The Samsung feels like a piece of art. I lean toward the Samsung for its uniqueness, but the iPhone is objectively more durable (it has a Class 5 water resistance rating vs the S26's IP68).

Display: Samsung Dominates (Again)

This one isn't close. The Galaxy S26 has a 6.8-inch Dynamic AMOLED 4X display with a 144Hz refresh rate and 3,000 nits peak brightness. The iPhone 18 Pro has a 6.9-inch Super Retina XDR ProMotion display with 120Hz and 2,500 nits. Numbers only tell part of the story, though.

Watching a 4K HDR video on the S26 is breathtaking. Colors are punchy without being oversaturated, blacks are truly deep, and the 144Hz refresh rate makes scrolling feel like butter. The iPhone's display is excellent — you won't complain about it — but the Samsung just looks more vibrant. The only area where the iPhone wins is color accuracy. If you're a photographer who edits photos on your phone, the iPhone's display is more true-to-life.

But for everyday use? Samsung takes the crown. I binged three episodes of "The Bear" on the S26, and I didn't want to stop.

Performance: The Chipset Battle

Both phones are fast. Like, comically fast. The Galaxy S26 uses the Qualcomm Snapdragon 9 Gen 3 (custom for Samsung), while the iPhone 18 Pro packs the A18 Bionic chip. In benchmark tests, they're neck and neck — the iPhone edges ahead in single-core performance, while the Samsung leads in multi-core and GPU tasks.

In real-world use, I couldn't tell the difference. Apps opened instantly, games ran at max settings, and multitasking was seamless. The Samsung has 16GB of RAM (vs the iPhone's 12GB), which means it keeps more apps in memory. I could switch between a heavy game like Genshin Impact, a video call, and a browser with 20 tabs open without any reloading. The iPhone started reloading apps after about 12 tabs.

Thermal management is better on the Samsung, too. After 30 minutes of gaming, the iPhone got noticeably warm — not hot, but uncomfortable. The S26 stayed cool, thanks to its vapor chamber cooling system.

Camera: Samsung's Zoom vs Apple's Consistency

This is the biggest differentiator. The Galaxy S26 has a 200MP main sensor, a 50MP ultra-wide, and a 50MP periscope telephoto with 10x optical zoom. The iPhone 18 Pro has a 48MP main, a 48MP ultra-wide, and a 12MP periscope telephoto with 5x optical zoom.

In good light, both cameras take stunning photos. Colors are different — Samsung leans toward warmer, more saturated tones, while Apple aims for accuracy. I took photos of a sunset at a beach, and the Samsung made the sky look more dramatic, while the iPhone captured the exact colors I saw with my eyes.

Where Samsung blows Apple out of the water is zoom. The 10x optical zoom on the S26 is incredible. I took a photo of a bird sitting on a tree branch 100 feet away, and I could see every feather. The iPhone's 5x zoom is good, but it's not even close. Samsung also has a new "Nightography 2.0" mode that captures better low-light zoom shots than ever. The iPhone's low-light photos are cleaner, but they lack detail at zoom levels beyond 5x.

For video, the iPhone is still king. The ProRes recording is smooth, the dynamic range is excellent, and the stabilization is magical. Samsung improved its video stabilization this year, but it still has some jitter when you're walking fast. If you shoot a lot of video, get the iPhone.

Battery Life and Charging: A Clear Winner

The Galaxy S26 has a 5,500mAh battery, while the iPhone 18 Pro has a 4,400mAh battery. That's a huge difference. In my testing, the S26 easily lasted a full day and a half with heavy use (gaming, streaming, camera). The iPhone barely made it through a full day — I had to charge it by 7 PM if I started at 7 AM.

Charging is another blowout. The S26 supports 45W wired charging and 25W wireless. The iPhone supports 35W wired and 15W MagSafe. The S26 went from 0 to 100% in 55 minutes. The iPhone took 1 hour 40 minutes. Samsung also includes a charger in the box (gasp!), while Apple still expects you to buy one separately.

If you hate charging your phone, the Samsung is the obvious choice.

Software and Ecosystem: The Real Decider

Here's where things get personal. One UI 7.0 (based on Android 16) is the best version of Samsung's software yet. It's clean, customizable, and feature-rich. You can split-screen apps, use the S Pen (if you buy the Ultra variant), and customize everything from the lock screen to the always-on display. Samsung also promises 7 years of OS updates, same as Apple.

But iOS is still more polished. The animations are smoother, the app ecosystem is more curated, and iMessage is still unbeatable if your friends and family use it. The iPhone also integrates seamlessly with my MacBook, iPad, and Apple Watch. The Samsung works with Windows laptops and Galaxy Watches, but the integration isn't as deep.

Here's the honest truth: if you're deeply embedded in Apple's ecosystem, the iPhone 18 Pro is the better choice. Switching is a pain. But if you're not, or if you're willing to leave, the Galaxy S26 offers more hardware for your money.

Price and Value

The Galaxy S26 starts at $1,099 for the base model (256GB). The iPhone 18 Pro starts at $1,199 for the same storage. That's a $100 difference. Samsung also offers trade-in deals that are much better — I got $650 for my old Galaxy S22, bringing the price down to $449. Apple offered me $350 for my iPhone 14 Pro.

If you're looking for the best value, the Samsung is the clear winner. You get more RAM, a better display, a better camera zoom, faster charging, and a lower price.

The Verdict

I'm sticking with my iPhone. Why? Because iMessage and my Apple Watch are too important to me. But if I were buying a phone from scratch today, I'd buy the Galaxy S26 without hesitation. It's the better phone for most people. Samsung finally caught up to Apple in performance and surpassed it in design, camera zoom, battery life, and charging speed. The iPhone 18 Pro is a fantastic phone — it's polished, reliable, and consistent. But the Galaxy S26 is more exciting, more capable, and more affordable.

So my recommendation: get the Samsung if you value cutting-edge hardware and don't mind leaving Apple's ecosystem. Get the iPhone if you're loyal to the ecosystem or shoot tons of video. You won't be unhappy either way, but one of them is clearly the better value.
