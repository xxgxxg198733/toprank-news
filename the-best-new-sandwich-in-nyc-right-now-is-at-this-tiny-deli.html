The Best New Sandwich in NYC Right Now Is At This Tiny Deli

TR
By David Kim · July 22, 2026 · 3 min read

My Week of Sandwich Obsession

I have a problem: I can't resist a good sandwich. When I moved to New York two years ago, I made it my mission to find the best ones. This week, I took it to the extreme: I ate 14 sandwiches from 14 different places in 7 days. My wallet hurts, and my jeans are tight, but I found something special.

The sandwich that blew my mind isn't from Katz's or Carnegie Deli (though those are classics). It's from a tiny spot in the East Village called Sammy's Sandwiches. I know, the name sounds generic, but trust me.

The Discovery

I stumbled on Sammy's last Tuesday while walking down St. Mark's Place. A handwritten sign in the window said 'Best Pastrami in the Village' — bold claim. Inside, it's cramped: four stools, a counter, and a guy named Dave who's been making sandwiches for 30 years. He used to work at a deli in Brooklyn before opening this spot in 2024.

I ordered the 'Sammy Special': pastrami, Swiss cheese, coleslaw, and Russian dressing on rye bread. It cost $14 — reasonable for NYC. The sandwich arrived in 5 minutes, wrapped in wax paper. It was massive: the meat stacked at least two inches high.

The Taste Test

First bite: the pastrami is smoked in-house. It's peppery, fatty, and tender — not dry like some delis. The coleslaw adds a crunch and acidity that cuts through the richness. The rye bread is toasted just enough to hold everything without falling apart. The Russian dressing is tangy, not too sweet.

I finished it in 10 minutes. I didn't want to. I wanted it to last longer. I immediately ordered another to take home. My girlfriend ate it and said, 'This is better than Katz's.' I didn't disagree.

How It Compares to the Competition

I went to Katz's later that week. Their pastrami is legendary — it's thicker, saltier, and more iconic. But it's also $26 for a sandwich that's mostly meat and bread. Sammy's offers a more balanced sandwich with better ingredients for less money. Also, Katz's is a zoo on weekends. Sammy's has no line.

I also tried Mile End Deli in Brooklyn (great smoked meat, but the bread was soggy) and Russ & Daughters (their lox bagel is still amazing, but it's not a sandwich). Sammy's wins because it's unexpected. It's not trying to be trendy — it's just a good sandwich made by a guy who cares.

The Secret Ingredient

I asked Dave what his secret is. He said, 'Time.' He smokes the brisket for 12 hours, then steams it for another hour before slicing. Most delis buy pre-smoked meat. He does it himself. Also, he makes his own coleslaw from scratch — cabbage, carrots, vinegar, and a touch of sugar. It's not from a bag.

Other Sandwiches I Tried (Ranked)

For context: I also had the Italian combo at Parm (good but overpriced at $18), the meatball sub at Fini Pizza (surprisingly excellent, $12), and a banh mi at Bánh Mì Saigon in Chinatown (still the best $8 sandwich in the city). But none topped Sammy's.

If you're in NYC this summer, skip the tourist spots. Go to Sammy's. Order the Sammy Special. Eat it on a bench in Tompkins Square Park. You'll thank me later.

One warning: they close at 4 PM and are closed Sundays. Dave says he 'doesn't believe in dinner hours.' That's the spirit.
