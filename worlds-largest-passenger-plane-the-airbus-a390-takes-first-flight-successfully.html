World's Largest Passenger Plane, the 'Airbus A390', Takes First Flight Successfully

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 354,029 views

Airbus successfully completed the maiden flight of its new A390 on June 6, 2026, in Toulouse, France. The aircraft, the world's largest passenger plane, took off at 9:00 AM local time and flew for 4 hours and 37 minutes, reaching an altitude of 12,000 meters and a top speed of Mach 0.89. The flight was deemed a complete success by Airbus CEO Guillaume Faury.

Specs and Capacity

The A390 is a double-decker, four-engine jet with a maximum takeoff weight of 650 tons. It can carry up to 600 passengers in a standard three-class configuration, or up to 850 in a high-density layout. The wingspan is 88 meters, and the fuselage is 77 meters long. The aircraft is powered by four Rolls-Royce Ultrafan engines, which are 25% more fuel-efficient than previous generation engines, reducing carbon emissions per passenger by 30% compared to the Boeing 747.

A New Era for Air Travel

Airbus claims the A390 will enable non-stop flights between virtually any two cities in the world, including routes like London-Perth, New York-Singapore, and Los Angeles-Dubai. The cabin features a revolutionary 'Sky Suite' design with wider seats, larger windows, and a state-of-the-art air filtration system. Airlines have already placed over 200 orders, with Emirates, Singapore Airlines, and Delta Air Lines among the first customers. The first commercial flight is expected in early 2028.

Competition and Challenges

The A390 is a direct competitor to Boeing's proposed 777X-10, which has been delayed due to technical issues. However, the sheer size of the A390 raises logistical challenges at airports, which must adapt their gates, runways, and tarmacs to accommodate the giant. Airbus says it has already worked with 50 major airports to prepare for the A390. The successful test flight is a major milestone for Airbus and the aviation industry, signaling a new era of 'super-jumbo' air travel.
