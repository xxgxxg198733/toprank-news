Tourists Flock to 'Silicon Valley of India' as Bengaluru Named World's Best Digital Nomad Destination

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

The 2026 Digital Nomad Index, published on June 3 by the travel website NomadList, has ranked Bengaluru (Bangalore), India, as the world's top destination for remote workers. The city, known as India's Silicon Valley, scored highest in categories like internet speed, cost of living, and community for digital nomads, beating perennial favorites like Lisbon, Bali, and Chiang Mai.

Why Bengaluru Won

The index evaluated 150 cities based on factors including average monthly cost (estimated at $1,200 for a comfortable lifestyle), average internet speed (150 Mbps), visa accessibility (India offers a 5-year e-tourist visa and a new digital nomad visa launched in 2025), and the number of co-working spaces and cafes. Bengaluru has over 500 co-working spaces, including major chains like WeWork and local hubs like 91springboard, along with a vibrant food and nightlife scene. The city's pleasant year-round climate (averaging 25°C) was also a major draw.

Rising Trend of 'Work from India'

The ranking reflects a broader trend of Western digital nomads choosing cities in South and Southeast Asia. The number of long-stay tourists in Bengaluru has increased by 40% year-over-year, according to the Karnataka Tourism Department. Many are attracted by the strong local tech community and opportunities for collaboration with Indian startups. The city also offers easy access to other travel destinations, like the beaches of Goa (a 6-hour drive) and the hill stations of Coorg.

Challenges and Tips for Visitors

While Bengaluru offers many advantages, visitors should be aware of challenges like traffic congestion (ranked among the worst in the world), air quality issues during the winter months, and a high level of humidity. NomadList recommends staying in areas like Indiranagar, Koramangala, or Whitefield, which have the best co-working spaces and cafes. Flights from major US cities like New York and San Francisco to Bengaluru start at around $800 round trip. The city is also home to a growing community of over 10,000 foreign digital nomads, making it easy to network and make friends.
