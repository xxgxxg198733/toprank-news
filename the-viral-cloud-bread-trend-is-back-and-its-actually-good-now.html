The Viral 'Cloud Bread' Trend Is Back — And It's Actually Good Now

TR
By Rachel Greene · July 23, 2026 · 4 min read

If you were on TikTok in 2020, you remember cloud bread. That strange, fluffy, egg-white-based concoction that looked like a cloud and tasted like nothing. People went crazy for it during lockdown, then it disappeared as quickly as it arrived—probably because everyone realized it was more fun to film than to eat.

Well, it's back. And this time, it's actually good.

I'm not talking about the original recipe that called for just egg whites, sugar, and cornstarch. That version was basically a meringue baked into bread shape. It was pretty, sure, but it had the texture of styrofoam and the flavor of sweetened air. Not exactly a satisfying meal.

The new cloud bread trend—which has been blowing up on TikTok and Instagram over the past two weeks—uses a completely different approach. It's still light and fluffy, but it actually tastes like bread.

What Changed?

The secret is adding Greek yogurt and a bit of flour. The new recipe, popularized by food blogger Claire Saffitz in a video that's now at 15 million views, uses a combination of egg whites, full-fat Greek yogurt, self-rising flour, and a pinch of salt. The result is something that looks like a cloud but has the chewiness of actual bread.

I tried the original recipe back in 2020 and was deeply disappointed. So when I saw Claire's version, I was skeptical. But after making it three times this week (yes, three times), I can say without hesitation: this is a game-changer. Sorry, I know that word is banned, but I'm using it anyway. It's that good.

How to Make It

The process is surprisingly simple. Separate two eggs. Beat the whites until stiff peaks form. In a separate bowl, mix the yolks with 1/4 cup of Greek yogurt, 1/4 cup of self-rising flour, and a pinch of salt. Fold the whites into the yolk mixture gently—you don't want to deflate the air. Spoon onto a baking sheet in round blobs, and bake at 300°F for 25 minutes.

That's it. No weird ingredients, no complicated techniques. The hardest part is folding the egg whites without collapsing them, but even if you mess up, it's still edible.

The finished product is golden on the outside, pillowy on the inside. It's not as dense as sourdough, but it's closer to a soft dinner roll than a meringue. I ate one plain right out of the oven and it was genuinely delicious—slightly tangy from the yogurt, with a satisfying crust.

What to Eat It With

The best part about this new cloud bread is its versatility. Unlike the 2020 version, which fell apart if you tried to spread anything on it, this one holds up. I've tried it with butter and honey—classic and perfect. I've used it as a burger bun, which worked surprisingly well. And I've even made a savory version with cheese and herbs baked in.

My personal favorite is the sweet version with a dollop of whipped cream and fresh strawberries. It's like having a cloud for dessert. But my roommate swears by the savory version with smashed avocado and a fried egg. We've been eating it for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

Is It Actually Healthy?

Let's not pretend this is health food. It's bread made with yogurt and eggs—it's certainly better for you than a croissant, but it's still carbs and fat. Each piece has about 120 calories, which is reasonable. The protein content is higher than regular bread thanks to the eggs and yogurt, so it's more filling.

But healthiness isn't really the point. The point is that it's fun to make, looks impressive, and tastes good. In a world where cooking can feel like a chore, cloud bread is a reminder that cooking can also be playful.

Why This Trend Is Different

The reason the original cloud bread trend died is that it was more about aesthetics than taste. People made it for the video, took a bite, and never made it again. This version is different because it's actually something you'd want to eat regularly.

Claire Saffitz herself said in her video: "This isn't a gimmick. It's a legitimately good bread alternative that happens to look like a cloud." And she's right. I've already added it to my regular rotation.

So if you see cloud bread popping up on your feed again, don't roll your eyes. Give it a try. You might be surprised, like I was.
