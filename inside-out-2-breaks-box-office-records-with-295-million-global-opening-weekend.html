'Inside Out 2' Breaks Box Office Records with $295 Million Global Opening Weekend

TR
By TopRank Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

Pixar's 'Inside Out 2' roared into theaters this weekend, collecting an estimated $295 million globally, the biggest opening weekend for any animated film in history. The sequel to the 2015 original shattered the previous record held by 'Frozen 2' ($290 million) and earned an A+ CinemaScore, a rare achievement for any film. Directed by Kelsey Mann, the film sees the return of Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust, now joined by new emotions like Anxiety, Envy, Embarrassment, and Ennui as Riley enters her teenage years.

Domestic and International Numbers

In North America, 'Inside Out 2' earned $155 million, the second-best opening of 2026 behind 'Avengers: Secret Wars' ($210 million). Internationally, it grossed $140 million, with strong performances in Mexico ($25 million), the UK ($18 million), and Japan ($15 million). The film opened in 55 markets and is expected to cross the $1 billion mark by its third weekend, a feat only a handful of animated films have achieved. The diverse cast of emotions, voiced by Maya Hawke (Anxiety) and Ayo Edebiri (Envy), has been particularly praised.

Critical Reception and Audience Response

The film holds a 96% rating on Rotten Tomatoes from critics, with many calling it a worthy successor that deepens the original's themes about emotional intelligence. Audiences have been equally enthusiastic, with sold-out screenings across the globe. The film's emotional climax, involving Riley's first panic attack, has been described as 'devastatingly beautiful' by reviewers. Parents, in particular, have praised the film for helping children understand complex emotions like anxiety and embarrassment. The film's marketing campaign, which included a viral TikTok trend where users recreated their own 'control panel' of emotions, has also been credited with driving audience interest.

What This Means for Pixar and Theaters

The success of 'Inside Out 2' is a massive win for Pixar, which has struggled with direct-to-streaming releases during the pandemic. The film's theatrical-only release strategy paid off, with families returning to cinemas in droves. For the overall box office, 'Inside Out 2' provides a much-needed boost after a slow spring. Disney CEO Bob Iger called the result 'a testament to the power of great storytelling and the theatrical experience.' The film's success also paves the way for a third installment, which Disney has already greenlit for a 2029 release.

If you haven't seen it yet, clear your schedule. 'Inside Out 2' is not just a great family film—it's one of the best movies of the year.
