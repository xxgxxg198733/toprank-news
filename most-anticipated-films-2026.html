The 10 Most Anticipated Films of the Second Half of 2026, From Marvel's Next to Nolan's Homer

TR
TopRank Editorial TeamMay 28, 2026⏱ 4 min read328,700 views

Box office analysts at Variety, The Hollywood Reporter, and Deadline have compiled tracking data — social media mention volume, trailer view counts, and pre-release audience surveys from Screen Engine/ASI — to project which films will dominate the second half of 2026. Here are the 10 with the strongest advance indicators.

#1 — "Avengers: Eternity War" (Marvel Studios, December 18): The conclusion of the Multiverse Saga, directed by Ryan Coogler ("Black Panther"). The confirmed cast list includes 31 named characters, spanning every Marvel Cinematic Universe film since 2008. The trailer, released during Super Bowl LX, accumulated 287 million views across platforms in 24 hours — the second-highest trailer debut in history, behind only "Avengers: Endgame" (289 million). Industry tracking service The Quorum projects a domestic opening weekend of $265-295 million.

#2 — "Dune: Messiah" (Warner Bros./Legendary, October 16): Denis Villeneuve completes his Frank Herbert adaptation trilogy with the most challenging and interior of the Dune novels. The film covers a narrower time period than either "Dune: Part Two" or the first film, focusing on Paul Atreides' (Timothée Chalamet) reckoning with the jihad conducted in his name. Florence Pugh reprises her role as Princess Irulan, now with significantly expanded screen time. The screenplay was co-written by Villeneuve and celebrated playwright Tony Kushner ("Angels in America").

#3 — "The Odyssey" (Universal, July 17): Christopher Nolan's adaptation of Homer's epic poem, shot on IMAX 65mm film across seven countries (Greece, Turkey, Italy, Morocco, Malta, Iceland, and the United Kingdom). Matt Damon plays Odysseus. Zendaya plays Circe. Anne Hathaway plays Penelope. The production built a full-scale Trojan horse for the sacking of Troy sequence, which occupies approximately 18 minutes of screen time and reportedly involved 1,500 extras. Nolan has emphasized that the film uses practical effects for all sequences except the encounter with Scylla, which required digital visual effects that he described as "unavoidable given the anatomical requirements of a six-headed sea monster."

#4 — "Spider-Man: Beyond the Spider-Verse" (Sony Pictures Animation, June 27): The trilogy concludes after multiple delays that co-director Phil Lord attributed to "the fact that we keep coming up with ideas that make the movie better and also more impossible to finish." Lord told Empire magazine that the film contains "over 240 unique animation styles representing different dimensions, each developed by a different artist or small team." The voice cast includes Shameik Moore, Hailee Steinfeld, and Oscar Isaac.

#5 — "The Wager" (Apple Original Films/Paramount, November 6): Martin Scorsese directs Leonardo DiCaprio in an adaptation of David Grann's 2023 nonfiction bestseller about the 1741 wreck of the HMS Wager, a British naval vessel that ran aground off the coast of Patagonia during a mission to capture a Spanish treasure galleon. The surviving crew split into factions, accused each other of mutiny and murder, and presented conflicting accounts upon their eventual return to England. The screenplay intercuts between the survivors' court-martial testimony and flashbacks to the wreck and its aftermath.
