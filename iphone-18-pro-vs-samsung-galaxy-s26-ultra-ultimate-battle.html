iPhone 18 Pro vs Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra: Ultimate Battle

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 274,828 views

The battle for smartphone supremacy continues in 2026 with Apple's iPhone 18 Pro and Samsung's Galaxy S26 Ultra. Both are technological marvels, but which one deserves your money? We've put them through rigorous testing to find out.

Design and Display

The iPhone 18 Pro features a titanium frame with a 6.7-inch Super Retina XDR OLED display (LTPO 2.0) with 120Hz ProMotion. The S26 Ultra counters with a 6.9-inch Dynamic AMOLED 3X display with 144Hz refresh rate and 5,000 nits peak brightness. The Samsung is larger and brighter, but the iPhone feels more refined in hand. Both are IP68 rated. Winner: Tie.

Camera Showdown

The iPhone 18 Pro sports a triple-lens setup: 48MP main (f/1.6), 48MP ultrawide, and 12MP periscope telephoto with 10x optical zoom. The S26 Ultra goes all out with a 200MP main, 50MP ultrawide, and dual telephoto lenses (10x and 5x optical). In daylight, both produce stunning shots, but Samsung's 200MP sensor captures more detail. At night, Apple's Night Mode still leads with better noise reduction. For video, the iPhone's new 'Cinematic Pro' mode with AI subject tracking is unmatched. Winner: Tie (Samsung for stills, Apple for video).

Performance and Battery

The A20 Bionic chip in the iPhone 18 Pro is a beast, with a 6-core CPU and 6-core GPU. The Snapdragon 8 Gen 4 in the S26 Ultra is equally powerful. In Geekbench 6, the iPhone scores 8,500 single-core and 22,000 multi-core, while the Samsung scores 7,800 and 21,500. Gaming performance is identical. Battery life is where they diverge: the S26 Ultra's 10,000mAh battery lasts 2 full days vs the iPhone's 4,500mAh lasting 1.5 days. The Samsung also charges faster: 65W wired vs 45W. Winner: Samsung (battery, charging).

Price and Verdict

The iPhone 18 Pro starts at $1,199, while the S26 Ultra starts at $1,399. If you're deep in the Apple ecosystem, the iPhone is the obvious choice. But for sheer hardware, display size, and battery life, the Samsung offers more value. Our pick: The Galaxy S26 Ultra is the better all-rounder, but the iPhone 18 Pro is the best for creators and gamers.
