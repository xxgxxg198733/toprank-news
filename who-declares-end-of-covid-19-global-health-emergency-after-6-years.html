WHO Declares End of COVID-19 Global Health Emergency After 6 Years

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 335,511 views

In a landmark moment, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared an end to the COVID-19 global health emergency today, June 5, 2026. The announcement comes exactly 6 years and 3 months after the WHO first declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) on January 30, 2020.

The Data Behind the Decision

The WHO's Emergency Committee met on June 3 and recommended the declaration be lifted. Global weekly cases have fallen to 50,000, down from a peak of 25 million in January 2022. Deaths are at 1,000 per week globally, compared to 100,000 at the height of the pandemic. Over 90% of the world's population now has some immunity through vaccination or prior infection.

What This Means

While the emergency is over, COVID-19 is now considered an endemic disease, like the flu. Travel restrictions, mask mandates, and vaccine passports are no longer recommended. However, the WHO emphasized that vulnerable populations should continue to receive booster shots. The global death toll stands at 18.5 million, making it the deadliest pandemic in a century.

Legacy and Lessons

The pandemic reshaped public health systems, accelerated mRNA vaccine technology, and permanently changed work and travel habits. 'We must never forget the lives lost and the sacrifices made,' said WHO Director-General Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus. 'But today, we celebrate a new chapter of hope.' The WHO will now focus on preventing the next pandemic, with a new Global Pandemic Treaty expected to be finalized by 2027.

For the average person, life has largely returned to normal. But the scars of the pandemic remain, from mental health issues to economic disruptions. The end of the emergency is a symbolic milestone that marks the beginning of a post-pandemic world.
