FIFA World Cup 2026: USA, Mexico, Canada Prepare for 48-Team Spectacular

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 495,661 views

With just 12 days until the opening match, excitement is building for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, which will be co-hosted by the United States, Mexico, and Canada. This year's tournament is historic: the first to feature 48 teams, the first to be hosted by three nations, and the first to use a 'Final Four'-style group stage. Organizers expect a record 5 million attendees across 16 venues, from the Azteca Stadium in Mexico City to the newly built SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles.

The New Format

The expanded field means 104 matches instead of the usual 64, with 16 three-team groups. The top two from each group advance to a 32-team knockout round, a change designed to reduce 'dead rubber' games. Critics worry about scheduling congestion, but FIFA President Gianni Infantino called it 'the most inclusive World Cup ever,' with more nations than ever before, including debutants like Cape Verde and Uzbekistan. The United States, as top-ranked host, will open the tournament against New Zealand in Houston on June 15.

Security and Logistics

Security is a top priority, with the Department of Homeland Security designating the event a 'Level 1' national security special event. Over 10,000 law enforcement officers will be deployed across stadiums and fan zones. The State Department has also streamlined visa processing for international fans, though some have reported delays. Transportation infrastructure, including expanded light rail in Los Angeles and high-speed rail corridors in Mexico, has been upgraded to handle the influx.

Stars to Watch

All eyes will be on Kylian Mbappé of France, widely considered the world's best player, as he leads the defending champions. Lionel Messi, now 38, has confirmed this will be his last World Cup, adding emotional weight to Argentina's campaign. Host nation USA boasts a young, dynamic squad led by Christian Pulisic and Yunus Musah, but faces a tough path through a group that includes England and Iran. As the world's biggest sporting event approaches, the countdown has truly begun.
