Uber Eats vs DoorDash vs Grubhub: Which Food Delivery App Actually Saves You Money?

TR
TopRankMay 26, 2026256K views

We ordered the exact same meal from the same restaurant on all three platforms every day for a month. The results were genuinely shocking — one platform was consistently 23% more expensive than the cheapest option.

The Methodology

For 30 consecutive days, we ordered the same meal — a pepperoni pizza and Caesar salad from a local restaurant located exactly 1.2 miles from our office. All orders were placed at the same time (12:15 PM) to control for surge pricing variations.

The Results

After 30 orders across all three platforms (90 total orders):

DoorDash: Average total per order: $28.47. Delivery fee averaged $3.99. Service fee averaged $2.85.

— Advertisement —

Uber Eats: Average total per order: $29.88. Delivery fee averaged $3.49. Service fee averaged $3.62.

Grubhub: Average total per order: $27.31. Delivery fee averaged $2.99 (with Grubhub+). Service fee averaged $2.12.

Subscription Math

If you order food delivery more than twice a month, the subscription memberships are worth it. DoorDash DashPass ($9.99/month) saved an average of $4.50 per order. Uber One ($9.99/month) saved about $3.80. Grubhub+ ($9.99/month) saved $4.20.

The Winner

For our specific use case (same restaurant, same time, daily orders): Grubhub+ was consistently cheapest. But the real finding was the inconsistency — on any given day, any of the three could be cheapest. The platform that saves you money depends on your specific restaurant, location, and order time. Use all three. Compare before you order.
