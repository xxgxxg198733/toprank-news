Man Wins $1 Million Lottery Twice in One Week Using Same Numbers: 'I Can't Believe It'

TR
By TopRank Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

When Jerry Thomas, a 54-year-old retired truck driver from Grand Rapids, Michigan, bought a Daily 4 lottery ticket last Monday, he was hoping for a small win. Instead, he hit the jackpot, winning $500,000. Incredibly, he played the same numbers—4-8-15-23-42—again on Sunday and won a second time, taking home another $500,000. After taxes, he pocketed a cool $1 million. 'I still can't believe it,' Thomas said at a press conference on Wednesday. 'I thought it was a glitch in the app.'

The Lucky Numbers and the Odds

The numbers Thomas used are famous as the 'Hatch' sequence from the TV show 'Lost,' which he says he's been playing for 20 years. The odds of winning the Daily 4 jackpot twice in one week with the same numbers are astronomical—roughly 1 in 10 trillion, according to probability experts at the University of Michigan. The Michigan Lottery confirmed that both drawings were independently verified and legitimate, calling it 'a statistical anomaly of epic proportions.'

What He Plans to Do With the Money

Thomas, who lives modestly with his wife of 30 years, said he plans to pay off his mortgage, buy a new truck, and donate $100,000 to a local children's hospital. He also said he will continue to play the same numbers. 'Why stop now?' he joked. His wife, Linda, said she initially didn't believe him. 'I thought he was pulling my leg. Then he showed me the bank statement.' The couple has hired a financial advisor and a lawyer to manage their sudden wealth.

Expert Advice for Sudden Lottery Winners

Financial experts warn that sudden wealth can be overwhelming, and many lottery winners end up broke within five years. Thomas has taken the right first steps: remaining anonymous, hiring professionals, and not making any major decisions for the first six months. 'The key is to treat it as a long-term asset, not a spending spree,' said financial planner Sarah Johnson. 'If he invests wisely, he could generate $50,000 a year in passive income.'

For now, Thomas is enjoying his 15 minutes of fame. 'I'm just a regular guy who got lucky,' he said. 'Twice.'
