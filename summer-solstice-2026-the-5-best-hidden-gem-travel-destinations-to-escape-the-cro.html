Summer Solstice 2026: The 5 Best Hidden Gem Travel Destinations to Escape the Crowds

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 460,322 views

As the summer solstice approaches on June 21, 2026, millions of travelers will flock to popular hotspots like Santorini, Bali, and the Amalfi Coast. But for those seeking a more authentic and less crowded experience, there are hidden gems waiting to be discovered. We've curated a list of five under-the-radar destinations that offer stunning solstice celebrations, natural beauty, and cultural immersion, all without the throngs of tourists.

1. Orkney Islands, Scotland

Experience the summer solstice at the ancient Neolithic site of the Ring of Brodgar, where the sun aligns perfectly with the standing stones. The Orkney Islands offer dramatic cliffs, puffin colonies, and a rich Viking history. Accommodation is limited, so book early. The islands are accessible by ferry from mainland Scotland.

2. Kotor, Montenegro

Often overshadowed by Dubrovnik, the Bay of Kotor is a breathtaking fjord-like inlet with medieval towns and towering mountains. For the solstice, the local festival 'Kotor Art' features open-air concerts and a bonfire on the waterfront. It's significantly cheaper than Croatia and far less crowded.

3. Salar de Uyuni, Bolivia

While the salt flats are famous for their mirror effect during the rainy season, the dry season (starting June) offers a different spectacle: a vast white landscape under a crystal-clear sky, perfect for stargazing. The solstice coincides with the Aymara New Year, a local indigenous celebration with rituals at sunrise.

4. Alentejo, Portugal

Portugal's Alentejo region, south of Lisbon, is a land of cork forests, vineyards, and white-washed villages. Unlike the Algarve, its beaches are largely empty. The town of Évora, a UNESCO site, hosts the 'Solstice Festival' with folk music and local wine tastings. It's an affordable alternative to the Porto and Lisbon crowds.

5. Gili Meno, Indonesia

While Bali is packed, the Gili Islands offer a quieter escape. Gili Meno, the smallest of the three, has no motorized vehicles and pristine coral reefs. For the solstice, snorkel with sea turtles and enjoy a beachside barbecue under the stars. It's a true paradise for digital detox.
