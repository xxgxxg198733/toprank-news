The 10 Best Shows to Watch This Week (July 20-26, 2026)

TR
By Amanda Brooks · July 21, 2026 · 4 min read

It's Tuesday, and I've spent way too many hours this past week staring at screens so you don't have to. The good news? There's a ton of great stuff to watch right now. The bad news? Finding it among the noise is harder than ever. So I did the work. Here are the 10 best shows to watch this week, ranked by how much they'll actually grab you — not by some algorithm.

1. The Bear: Season 4 (Hulu)

If you're not watching The Bear, what are you even doing? Season 4 premiered last Friday, and it's already the best season yet. The kitchen chaos is dialed up, but what really got me was Richie's arc. This is the season where he finally stops being a caricature and becomes something heartbreakingly real. I won't spoil anything, but there's a scene in episode 3 that made me pause and just sit in silence for a minute. Jeremy Allen White is doing career-best work, and that's saying something.

2. House of the Dragon: Season 3 (HBO)

Look, I know season 2 had its slow moments. But season 3, which kicked off Sunday, is a return to form. The first episode opens with a battle sequence that's up there with anything from the parent show. Rhaenyra's grief is palpable — Emma D'Arcy is terrifying and fragile at the same time. If fantasy isn't your thing, skip to #3. But if you're here for dragons and political scheming, this is appointment viewing.

3. The Sympathizer (HBO)

HBO's adaptation of Viet Thanh Nguyen's novel is still airing new episodes, and it's the most underrated show of the year. Robert Downey Jr. plays multiple roles, and it's weirdly brilliant. The show is a spy thriller, a satire, and a meditation on identity all at once. Episode 5, which aired Monday, has a 20-minute single take that's technically stunning. If you missed the first few episodes, binge them now.

4. Eric (Netflix)

Benedict Cumberbatch stars in this limited series that just dropped on Netflix. He plays a puppeteer in 1980s New York whose son goes missing. It's dark, weird, and surprisingly moving. The puppet subplot — his son's creation, Eric — could have been ridiculous, but Cumberbatch sells it completely. I cried at the end. Don't judge me.

5. Presumed Innocent (Apple TV+)

This legal thriller with Jake Gyllenhaal is the kind of show you can't stop watching. The final episode airs this Wednesday. I've been recommending it to everyone. It's tightly plotted, the performances are excellent (Ruth Negga is phenomenal), and it actually sticks the landing — something most limited series fail to do. If you haven't started, binge it tonight.

6. The Acolyte (Disney+)

Look, the Star Wars fandom is divided on this one. But I think it's genuinely good. The latest episode, which came out Tuesday, expands the mystery of the Sith assassin in a way that finally pays off the slow build. Amandla Stenberg is doing interesting work here, and the fight choreography is the best in live-action Star Wars since the prequels. If the haters are getting you down, ignore them and form your own opinion.

7. Evil: Season 4 (Paramount+)

This show is still on the air and still bonkers in the best way. Season 4 just passed its halfway point, and it's delivering the perfect mix of horror, comedy, and theological debate. The episode this week had a possessed doll, a exorcism via Zoom, and a genuinely touching moment between Kristen and David. This is the most fun show on television right now, and nobody's talking about it.

8. Bridgerton: Season 4 (Netflix)

I know, I know — it's fluffy. But sometimes you need fluff. Season 4, which is currently the #1 show on Netflix, focuses on Benedict's love story. It's charming, visually gorgeous, and exactly what a summer binge should be. Plus, the soundtrack is full of orchestral covers of 2025 pop songs. Yes, Chappell Roan's "Good Luck, Babe" gets the string quartet treatment. Yes, it works.

9. The Boys: Season 5 (Amazon)

The final season of The Boys premiered last month and is almost over — two episodes remain. This week's episode was one of the most brutal, darkly funny hours of television I've seen. Homelander is at his most unhinged, and Antony Starr is committing so hard to the role that I'm genuinely worried about him. If you're watching, you know what I mean. If you're not, start from season 1 — it's worth the investment.

10. Industry: Season 3 (HBO)

This show about London bankers is criminally underrated. Season 3 started last week, and the new episode this Monday was a banger. The show has evolved from "Euphoria in suits" into something genuinely sophisticated about capitalism and ambition. Myha'la Herrold deserves an Emmy. Watch it before everyone else discovers it and you lose the ability to be smug about it.

Honorable mention: Fargo season 6 is also airing on FX, and it's great — but you need to have seen the other seasons. If you have, you already know. If you haven't, start.
