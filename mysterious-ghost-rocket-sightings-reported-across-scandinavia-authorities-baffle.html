Mysterious 'Ghost Rocket' Sightings Reported Across Scandinavia, Authorities Baffled

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 05, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 55,430 views

Residents across Scandinavia reported a strange phenomenon on the night of June 3, 2026: a bright, cigar-shaped object moving at high speed across the sky, leaving a glowing trail. Dubbed 'ghost rocket' by local media, the sightings occurred in Norway, Sweden, and Finland, with hundreds of witnesses describing a silent, slow-moving craft. Military authorities have launched an investigation but have found no debris or explanation.

Details of the Sightings

The first reports came from Tromsø, Norway, at 10:15 PM local time. Witnesses described the object as 'larger than a commercial airliner' with no wings or visible lights. It moved from west to east, taking about 5 minutes to cross the sky. Similar sightings were reported in Stockholm, Sweden, and Oulu, Finland. Some witnesses captured video on their phones, showing a bright, elongated shape with a faint blue glow.

Official Response

The Norwegian Defence Research Establishment has ruled out conventional aircraft, weather balloons, or satellites. 'We have checked radar data and flight logs, and no known object matches the description,' said Major Lars Eriksen. The Swedish Air Force launched a fighter jet to intercept the object, but it disappeared from radar before the jet arrived. No missile launches or space debris re-entries were scheduled.

Theories and Explanations

Speculation ranges from a secret military test to a meteorite or space junk. Astronomer Dr. Sofia Lindgren suggests it could be a rare atmospheric phenomenon called a 'space jellyfish,' caused by a rocket exhaust in sunlight. However, the lack of sound and slow speed are unusual for such events. Conspiracy theories have flooded social media, with some linking it to UFOs. The governments of Norway and Sweden have pledged to release a full report within two weeks.
