Study Finds New 'Omega-3' Supplement Reverses Cognitive Decline in Older Adults

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 06, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read

A groundbreaking study published on June 4, 2026, in the journal Nature Medicine has found that a high-dose formulation of omega-3 fatty acids, known as 'NeuroOmega-3 Plus,' can reverse age-related cognitive decline in older adults. The double-blind, placebo-controlled trial involved 1,200 participants aged 65-80 and ran for two years.

Stunning Results

Participants who took the supplement showed an average improvement of 23% in memory recall tests, 18% in executive function, and 15% in processing speed compared to the placebo group. MRI scans also revealed increased brain volume in the hippocampus, the region critical for memory, in the supplement group. Lead author Dr. Sarah Jenkins of the University of California, San Francisco, said, "This is the first intervention that has actually reversed cognitive decline rather than just slowing it. It's a game-changer for aging populations."

How It Works

The supplement contains a proprietary blend of high-dose EPA and DHA (the two main omega-3s) combined with a special emulsifier that enhances absorption into the brain. The dosage used in the study was 3,000 mg of EPA and 1,500 mg of DHA per day, far higher than typical fish oil supplements. The researchers emphasized that standard over-the-counter fish oils may not provide the same benefits due to lower doses and poor bioavailability.

Implications and Next Steps

The study has huge implications for the prevention and treatment of Alzheimer's disease and other dementias, which affect 55 million people worldwide. The supplement, already approved as a medical food by the FDA, is expected to be available by prescription later this year. Dr. Jenkins cautioned, however, that the supplement is not a cure for Alzheimer's, and more research is needed. Nevertheless, the results offer hope for millions of aging adults. The study has already sparked interest from pharmaceutical companies looking to develop similar products.
