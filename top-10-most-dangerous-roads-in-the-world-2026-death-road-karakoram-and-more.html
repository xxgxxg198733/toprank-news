Top 10 Most Dangerous Roads in the World 2026: Death Road, Karakoram, and More

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 04, 2026 ⏱ 2 min read 95,224 views

If you thought your daily commute was stressful, imagine driving on a road carved into a sheer cliff face without guardrails. Every year, road accidents claim 1.3 million lives globally, and some roads are statistically deadlier than others. Here are the top 10 most dangerous roads in the world for 2026, based on fatality data from the International Road Federation and traveler reports.

1-5: The Deadliest Routes

Number one is Bolivia's Yungas Road, also known as 'Death Road.' It drops 3,600 meters in 64 kilometers, with fog and landslides common. In 2025, 15 cyclist deaths were reported. Second is the Karakoram Highway in Pakistan, which runs through active fault lines and avalanche zones. Third is India's Zoji La Pass, a high-altitude military road with no shoulders. Fourth is the Dalton Highway in Alaska, famous for ice and 24-hour darkness. Fifth is the Stelvio Pass in Italy, with 48 hairpin turns and sheer drops.

6-10: Roads That Demand Respect

Sixth is the Transfăgărășan in Romania, beautiful but prone to rockfalls. Seventh is the North Yungas Road in Peru, another mountain cliff-hugger. Eighth is the Skippers Canyon Road in New Zealand, unpaved and only one car wide. Ninth is the Himalayan Highway in Nepal, where buses sometimes tip over. Tenth is the Guoliang Tunnel in China, hand-carved into a mountain wall. All of these require extreme caution, experienced drivers, and ideally a 4x4 vehicle.

How to Survive a Drive on a Dangerous Road

Safety experts recommend checking weather forecasts, driving during daylight, and never using cruise control on twisty roads. For extreme routes like Death Road, consider hiring a local driver. Also, ensure your vehicle has good brakes, tires, and a first-aid kit. 'Don't be a hero,' warns travel blogger Mark Johnson, who has driven 9 of these 10. 'The views might be worth it, but only if you make it home.' For thrill-seekers, these roads offer adventure; for the unprepared, they offer tragedy.
