I Tried Every Taco at Taco Bell's New Menu and Ranked Them (You'll Hate #1)

TR
By Hannah Powell · July 18, 2026 · 6 min read

I have a complicated relationship with Taco Bell. On one hand, I know it's not 'real' Mexican food (whatever that means). On the other hand, at 2 AM after a few beers, a Crunchwrap Supreme has saved my life more times than I can count. So when I heard that Taco Bell was launching eight new items on July 14, 2026 — their biggest menu refresh in five years — I knew I had to try them all. In one sitting. For science.

The Contenders: What We're Working With

The new menu is called 'Cantina Fresca' — a name that sounds like a resort you can't afford. It includes four new tacos (two hard shell, two soft), two new burritos, a quesadilla variant, and a 'street corn' side that everyone's talking about. I ordered one of everything, plus a Baja Blast for hydration. Total cost: $34.57. Total calories: I'm guessing around 4,000, but I'm too scared to calculate.

I ate everything within 45 minutes, taking notes between bites. I ranked them from worst to best based on taste, texture, value, and how much I'd actually order it again. Let's get into it.

8. The 'Cantina Fresca' Hard Shell Taco — A Disappointment

I'll start with the biggest letdown. This is a standard hard shell taco with a new 'fresca salsa' — basically pico de gallo with some lime. The problem? The salsa makes the shell soggy within about 30 seconds. By the time I got it to my table, the bottom was already falling apart. The flavor was fine — fresh, bright, with a nice kick from jalapeño — but the structural integrity is a disaster. Taco Bell has been making hard shell tacos for 60 years. They should know better.

Also, it's $2.99 for a single taco. That's more than a Doritos Locos Taco, which is objectively better. Skip this one.

7. The 'Fajita Chicken' Soft Taco — Boring but Fine

This is exactly what it sounds like: a flour tortilla with grilled chicken, bell peppers, onions, and a squirt of sour cream. It tastes like something you'd get at a mall food court in 1998. Not bad, not great. The chicken was dry in my order (maybe I got a bad batch?), and the vegetables were limp. For $3.49, I'd rather get two regular soft tacos, which are cheaper and more satisfying.

The 'street corn' side, by the way, is fantastic. It's elote-style corn with cotija cheese, chili powder, and a creamy sauce. I'd eat that by the bucketful. But the fajita taco? Meh.

6. The 'Double Steak' Quesadilla — Overpriced and Overhyped

Taco Bell's quesadillas have always been a guilty pleasure — that weird, creamy jalapeño sauce is addictive. The new version adds double steak (two portions instead of one) and costs $5.99. The steak was actually pretty good — tender, seasoned well, not too chewy. But the quesadilla itself was undercooked. The cheese wasn't fully melted, and the tortilla had pale spots where it didn't hit the griddle. For $6, I expect perfection. This was a C+.

Pro tip: if you want a good quesadilla, order the regular one and add steak. You'll save a dollar and get better results.

5. The 'Chili Verde' Burrito — A Solid Mid-Tier Option

This one surprised me. It's a flour tortilla filled with shredded pork in a green chili sauce, plus rice, beans, and cheese. The pork was tender and had a nice smoky flavor, though the sauce was a little thin — it leaked out of the bottom of the burrito and made a mess. The portion was generous (I'd guess 10 ounces), and at $4.99, it's one of the better values on the new menu.

My complaint: the rice-to-meat ratio was off. Too much rice, not enough pork. I'd eat it again, but I'd ask for extra meat.

4. The 'Cantina Fresca' Soft Taco — A Worthy Upgrade

This is the same concept as the hard shell version, but it works way better because the soft tortilla doesn't get soggy. The fresca salsa is the star here — fresh tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime that cuts through the richness of the seasoned beef. It's $2.79 and actually tastes fresh, which is rare for fast food.

I'd rank this higher if the portion were bigger. It's a small taco — maybe 3 ounces of filling. I ate two and was still hungry. But as a light snack or a side to a burrito, it's great.

3. The 'Al Pastor' Burrito — The Dark Horse

Here's where things get good. This burrito features al pastor-style pork (marinated with pineapple, achiote, and spices) along with grilled pineapple chunks, rice, black beans, and a creamy chipotle sauce. The pork was juicy and had a legit char on the edges — I actually double-checked that I was at Taco Bell. The pineapple added sweetness that balanced the heat from the chipotle.

At $5.49, it's the most expensive burrito on the menu, but it's also the best. The only downside: it's huge (probably 12 ounces) and messy. You'll need a fork for the last few bites. But for a fast food burrito, this is impressive.

2. The 'Crispy Chicken' Taco — A Game Changer

I did not expect to love this. It's a hard shell taco filled with crispy chicken strips (not ground beef), shredded lettuce, pico de gallo, and a lime crema. The chicken was genuinely crispy — not just 'fast food crispy' but actually crunchy, like a good chicken tender. The crema added a tangy creaminess that tied everything together.

The shell held up better than the regular hard shell taco, probably because the chicken is drier than the beef. I ate three of these over the course of the week and they were consistently good. At $3.29 each, they're a great deal.

1. The 'Street Corn' Side — Yes, It's That Good

I know, I know — a side dish topped the list? But I'm serious. Taco Bell's new elote-style street corn is the best thing they've introduced since the Crunchwrap. It's fresh corn (not canned, surprisingly sweet), tossed in a creamy sauce made with mayo, lime, and chili powder, then topped with cotija cheese and a sprinkle of Tajín. It tastes like something you'd get from a street vendor in Mexico City.

The portion is generous — a medium-sized cup for $2.49. I've gone back three times just for this. It's vegetarian, gluten-free (if you care about that), and genuinely delicious. My only complaint: they need to offer a large size. I'd pay $4 for a bigger version.

If you go to Taco Bell for the new menu, skip the hard shell taco and the overpriced quesadilla. Get the crispy chicken taco, the al pastor burrito, and definitely the street corn. Your taste buds will thank you. Your digestive system? That's between you and your bathroom.
