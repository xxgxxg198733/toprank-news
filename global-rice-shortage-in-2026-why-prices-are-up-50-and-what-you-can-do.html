Global Rice Shortage in 2026: Why Prices Are Up 50% and What You Can Do

TR
TopRank Editorial Team June 03, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read 270,076 views

A severe global rice shortage has pushed prices to record highs in 2026, with the cost of the staple grain rising by 50% since January. According to the UN Food and Agriculture Organization, the crisis is driven by extreme weather in major producing countries like India, Thailand, and Vietnam, combined with export bans aimed at protecting domestic supplies.

The Causes

India, the world’s largest rice exporter, imposed a ban on non-basmati rice exports in late 2025 following a devastating monsoon season that ruined crops. Thailand and Vietnam have also reduced exports due to drought conditions exacerbated by El Niño. Global rice production is down 15% this year, the largest decline in decades. “This is a perfect storm,” said agricultural economist Dr. Raj Patel. “Climate change is making these events more frequent and severe.”

Impact on Consumers

The price of a 5-kilogram bag of rice has jumped from $10 to $15 in the US, and from $8 to $12 in Europe. In countries like Nigeria and Indonesia, where rice is a dietary staple, the impact is even more severe. Food banks are struggling to meet demand, and reports of panic buying have emerged in some regions. “We’re seeing families cut back on meals,” said UN aid coordinator Maria Santos.

What You Can Do

Experts recommend reducing food waste and substituting rice with alternatives like quinoa, millet, or couscous. Many supermarkets are limiting rice purchases to prevent hoarding. Governments are also stepping in: the US has released 500,000 tons from strategic reserves, and India may ease its ban later this year. For now, consumers should plan ahead and stay calm. “The panic is making things worse,” said Dr. Patel. “We need collective action, not hoarding.”
